## 4. Market Research (For Business Idea Articles)

### Capital Requirements
**Minimum capital to start:** Rp20-35 million  
**Breakdown:**  
- Inventory (5-10 wedding gowns): Rp10-40 million (Rp2-4M per designer gown)  
- Storage/display setup: Rp2-5 million  
- Marketing/website: Rp3-10 million  
- Legal/business registration: Rp600k-3.8 million  
- Working capital (3 months): Rp5-10 million  
**Total:** Rp20-35 million  

**Where to buy:**  
- Import from China/Taiwan via Alibaba/Shopee wholesale: Rp1.5-2.5M per gown  
- Local suppliers in Jakarta (Tanah Abang, Grand Indonesia Mall): Rp2-4M per gown  
- Designer collaborations: Rent from established bridal shops initially  
- Price comparison: Shopee bulk orders 30-50% cheaper than retail  

### Revenue Potential
**Average selling price:** Rp500k-2M per rental (3-7 days)  
**Cost per unit:** Rp100k-500k (dry cleaning, maintenance)  
**Profit margin:** 60-80% after costs  

**Monthly projection:**  
- Conservative (beginner): Rp5-10 million revenue (10-20 rentals/month)  
- Moderate (6 months in): Rp15-25 million revenue  
- Optimistic (1 year established): Rp30-50 million revenue  

**Time to break-even:** 3-6 months with proper marketing  

### Market Demand
**Search volume:** 10K-50K monthly searches for "sewa gaun pengantin" on Google Indonesia  
**Trend:** Increasing 15-20% YoY due to affordable luxury demand  
**Seasonality:** Peak November-March (wedding season), 60% of annual revenue; off-season April-October  
**Competition level:** Medium-high in urban areas, low in smaller cities  
**Target customers:** Millennial brides (25-35), working mothers, budget-conscious couples  

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
**Requirement:** Nomor Induk Berusaha (NIB)  
**Regulation:** UU Cipta Kerja 2024, KBLI 82302 (Special Event Organizer)  
**Process:** Register via OSS online system, provide business details  
**Cost:** Free  
**Timeline:** 1-3 days  
**Authority:** Kemeninvest  
**Link:** oss.go.id  

**Requirement:** NPWP Perusahaan  
**Regulation:** UU Pajak Penghasilan  
**Process:** Online registration via djp.go.id  
**Cost:** Free  
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks  
**Authority:** Direktorat Jenderal Pajak  
**Link:** pajak.go.id  

**Requirement:** Business Entity (CV/PT)  
**Regulation:** UU Perseroan Terbatas  
**Process:** Notary establishment, register with AHU  
**Cost:** Rp1-5 million  
**Timeline:** 1-2 months  
**Authority:** Kemenkumham  
**Link:** ahu.go.id  

**Requirement:** Business License for Rental Services  
**Regulation:** Local government regulations  
**Process:** Apply through local Disperindag  
**Cost:** Rp500k-1M  
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks  
**Authority:** Local government  
**Link:** Local Disperindag website  

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies

**Case Study: Yunbridal (Jakarta)**  
**Background:** Started 2019 by Yunica Chandra, focuses on local designer gowns  
**Starting point:** Small boutique with 5-10 gowns, targeting millennial brides  
**Result:** Now serves customers across Java, revenue Rp50-100M/month, expanded to 50+ gown collection  
**Timeline:** 4 years to established brand  
**Key learning:** Local production beats imports for quality and margins; social media marketing crucial for reach

**Case Study: Davina Gallery (Padangsidimpuan)**  
**Background:** Wedding gown rental service since 2019  
**Starting point:** Home-based with basic collection  
**Result:** 475+ 5-star reviews, expanded to out-of-town delivery, integrated sharia-compliant services  
**Timeline:** 5 years growth  
**Key learning:** Digital marketing and partnerships with MUA/photographers boost customer loyalty  
**Source:** AICIEF proceeding paper  

**Case Study: Golden Gallery (Medan)**  
**Background:** MSME bridal rental service  
**Starting point:** Limited collection amid competition  
**Result:** Eligible for Islamic financing (murabahah scheme), expanded inventory, improved competitiveness  
**Timeline:** Ongoing expansion  
**Key learning:** Islamic financing enables sustainable growth without high interest debt

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
**Myth:** Wedding gown rental business requires millions in designer inventory upfront  
**Reality:** Start with 5-8 affordable gowns (Rp10-20M total) and expand gradually  
**Why it matters:** Many women delay starting due to perceived high barriers  
**Source:** Startup cost analysis from multiple Indonesian businesses  

**Myth:** Competition is too high in urban areas like Jakarta  
**Reality:** Low barriers in smaller cities, online marketplaces create nationwide opportunities  
**Why it matters:** Opens doors for entrepreneurs outside major cities  
**Source:** Rentech Digital report showing 1,148+ services nationwide  

**Myth:** Business only profitable during wedding season  
**Reality:** Year-round demand from pre-wedding photoshoots, parties, and corporate events  
**Why it matters:** Ensures stable income throughout year  
**Source:** Bridestory survey showing diverse bridal service usage
