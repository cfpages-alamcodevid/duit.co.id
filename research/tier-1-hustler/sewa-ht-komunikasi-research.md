## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
Requirement: NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha)
KBLI: 77309 (Rental Equipment).
Regulation: SDPPI (Ditjen Sumber Daya dan Perangkat Pos dan Informatika).
Critical Note: DONT use RAPI (142MHz) or ORARI (144MHz) for business.
Solution for beginners: Use PMR409 frequencies (409MHz) which are "License-Free" for low power (500mW) devices.

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: "Pake HT apa aja boleh, frekuensi kan udara gratis."
Reality: Airwaves are state-owned. Balmon (Balai Monitor) frequently raids events to check for illegal frequency use.
Myth: "Jangkauan HT bisa berkilo-kilo meter."
Reality: For 500k HTs, realistic range in a city is only 500m - 1km. Managing expectations is key.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Product Selection
- Start with 10 units. It's the standard for a small EO team.
- Choose WLN for budget or Icom V80 for durability.
Step 2: Legal Check
- Ensure devices are "Postel Certified" (Official stickers).
- Learn which frequencies are "safe" (PMR409).
Step 3: Marketing
- Register on Google Maps.
- Create a price list for 1-day, 3-day, and 1-week rentals.
Step 4: Operations
- Always provide a full charge.
- Include a "How-to-use" cheat sheet.
Step 5: Maintenance
- Wipe earsets with alcohol after every use (Hygiene).
- Deep discharge/charge batteries once a month.
