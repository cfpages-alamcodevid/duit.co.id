### Quality Control Checklist

```
[ ] Spelling check — especially Indonesian slang, English loanwords ("konten", "platform", "engagement")
[ ] Timing sync — subtitle appears when spoken, disappears before next speech
[ ] Reading speed — max 2 lines, 20 characters per line for Indo, 35 for English; 1 second per line minimum
[ ] Line breaks — split at natural pause points, not in middle of phrase
[ ] Font consistency — same font, size, color throughout video (unless stylized)
[ ] No overlapping — subtitles should not cover faces, logos, or key visuals
[ ] Character names — [SPEAKER] format for interviews/podcasts, used only when speaker changes
[ ] Sound effects — [MUSIC], [APPLAUSE], [LAUGHTER] in brackets for accessibility
[ ] Language hybrid detection — Indonesian-English code-switching ("I was like, gitu loh") handled correctly
[ ] Punctuation consistency — period at end of subtitle, comma for natural pauses
[ ] Profanity — censor if client requests, note in SRT/transcript original
```

### Indonesian-Language Subtitle Best Practices

```
1. Ejaan: Gunakan PUEBI (Pedoman Umum Ejaan Bahasa Indonesia)
   - "di" vs "di-" (prefix): "di rumah" vs "dimakan"
   - Wrong: "di buat" → Correct: "dibuat"
   - Kata serapan: "aktif" not "aktip", "teknis" not "teknik"

2. Code-switching: Indonesian mixed with English is extremely common
   - Caption as spoken, don't "clean up" to pure Indonesian
   - "Gue like, bener-bener relate sama video ini" → keep as spoken
   - Exception: if client requests formal Indonesian version

3. Regional slang: Jaksel accent, Eastern dialect, etc.
   - "gue" → natural for casual content, "saya" for formal/professional
   - "banget" → standard, "bgt" → only if spoken that way
   - "anjir", "anjay", "wkwk" → caption EXACTLY as spoken unless client requests sanitized

4. Numbers and dates:
   - "Rp 50.000" (with period as thousand separator, not comma)
   - Dates: "12 Mei 2026" (not May 12, 2026)
   - Time: "15.30 WIB" (24-hour format with WIB/WITA/WIT)

5. Reading speed for Indonesian:
   - Indonesian is more syllabic than English → needs slightly more time per character
   - Max 20 characters per line for comfortable reading
   - Min duration: 1 second for short line, 3-4 seconds for full 2-line caption

6. Foreign terms:
   - English terms commonly used: caption as-is ("engagement", "algorithm", "deadline")
   - Explain in parentheses if obscure term: "doxxing (penyebaran data pribadi)"
```

## 12. Common Myths & Misconceptions

```
Myth: "Subtitle cuma tempel teks doang, siapa aja bisa."
Reality: Subtitle short-form video butuh timing presisi, pemahaman pacing konten, dan gaya caption yang engaging. Animated caption like "Alex Hormozi style" butuh skill editing.
Why it matters: Harga jual jadi rendah karena dianggap "pekerjaan enteng". Positioning sebagai "animated caption specialist" bisa 3x lipat harga.
Source: Forum Kaskus + Fiverr listing analysis

Myth: "AI auto-subtitle udah sempurna, nggak perlu manual."
Reality: AI auto-subtitle akurasi 85-90% untuk Bahasa Indonesia. Masih ada error: code-switching (Indo-Inggris), nama orang, istilah teknis, slang daerah, timing kadang meleset.
Why it matters: Human QC adalah value proposition utama. "AI-generated, human-perfected" adalah positioning yang kuat.
Source: Testing CapCut auto-subtitle + Whisper on Indonesian content

Myth: "Creator Indonesia nggak mau bayar untuk subtitle."
Reality: Banyak creator MAU bayar. Yang bikin mereka ragu adalah kualitas. Tawarkan 1 Reels gratis sebagai demo untuk menunjukkan value. Begitu lihat perbedaan engagement, mereka mau bayar.
Why it matters: Barrier bukan budget, tapi trust. Strategi "free first video" works.
Source: Testimoni case studies + forum creator groups

Myth: "Harus bisa Bahasa Inggris fasih untuk subtitle."
Reality: Untuk caption-only Bahasa Indonesia, cukup kemampuan Bahasa Indonesia standar. Bilingual memang butuh Inggris, tapi SMK Multimedia biasanya dapat basic English dari sekolah. Bisa improve sambil jalan.
Why it matters: Jangan biarkan "saya nggak jago Inggris" jadi alasan untuk tidak mulai. Mulai dengan caption Indonesia dulu, upgrade ke bilingual setelah percaya diri.
Source: Analisis kompetensi lulusan SMK Multimedia

Myth: "Pesaing banyak dari India/Pakistan di Fiverr yang charge $5."
Reality: Creator LOKAL lebih pilih subtitle Indonesia yang paham slang, budaya, dan konteks lokal. Harga $5 Fiverr biasanya untuk English-only, kualitas seadanya, dan tidak paham konteks Indonesia.
Why it matters: Posisikan diri sebagai "Indonesian subtitle specialist", bukan "cheap subtitler". Harga lokal Rp 30-100k/video sangat kompetitif untuk kualitas yang tepat.
Source: Fiverr gig analysis for Indonesian-language subtitles
```
