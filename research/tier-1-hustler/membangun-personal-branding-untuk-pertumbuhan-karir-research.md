### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
For personal branding (online presence):
- Data privacy compliance under UU PDP (Personal Data Protection)
- Business registration if offering paid services (PT Perorangan)
- Tax obligations for income from coaching/courses

```
Requirement: Business registration for paid services
Regulation: UU No. 11 Tahun 2020 tentang Cipta Kerja (PT Perorangan)
Process: Register online via AHU
Cost: Rp 50,000-100,000
Timeline: 1-3 days
Authority: Ministry of Law and Human Rights
Link: https://ahu.go.id

Requirement: Tax reporting for freelance income
Regulation: UU No. 36 Tahun 2008 tentang Pajak Penghasilan
Process: Annual SPT reporting
Cost: Free (online)
Timeline: March 31 annually
Authority: DJP (Direktorat Jenderal Pajak)
Link: https://djponline.pajak.go.id
```

### 9. Action Steps Outline
```
Step 1: Define your personal brand (niche & value proposition)
- Details: Identify expertise, target audience, unique selling points
- Example: "Content creator specializing in career tips for fresh graduates"
- Warning: Don't try to be everything to everyone

Step 2: Optimize online presence (LinkedIn, Instagram, personal website)
- Details: Professional headshot, compelling bio, portfolio showcase
- Example: LinkedIn headline with keywords employers search
- Warning: Keep content consistent across platforms

Step 3: Create valuable content consistently
- Details: Weekly posts, articles, videos about your expertise
- Example: "5 tips for job interview success" thread
- Warning: Quality over quantity, engage with audience

Step 4: Network strategically
- Details: Connect with industry leaders, join communities
- Example: Attend virtual networking events, comment on expert posts
- Warning: Build genuine relationships, not just collect connections

Step 5: Track progress and adjust
- Details: Monitor engagement metrics, job offers, networking results
- Example: Monthly review of profile views and connections
- Warning: Be patient, results take time
```
