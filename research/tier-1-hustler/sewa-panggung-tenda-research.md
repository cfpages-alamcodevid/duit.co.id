## 4. Market Research (For Business Idea Articles)

**Capital Requirements:**
Minimum capital to start: Rp 20.000.000 (Micro) - Rp 150.000.000 (Small-Medium)
Breakdown (Micro):
- 2 Units Standard Tent (4x6m): Rp 8.000.000 (Welded locally).
- 50 Plastic Chairs (Lion Star/Napolly): Rp 5.000.000.
- Standard Cloth Decoration (Plafon): Rp 2.000.000.
- Small Stage (Panggung Triplek): Rp 3.000.000.
- Small Storage/Transport: Rp 2.000.000.
Total: ~Rp 20 Million

Breakdown (Small-Medium):
- 5 Units Decor Tents: Rp 50.000.000.
- 200 Futura Chairs + Covers: Rp 40.000.000.
- Professional Stage (Rigging): Rp 25.000.000.
- Used Pick-up Truck: Rp 50.000.000.
Total: ~Rp 165 Million

Where to buy:
- Local welding workshops (Tukang Las) for tent frames.
- Furniture wholesalers for chairs.
- Tanah Abang or local textile markets for decorative fabrics.

**Revenue Potential:**
Average selling price:
- Standard Tent: Rp 500.000 - Rp 1.000.000 / day
- Decor Tent: Rp 2.000.000 - Rp 5.000.000 / day
- Package (Tent + Stage + 100 Chairs): Rp 5.000.000 - Rp 15.000.000
Cost per unit (Labor to install/remove + Laundry for cloths): 30% of revenue.
Profit margin: 70%

Monthly projection:
- Conservative (2 home events): Rp 10.000.000 revenue -> Rp 7.000.000 profit.
- Moderate (4 events + daily rentals): Rp 25.000.000 revenue -> Rp 17.500.000 profit.
- Optimistic (Vendor for WO/EO): Rp 50.000.000+.

Time to break-even: 10 - 18 months.

**Market Demand:**
Search volume: High during wedding months (Bulan Haji, Bulan Syawal).
Trend: Increasing demand for "Tenda Transparan" and "Rustic" themes.
Seasonality: Low during Bulan Ramadan and rainy season (outdoor events drop).
Competition level: High (local), must compete on service and cloth cleanliness.
Target customers: Neighborhood families, local SMEs (Bazar), Religious groups.

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
Requirement: NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha)
KBLI: 77309 (Aktivitas Penyewaan dan Sewa Guna Usaha Tanpa Hak Opsi Mesin, Peralatan dan Barang Nyata Lainnya YTDL)
Permit: Usually just local neighborhood (RT/RW) notification for installations blocking roads.

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
Case Study: "Tenda Berkah" (Yogyakarta) started with 1 tent for neighborhood use. They reinvested all profits into buying 20 more chairs each month. After 3 years, they own a warehouse and a truck.
Key learning: Reinvestment is crucial. Don't spend the profits; buy more assets until you reach critical mass.

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
Persona: "Indra, 30 tahun, punya lahan kosong di belakang rumah."
Scenario: Indra builds 2 tent frames (8jt) and buys 50 chairs (5jt). He partners with a local cook (Katering) who recommends him to every client.
Result: He gets booked for 3 pengajian/month and 1 small wedding. Profit 8jt/month. ROI in 2 months!

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: "Bisnis musiman, kalau nggak ada kawinan sepi."
Reality: Events like duka (funeral), pengajian, and community meetings happen year-round. Tents are always needed.
Myth: "Harus punya truk sendiri."
Reality: Many beginners rent a pick-up per trip (Rp 150k-300k) until they can afford their own.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Inventory Planning
- Start with 100 chairs and 2 tents. This is the minimum "home event" package.
Step 2: Sourcing
- Find a local welder to make sturdy frames.
- Buy high-quality fabrics that don't fade easily.
Step 3: Marketing
- Banner in front of the house/warehouse.
- WhatsApp groups for local RT/RW.
Step 4: Operations
- Hire 2-3 part-time "kuli angkut/pasang" paid per job.
Step 5: Quality Control
- Wash fabrics after every use.
- Inspect tent frames for rust.
