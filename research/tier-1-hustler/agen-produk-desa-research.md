### 4. Market Research (For Business Idea Articles)

Capital Requirements:
Minimum capital to start: Rp 2-3 juta (realistic for beginners)
Breakdown:
- Initial inventory/samples: Rp 1-1.5 juta (small batches from suppliers like Tani Mart or local cooperatives)
- Marketing budget: Rp 500-1 juta (social media ads, packaging)
- Basic tools (phone, transport): Rp 300-500 ribu
- Operating buffer: Rp 500 ribu (first month contingencies)
Total: Rp 3-5 juta for scalable start

Where to buy:
- Honey: Local cooperatives like Madu Muria (Kudus), or suppliers via Shopee/Tokopedia
- Coffee: Specialty beans from Toraja or Jawa Barat suppliers, e.g., Farid Journey Coffee
- Spices: Jahe, temulawak, kayu manis from markets in Solo or traditional suppliers

Revenue Potential:
Average selling price: Honey Rp250,000/kg, Coffee Rp200,000/kg, Spices Rp100,000/kg (premium markup)
Cost per unit: 50-70% of selling price (depending on supplier margins)
Profit margin: 30-50% per transaction

Monthly projection:
- Conservative: Rp 1-2 juta (10-20 sales/month, focus local)
- Moderate: Rp 3-5 juta (online expansion, 20-30 sales)
- Optimistic: Rp 7-10 juta (established network, bulk orders)

Time to break-even: 1-3 months with consistent sales and low overhead.

Market Demand:
Search volume: High for "madu premium indonesia", "kopi specialty", "rempah tradisional" (10k-50k monthly searches)
Trend: Increasing with health trends, organic demand up 20-30% 2025-2026
Seasonality: Peak during holidays (Ramadan, Lebaran) for spices, year-round for coffee/honey
Competition level: Medium in urban areas, low in connecting rural-urban
Target customers: Health-conscious millennials, urban families seeking authentic products

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
Requirement: BPOM certification for food products like honey, coffee blends, spice mixes
Regulation: Peraturan BPOM No. 31/2018 tentang Label Pangan Olahan
Process: Register products, obtain certificates; for agents, ensure suppliers have certification
Cost: Rp 500k-2M for certification (depending on scale)
Timeline: 1-3 months
Authority: Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan (BPOM)
Link: https://www.pom.go.id/

Requirement: Halal certification
Regulation: UU No. 33/2014 tentang Jaminan Produk Halal
Process: Apply through LPPOM MUI, audit required
Cost: Rp 1-5M
Timeline: 2-4 months
Authority: Majelis Ulama Indonesia (MUI)
Link: https://www.halalmui.org/

Requirement: Business license (NIB) for trading
Regulation: UU No. 11/2020 tentang Cipta Kerja
Process: Online registration via OSS
Cost: Free to Rp 50k
Timeline: 1-2 days
Authority: Kementerian Investasi/BKPM
Link: https://oss.go.id/

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Produk desa selalu murah dan berkualitas rendah
Reality: Premium rural products like forest honey and specialty coffee command high prices (Rp150k-300k/kg) due to authenticity and health benefits.
Why it matters: Prevents underpricing, allows proper margins for agents.
Source: Price data from KampusKopi.com and BigGo

Myth: Sulit mendapat supplier di desa
Reality: Cooperatives like Tani Mart and local networks provide reliable sources with halal/BPOM certified products.
Why it matters: Reduces barriers for beginners.
Source: Cooperative profiles

Myth: Bisnis agen butuh modal besar
Reality: Start with Rp2-3M, focus on dropshipping-like model from rural suppliers.
Why it matters: Encourages low-risk entry for hustlers.
Source: Market research projections
