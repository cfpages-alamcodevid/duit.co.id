## 4. Market Research (Business Idea)

### Capital Requirements
**Minimum capital to start:** Rp25-50 million  
**Breakdown:**  
- PS4/PS5 consoles (2-4 units): Rp20-40 million  
- TV/monitor equipment: Rp4-8 million  
- Controllers and accessories: Rp1-2 million  
- Transportation/delivery setup: Rp2-4 million  
- Marketing and permits: Rp1-2 million  
**Total:** Rp28-56 million  

**Where to buy equipment:**  
- Shopee/Tokopedia for new/used consoles  
- Local electronics markets for TVs  
- Official Sony dealers for controllers  
- Grab/Bike messengers for initial delivery  

**Revenue Potential:**  
**Average selling price:** Rp15,000-25,000 per rental hour  
**Cost per rental:** Rp3,000-5,000 (electricity, maintenance)  
**Profit margin:** 70-80%  
**Monthly projection:**  
- Conservative: Rp3-5 million (20 rentals/week)  
- Moderate: Rp6-8 million (40 rentals/week)  
- Optimistic: Rp10-15 million (60+ rentals/week)  
**Time to break-even:** 3-6 months  
**Market Demand:**  
**Search volume:** High interest in gaming rentals among urban youth  
**Trend:** Increasing due to limited personal console ownership  
**Seasonality:** Peak during weekends, holidays, exam breaks  
**Competition level:** Medium in cities, low in residential areas  
**Target customers:** Students, young workers, families seeking affordable entertainment  

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
**Requirement:** Business Identification Number (NIB)  
**Regulation:** Government Regulation No. 5/2021 on Business Licensing  
**Process:** Register through Online Single Submission (OSS) system  
**Cost:** Free (administrative fee only)  
**Timeline:** 1-3 days approval  
**Authority:** Ministry of Investment/BKPM  
**Link:** https://oss.go.id/  

**Requirement:** KBLI Code 77299  
**Regulation:** Central Statistics Agency classification  
**Process:** Include in NIB application  
**Cost:** No additional cost  
**Timeline:** Part of NIB process  
**Authority:** BPS (Statistics Indonesia)  
**Link:** https://www.bps.go.id/  

**Requirement:** Annual business reporting  
**Regulation:** PP 5/2021  
**Process:** Submit activity report to government by January 15  
**Cost:** Free  
**Timeline:** Annual  
**Authority:** Local Investment Office  
**Link:** https://oss.go.id/

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

**Myth:** Gaming console rentals are dying due to mobile gaming  
**Reality:** Console rentals complement mobile gaming, offering premium offline multiplayer experiences  
**Why it matters:** Youth still seek social gaming experiences unavailable on mobile  
**Source:** Multiple 2025-2026 business reports showing sustained demand  

**Myth:** Only kids and teenagers rent gaming consoles  
**Reality:** Young adults and families also rent for special occasions and limited budgets  
**Why it matters:** Expands target market beyond just students  
**Source:** Arafah Rianti case study showing multi-generational appeal  

**Myth:** Rental businesses require massive capital investment  
**Reality:** Can start with 2-4 units for Rp25-50 million, scalable growth model  
**Why it matters:** Makes business accessible to youth with limited savings  
**Source:** Various startup guides showing tiered investment options  

**Myth:** Competition is too fierce in major cities  
**Reality:** Home delivery model creates niche market even in competitive areas  
**Why it matters:** Allows youth to compete through service differentiation  
**Source:** Successful urban delivery businesses like Tambak7  

## 9. Action Steps Outline

**Step 1: Research & Planning (1-2 weeks)**  
- Analyze local youth gaming demand and competition  
- Calculate realistic startup budget (Rp25-50 million)  
- Identify target delivery areas (student housing, residential complexes)  
- Create basic business plan with revenue projections  
- Details needed: Market research data, location analysis, pricing strategy  
- Example: Survey 50 potential customers about gaming preferences  
- Warning: Don't underestimate transportation costs in delivery model  

**Step 2: Legal & Administrative Setup (1 week)**  
- Register business through OSS for NIB  
- Obtain KBLI 77299 classification  
- Set up business entity (individual or CV)  
- Prepare necessary permits and insurance  
- Details needed: ID documents, location address, business description  
- Example: Use online OSS portal for quick registration  
- Warning: Ensure all documents are complete to avoid delays  

**Step 3: Equipment Acquisition (1-2 weeks)**  
- Purchase 2-4 PS4/PS5 units with controllers  
- Buy compatible TVs/monitors (32-43 inch)  
- Source original games and accessories  
- Set up delivery logistics (vehicle, packaging)  
- Details needed: Equipment specifications, supplier comparisons  
- Example: Test all equipment before launch  
- Warning: Buy from reputable sellers to avoid counterfeit products  

**Step 4: Operations Setup (1 week)**  
- Configure pricing structure (hourly/daily rates)  
- Develop delivery and pickup procedures  
- Create maintenance schedule for equipment  
- Set up customer service protocols  
- Details needed: Standard operating procedures, safety guidelines  
- Example: Practice delivery routes before launch  
- Warning: Have backup equipment for technical issues  

**Step 5: Marketing & Launch (Ongoing)**  
- Build social media presence targeting youth  
- Partner with local student communities  
- Offer promotional discounts for first customers  
- Collect customer feedback for improvements  
- Details needed: Marketing budget, content calendar, customer database  
- Example: Create Instagram/TikTok content showing delivery service  
- Warning: Start small to manage initial demand
