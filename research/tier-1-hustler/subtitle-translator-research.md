### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: "You need expensive software to start subtitle translation"
Reality: Free tools like Aegisub and Subtitle Edit are professional-grade and used by industry
Why it matters: Removes financial barrier for beginners
Source: Freelance translator forums on Reddit and Facebook groups

Myth: "Subtitle translation is just direct word-for-word translation"
Reality: Requires understanding context, timing, cultural adaptation, and subtitle formatting rules
Why it matters: Quality work demands more than basic translation skills
Source: Professional translation guidelines from Netflix and YouTube

Myth: "Only native speakers can do good subtitle translation"
Reality: Bilingual speakers with good language skills and cultural knowledge can excel with practice
Why it matters: Opens opportunity for Indonesian freelancers to serve global markets
Source: Case studies from successful non-native translators

### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Master subtitle editing software
- Details: Learn Aegisub, Subtitle Edit, or Veed.io basics
- Example: Practice with TED Talk videos
- Warning: Don't start with paid clients until confident

Step 2: Build language proficiency portfolio
- Details: Create samples in target language pairs
- Example: Translate 3 different video types (educational, entertainment, documentary)
- Warning: Get feedback from native speakers before offering services

Step 3: Set up freelance profiles
- Details: Create accounts on Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer.com
- Example: Write compelling profile with samples
- Warning: Start with low rates to build reviews

Step 4: Price your services competitively
- Details: Research market rates, calculate based on time and complexity
- Example: Rp 10,000-20,000 per minute depending on language pair
- Warning: Factor in revision time and client communication

Step 5: Market to target clients
- Details: Reach out to local YouTubers, production houses via social media
- Example: Offer free sample translations to build portfolio
- Warning: Focus on quality over quantity of pitches
