## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
Case Study: "Basecamp Outdoor" (Semarang) started with 2 personal tents. They focused on "Fragrant Sleeping Bags" as their USP. While competitors had smelly bags, they used specialized laundry. Within 2 years, they were the biggest in the city.
Key learning: Hygiene is the best marketing. People will pay 5k more for a tent that doesn't smell like sweat.

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: "Barang outdoor cepet rusak kalau disewain."
Reality: If you use the right cleaning methods (don't machine wash tents!) and check for damage upon return, gear can last years.
Myth: "Harus merk mahal (Eiger) baru laku."
Reality: Many hikers prefer "Function over Brand". Cheaper durable brands like Great Outdoor or Consina have better ROI.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Inventory Selection
- Start with Tents, Matras, and SB (The Big Three).
- Add Carriers and Shoes later as they are harder to fit.
Step 2: Pricing Strategy
- Use "24-Hour" system.
- Offer "Package Deals" (Tenda + Kompor + Matras) for better value.
Step 3: Marketing
- Google Maps is mandatory.
- Join local hiking Facebook groups.
Step 4: Operational SOP
- Check-out: Show customer that the tent is complete.
- Check-in: Inspect for rips or missing stakes.
- Maintenance: Dry gear completely before storing to prevent mold.
Step 5: Value-Added Services
- Sell gas canisters, raincoats, and instant noodles.
