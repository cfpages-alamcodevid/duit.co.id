### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

Requirement: Pitch deck content must comply with OJK regulations for startup fundraising
Regulation: UU No. 8 Tahun 1995 tentang Pasar Modal and POJK No. 14/POJK.04/2020
Process: Register as equity crowdfunding participant if seeking public investment
Cost: Rp 0 for basic compliance, Rp 1-2 million for legal consultation
Timeline: 1-2 weeks for compliance check
Authority: OJK Indonesia
Link: https://ojk.go.id/

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

Myth: Pitch decks need to be 20+ slides to be comprehensive
Reality: Best pitches are 10-12 slides focused on key metrics
Why it matters: Investors are busy, long decks get skimmed
Source: Sequoia Capital research adapted for Indonesia context

Myth: Early startup need perfect financial projections
Reality: Show assumptions behind numbers, 3 scenarios (worst/base/best)
Why it matters: Invests trust when plans change
Source: YC startups that succeeded in Indonesia

### 9. Action Steps Outline

Step 1: Research your market thoroughly
- Details needed: Identify TAM/SAM/SOM, competitor analysis, customer segments
- Example: Use Google Analytics and social listening for local market
- Warning: Don't underestimate local competitors

Step 2: Build financial projections
- Details needed: Revenue model, cost structure, break-even timeline
- Example: Use spreadsheet with 3-year projection
- Warning: Use conservative assumptions

Step 3: Create the pitch deck
- Details needed: 10-12 slides with hook, problem, solution, team, ask
- Example: Use Canva templates with local data
- Warning: Less is more, avoid jargon

Step 4: Practice and get feedback
- Details needed: Present to mentors, record yourself
- Example: Use OuiPitch or similar tools
- Warning: Don't memorize, be conversational

Step 5: Schedule meetings with investors
- Details needed: Warm introductions, follow-up etiquette
- Example: Use AngelList or LinkedIn
- Warning: Don't send deck alone, need context meeting
