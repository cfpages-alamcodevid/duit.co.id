## 4. Market Research

### The "Legacy" Landscape in Indonesia

**Key Market Observations:**

1. **Aging Founder Generation:** Many of Indonesia's largest businesses were founded in the 1970s-1990s. Founders are now in their 60s-90s. The succession wave is happening NOW.

2. **"Orang Tua Tidak Boleh Mati" Mentality:** Deep cultural taboo around discussing death and succession. Many Indonesian business owners avoid the topic entirely. This is the single biggest barrier to succession planning.

3. **Hukum Waris Complexity:**
   - Indonesia has multiple inheritance law systems:
     - **Hukum Waris Perdata (BW/KUHPerdata)** — for non-Muslims
     - **Hukum Waris Islam (Kompilasi Hukum Islam)** — for Muslims
     - **Hukum Waris Adat** — varies by region
   - This complexity makes intestate (without will) succession extremely messy for business owners

4. **Low Will/Testament Penetration:** Very few Indonesian business owners have a formal will (surat wasiat) or trust structure. Most rely on informal "family agreements" that often break down.

5. **The PT/Corporate Structure Gap:** Many SMEs are structured as PT but without clear shareholder agreements, buy-sell provisions, or succession clauses in the Articles of Association (Anggaran Dasar).

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### Testament / Will (Surat Wasiat)

```
Requirement: Formal last will and testament
Regulation: 
- KUHPerdata (BW) Pasal 874-1022 for non-Muslims
- Kompilasi Hukum Islam (KHI) for Muslims
- Notaris required for formal will
Process: Drafted by notaris, registered in central will registry
Cost: Rp 2-10 juta depending on complexity
Timeline: 3-7 working days for drafting
Authority: Kantor Notaris
Notes: Indonesia recognizes two types of wasiat — wasiat umum (general will) and wasiat khusus (specific bequest). For Muslims, the will is limited to max 1/3 of estate unless heirs consent.
```

### Company Succession / Shareholder Agreement

```
Requirement: Succession clause in PT's Articles of Association (Akta Pendirian/Anggaran Dasar) + Shareholder Agreement
Regulation: UU No. 40 Tahun 2007 tentang Perseroan Terbatas
Process: 
1. Review current Anggaran Dasar for succession gap
2. Draft shareholder agreement with buy-sell provisions
3. Include cross-purchase or redemption clauses
4. Notarial deed required for AD changes
Cost: Rp 5-25 juta (lawyer + notary)
Timeline: 2-4 weeks
Authority: Notaris + Kemenkumham (for AD changes)
```

### Hibah (Gift During Lifetime)

```
Requirement: Hibah deed (Akta Hibah)
Regulation: KUHPerdata Pasal 1666-1693; KHI for Muslims
Process: Notarial deed signed by giver and receiver
Cost: Rp 1-5 juta (notary fee)
Tax: Subject to PPh Final (if business assets transferred)
Notes: Often used as succession planning tool — transferring assets gradually during lifetime. However, has limitations: legitimate portie (legitieme portie) rules protect forced heirs.
```

### Trust / Perwalian

```
Requirement: Trust structure (limited in Indonesia's civil law system)
Regulation: Not fully recognized like common law trusts. Alternatives: foundations (yayasan), nominee arrangements, or offshore trusts for high-net-worth
Process: Complex structuring usually involving Singapore or offshore trusts
Cost: US$5,000-50,000+ depending on complexity
Timeline: 4-12 weeks
Authority: International trust companies + Indonesian legal counsel
Notes: For most SME owners, the simpler approach is a combination of will + shareholder agreement + insurance. Pure trusts are more for high-net-worth (US$5M+).
```

### Tax Implications

```
Requirement: Tax planning for asset transfer
Regulation: UU PPh, UU PPN, UU Bea Perolehan Hak atas Tanah dan Bangunan (BPHTB)
Key Points:
1. Indonesia does NOT have inheritance tax (pajak warisan) as of 2026
2. BUT: transfer of assets triggers PPh Final on certain asset types
3. Land and building transfers trigger BPHTB (~5% of NJKP value)
4. Shares transfer via inheritance is subject to PPh Final (0.1% of value)
5. Business assets transferred inter vivos (during lifetime) may be subject to PPh
6. Best practice: structure via PT or holding company to minimize tax impact
```

## 7. Practical Frameworks

### Framework 1: The 5-Step Succession Roadmap for SME Owners

**Step 1: Start the Conversation (6-12 months before action)**
- Break the "orang tua tidak boleh mati" taboo
- Hold a family meeting with spouse and adult children
- Discuss: who wants the business? Who has capability?
- Writer note: This is often the HARDEST step. Frame it as "stewardship, not death planning."

**Step 2: Document Everything (3-6 months)**
- List all business assets (PT shares, equipment, IP, contracts, goodwill)
- List personal assets (property, investments, vehicles)
- List debts and liabilities
- Create a business valuation baseline
- Writer note: Most SME owners cannot name their asset value within 30%.

**Step 3: Choose Your Succession Model (2-4 months decision)**
- **Option A: Family succession** — hand over to children/relatives
  - Requires: training period (3-5 years), clear role definition
- **Option B: Professional management** — keep ownership in family, hire CEO
  - Requires: strong board, clear KPIs, separation of ownership & management
- **Option C: Sale/Merger** — sell to partner, competitor, or strategic buyer
  - Requires: business ready for due diligence, clean financials
- **Option D: Gradual transfer via hibah** — transfer assets slowly over time
  - Writer note: Most SMEs don't realize they have options. Many default to Option A without preparation.

**Step 4: Legal Implementation (1-3 months)**
- Draft or update will (surat wasiat) with notaris
- Update PT's Anggaran Dasar with succession clauses
- Draft shareholder agreement with buy-sell provisions
- Consider life insurance for liquidity (to pay inheritance tax/buy out siblings)
- Writer note: Estimate total legal cost Rp 10-30 juta for SME — affordable relative to the risk.

**Step 5: Communication & Handover (6-12 months transition)**
- Communicate succession plan to employees
- Mentor successor Over 12-24 months
- Phase out founder involvement gradually
- Document all operational knowledge (SOPs, supplier lists, key contacts)
- Writer note: Abrupt handover kills businesses. A 1-2 year transition is minimum.

### Framework 2: The 4-Box Estate Planning Matrix

| | **Financial Assets** | **Business Assets** |
|---|---|---|
| **During Lifetime** | Build emergency fund, invest for retirement | Create buy-sell agreement, train successor |
| **After Death** | Will + life insurance for liquidity | Shareholder agreement, business continuation plan |

### Framework 3: Wealth Erosion Math — What Happens Without Planning

**Scenario: Budi, owner of PT Maju Jaya (trading company)**

Without succession planning:
- Budi dies suddenly at age 58
- No will, no shareholder agreement
- Assets frozen during probate: 6-24 months
- No one authorized to sign for PT — operations grind to halt
- Wife and 3 children each have equal claim per law (1/4 each)
- But only 1 child knows the business — other 2 want cash
- Forced sale of business assets at distressed prices
- Loss of key customers, suppliers lose trust
- Estimated wealth erosion: 40-70% of business value

With succession planning:
- Will drafted, shareholder agreement in place
- Successor appointed quickly (within 2-4 weeks)
- Life insurance payout covers immediate liquidity needs
- Non-business heirs receive cash/life insurance — business heir gets shares
- Business continues uninterrupted
- Estimated wealth preservation: 85-95% of business value

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

```
Myth: "Warisan hanya masalah orang kaya/sudah memiliki aset besar"
Reality: Legacy thinking applies to ANY business owner, regardless of business size. The principles of continuity, asset protection, and family governance scale up and down.
Why it matters: Small business owners are equally vulnerable to disputes, tax issues, and business collapse upon sudden death or incapacity.
Source: Indonesian inheritance law applies to all citizens regardless of wealth level.
```

```
Myth: "Anak saya pasti mau meneruskan bisnis"
Reality: Many children of business owners have NO interest in continuing the family business. CNBC Indonesia reported that generational disinterest is a growing global trend — Dalian Wanda's heir publicly refused to take over.
Why it matters: Assuming succession without confirmation leads to family conflict and business collapse.
Source: CNBC Indonesia, April 2026
```

```
Myth: "Saya masih muda (40-50 tahun), masih bisa pikirkan nanti"
Reality: The best time to start legacy thinking is when you are healthy and clear-headed. Emergency planning (after diagnosis/crisis) leads to rushed, suboptimal decisions.
Why it matters: Sudden death, divorce, disability can happen at any age.
```

```
Myth: "Indonesia tidak ada pajak warisan, jadi tidak perlu repot"
Reality: No inheritance tax does NOT mean no tax at all. Business asset transfers trigger PPh, BPHTB, and other taxes. More importantly, the real cost of not planning is business disruption — not just taxes.
Why it matters: Focus on business continuity, not just tax avoidance.
Source: UU PPh, UU BPHTB
```

```
Myth: "Yang penting bisnis berjalan, urusan warisan bisa diatur kemudian"
Reality: If the business owner dies without a succession plan, the business CANNOT legally continue until the estate is settled. Bank accounts frozen, contracts cannot be signed, payroll cannot be processed.
Why it matters: The business stops immediately. Employees go unpaid. Customers move to competitors.
Source: Corporate law practice in Indonesia
```

```
Myth: "Satu anak akan melanjutkan — yang lain pasti setuju"
Reality: Even in harmonious families, unequal inheritance (one child gets the business, others get cash/assets) causes friction. Without clear documentation, disputes can tear families apart.
Why it matters: Prevent family conflict before it happens.
```
