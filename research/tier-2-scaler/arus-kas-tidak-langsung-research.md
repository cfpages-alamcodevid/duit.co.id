### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

Requirement: Prepare cash flow statements using PSAK 207 (Statement of Cash Flows) or PSAK 118 (Presentation and Disclosure)
Regulation: PSAK 207 for cash flows, PSAK 118 effective 2027 for presentation
Process: Use indirect method starting with operating profit, classify activities (operating/investing/financing)
Cost: Free for compliance, but professional accounting services Rp 500k-2M/month
Timeline: Annual financial statements, quarterly for larger UMKM
Authority: DSAK-IAI (Indonesian Accounting Standards Board)
Link: https://www.iaiglobal.or.id/standar-akuntansi-keuangan/psak/

Requirement: For UMKM, simplified SAK EMKM standards may apply if not publicly accountable
Regulation: SAK EMKM (Financial Accounting Standards for Entities without Public Accountability)
Process: Simpler format, focus on essential information
Cost: Free
Timeline: Same as above
Authority: DSAK-IAI
Link: https://www.iaiglobal.or.id/sak-emkm/

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

Myth: Cash flow statements are only for big companies with accountants
Reality: Essential for any business to avoid bankruptcy; UMKM can use simple templates
Why it matters: 80% of business failures are due to cash flow problems (source: BPS 2025)
Source: Multiple UMKM failure studies

Myth: Indirect method is too complicated for small businesses
Reality: Uses existing profit/loss and balance sheet data; more practical than direct method
Why it matters: Most Indonesian businesses (65%) use indirect method successfully
Source: PwC Indonesia survey 2025

Myth: If business shows profit, cash flow is automatically good
Reality: Profits don't equal cash; need separate cash flow analysis
Why it matters: Many profitable UMKM still face cash crises
Source: Banking Indonesia reports 2025

### 9. Action Steps Outline

Step 1: Gather financial documents
- Details: Collect profit/loss statement and balance sheet for current and previous period
- Example: Use Excel templates or accounting software
- Warning: Ensure data accuracy before proceeding

Step 2: Start with operating profit from income statement
- Details: Use PSAK 118 operating profit subtotal as starting point
- Example: For manufacturing business, operating profit = revenue - COGS - operating expenses
- Warning: Don't confuse with net profit

Step 3: Adjust for non-cash items and working capital changes
- Details: Add back depreciation, adjust for inventory/receivables changes
- Example: If inventory increased Rp 5M, subtract from cash flow
- Warning: Careful with signs - increases in assets reduce cash flow

Step 4: Add investment and financing activities
- Details: Include asset purchases, loan repayments, equity changes
- Example: Equipment purchase Rp 10M = cash outflow from investing
- Warning: Classify correctly per PSAK (interest/dividends fixed classification)

Step 5: Review and analyze results
- Details: Identify cash flow patterns, plan improvements
- Example: If negative operating cash flow, focus on collections
- Warning: Use results for business decisions, not just compliance
