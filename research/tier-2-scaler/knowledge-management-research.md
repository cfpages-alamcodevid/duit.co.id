### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess Current Knowledge State
- Details: Conduct knowledge audit, identify gaps, survey employees
- Example: Use checklist of existing documentation
- Warning: Don't assume current practices are adequate

Step 2: Define KMS Objectives
- Details: Align with business goals (scaling, innovation, retention)
- Example: Reduce onboarding time by 50%
- Warning: Vague goals lead to poor implementation

Step 3: Choose Technology Platform
- Details: Evaluate web-based vs on-premise, integration capabilities
- Example: Lark or Notion for collaborative features
- Warning: Over-engineer with unnecessary features

Step 4: Design Knowledge Structure
- Details: Create taxonomy, tagging system, access controls
- Example: Categories: Processes, Best Practices, Training
- Warning: Poor structure leads to unused system

Step 5: Implement and Train
- Details: Pilot with small team, provide training, establish champions
- Example: 2-week pilot program
- Warning: Lack of training causes resistance

Step 6: Monitor and Improve
- Details: Track usage metrics, gather feedback, iterate
- Example: Monthly review meetings
- Warning: Set and forget leads to failure
