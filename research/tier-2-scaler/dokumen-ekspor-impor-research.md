## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
Requirement: Register as Exporter/Importer with Customs using NPWP and obtain AEO certification
Regulation: Peraturan Menteri Keuangan No. 75/PMK.01/2012 concerning Customs Procedures
Process: Online registration via CEISA portal, document submission, compliance audit
Cost: Rp 100rb-500rb for basic registration, Rp 1-5 juta for AEO
Timeline: 1-2 months
Authority: Dirjen Bea dan Cukai (DJBC), Ministry of Finance
Link: https://ceisa.kemenkeu.go.id/

Requirement: Obtain Commercial Invoice and Packing List
Regulation: UU Perdagangan No. 7 Tahun 2014, PP No. 36 Tahun 2018
Process: Prepare standardized documents through accredited seller/buyer
Cost: Rp 50rb-200rb per document set
Timeline: 1-3 days
Authority: Ministry of Trade
Link: https://www.kemendag.go.id/

Requirement: Certificate of Origin for preferential trade agreements
Regulation: ASEAN Trade in Goods Agreement (ATIGA)
Process: Apply through chamber of commerce within 5 days of order
Cost: Rp 100rb-500rb per certificate
Timeline: 3-7 days
Authority: Indonesia Chamber of Commerce (Kadin)
Link: https://www.kadin.id/certificates

Requirement: Phytosanitary/Health certificates for food/agricultural exports
Regulation: Permentan No. 23 Tahun 2022
Process: Laboratory testing and certification
Cost: Rp 500rb-2 juta depending on product type
Timeline: 7-14 days
Authority: Ministry of Agriculture (Karantina Pertanian)
Link: https://karantina.pertanian.go.id/

Requirement: Customs Declaration via CEISA system
Regulation: Peraturan Menteri Keuangan PMK 155/2022
Process: Electronic submission before goods arrival/departure
Cost: Rp 50rb per declaration (if using broker)
Timeline: 24 hours
Authority: Dirjen Bea dan Cukai
Link: https://ceisa.kemenkeu.go.id/

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Export paperwork is too complex and expensive for small businesses
Reality: With CEISA digitization and AEO program, SMEs can achieve compliance for under Rp 5 juta initial setup, breaking even on first $50k shipment
Why it matters: Many SMEs miss international growth opportunities due to this false belief
Source: DJBC SME guide 2025

Myth: Imports always involve high duties that eliminate profit margins
Reality: Under RCEP, duties on many electronics/manufactured goods are 0%, and proper H.S. classification can reduce duties by 50%
Why it matters: Prevents entrepreneurs from exploring import arbitrage opportunities
Source: Ministry of Trade RCEP implementation report 2024

Myth: You need physical certificates for every export shipment
Reality: Digital CEISA allows electronic certificates for standard exports, reducing time from 2 weeks to 1 day
Why it matters: Digital shift in 2026 makes paperless trade standard
Source: World Trade Organization digital trade trends 2025

## 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Registrasi Dasar (Register as trader)
- Details needed: NPWP update, customs number application, basic company docs
- Example: Company with Rp 10 billion turnover, AEO tier 2 application
- Warning: Avoid using expired NPWP or wrong business type

Step 2: Identifikasi Produk dan Pasar (Product and market selection)
- Details needed: H.S. code classification, target country regulations
- Example: Textile exporter checking EU textile quotas
- Warning: Underestimating seasonal demand fluctuations

Step 3: Prepare Dokumen Inti (Core document preparation)
- Details needed: Invoice, packing list, CO if needed
- Example: Furniture export package including phytosanitary cert
- Warning: Mismatched invoice values leading to customs penalties

Step 4: Penggunaan Sistem CEISA (CEISA system usage)
- Details needed: Register account, create profiles, submit declarations
- Example: Electronically submit PPIA for export notification
- Warning: Technical errors causing system rejection after deadline

Step 5: Kerjasama dengan Broker dan Logistik (Broker and logistics partnership)
- Details needed: Choose accredited PPJK, negotiate freight rates
- Example: Fixed-cost routing for consistent pricing
- Warning: Choosing unlicensed brokers leading to fraud risk
