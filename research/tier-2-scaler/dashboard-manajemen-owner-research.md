### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess business needs and current processes
- Details: Inventory audits, sales tracking review, budget vs actual analysis, employee metrics collection.
- Example: Spend 1 week documenting manual processes.
- Warning: Don't skip inventory counts; inaccurate data leads to bad dashboards.

Step 2: Choose and purchase dashboard tool
- Details: Compare Jurnal, Accurate, Biztrack; focus on dashboard features for sales/inventory/cashflow/people.
- Example: Free trial Accurate for 30 days, input data.
- Warning: Ensure tool integrates with existing accounting; changing tools post-setup costs double.

Step 3: Set up key metrics and configure alerts
- Details: Define KPIs (sales conversion 20%, inventory turnover 5x/year, cash runway 6 months, employee productivity 80%).
- Example: Configure sales dashboard with weekly targets.
- Warning: Start simple; too many metrics overwhelm beginners.

Step 4: Train team and integrate into operations
- Details: Sessions on data input, report generation; assign data entry roles.
- Example: 2-hour training session with consultant.
- Warning: Without training, resistance from employees can delay adoption 2 months.

Step 5: Monitor, analyze, and iterate dashboard
- Details: Weekly reviews, adjust KPIs based on business changes (e.g., seasonal sales).
- Example: End-month deep dive on cashflow anomalies.
- Warning: Ignore data trends at peril; leads to repeating mistakes.
