### 5. What Makes a Business Sellable (The "Sellability Score")

Based on research synthesis from Forbes, Protemus Exit Readiness Framework, and Indonesian M&A advisory practices:

**The 5 Dimensions of Sellability:**

**1. Owner Independence (Bobot: 30%)**
- Can the business run 30 days without the owner?
- Are SOPs documented?
- Is there a management team (even 1-2 people) that makes decisions?
- **Reality:** Most Indonesian SMEs fail here — "bisnis itu saya"

**2. Financial Hygiene (Bobot: 25%)**
- Are business and personal finances separated?
- Is there clean bookkeeping (at least 2 years)?
- Are tax reports filed correctly?
- **Reality:** This is the #1 deal-killer in due diligence

**3. Revenue Predictability (Bobot: 20%)**
- Is revenue consistent month-to-month?
- Is there recurring revenue (subscription, retainer, repeat customers)?
- Is customer base diversified (not 1-2 clients)?
- **Reality:** Buyers pay premium for predictability

**4. Legal & Structure Cleanliness (Bobot: 15%)**
- Is the business entity properly registered (PT, CV, NIB)?
- Are permits complete (BPOM, Halal, PIRT, Sertifikasi)?
- Is intellectual property protected (HAKI, merek)?
- **Reality:** Legal issues can kill a deal at the last minute

**5. Growth Story (Bobot: 10%)**
- Is there a clear growth trajectory?
- Is the market growing?
- Are there untapped opportunities a buyer could exploit?
- **Reality:** Buyers don't buy history — they buy future potential

**Sellability Score Interpretation:**
- **0-30:** Not sellable. You don't own an asset, you own a job.
- **31-60:** Potentially sellable with 12-24 months of preparation
- **61-80:** Attractive to strategic buyers and local PE
- **81-100:** Premium valuation — multiple exit options

### 6. Market Research: The "Business as Asset" vs "Business as Job" Divide

**The Core Insight:**
> "If your business can't operate without you, it's not an asset — it's a job with extra steps and higher risk."

**Why most Indonesian small businesses are NOT assets:**
1. **Owner is the product** — 78% of UMKM operate solo (60 Decibels Indonesia Report 2025)
2. **No SOP/System** — Business runs on the owner's memory and relationships
3. **Financial commingling** — Personal and business accounts mixed (common in >90% of UMKM without legal entity)
4. **No transferable value** — The business has no value to anyone except the current owner
5. **Revenue is unpredictable** — No recurring revenue model, no contracts, no retention system

**The "Exit Premium":**
- A business designed for exit commands 1.5x - 2.5x higher multiple than one that isn't
- Reason: Lower risk for buyer, smoother transition, proven systems

**What buyers look for (Forbes, 2026):**
- Pendapatan yang dapat diprediksi dan konsisten
- Profit yang kuat dan konsisten
- Laporan keuangan yang bersih dan transparan
- Operasional yang jelas dan dapat diskalakan
- Basis pelanggan yang beragam

### 7. Indonesian Cultural Context: The Resistance to Exit Thinking

**Cultural barriers specific to Indonesia:**

1. **"Ini bisnis keluarga, tidak akan dijual"** — 95% of businesses are family businesses (Deloitte), and the very concept of selling is taboo
2. **"Saya harus mewariskan ke anak"** — Strong cultural expectation to pass the business to children, even when children aren't interested (only 31% of NextGen willing to take over per Sun Life survey)
3. **"Bisnis ini saya yang bangun dari nol"** — Emotional attachment prevents objective valuation; founders see their "blood, sweat, and tears" as value, but buyers only see future cash flows
4. **"Ah, nanti saja mikir exit"** — Present bias; survival and growth consume all attention, exit planning seems like a "future problem"
5. **Collectivist culture (gotong royong)** — Sundanese and Javanese business culture prioritize family involvement over professional management; hiring non-family managers is resisted (MDPI Sustainability study)
6. **High power distance** — Founders struggle to delegate to professionals; "owner is the best manager" mentality (Hofstede dimensions research)

**The numbers that prove this:**
- Only 27% of Asian family business owners have complete succession plans (Sun Life 2025)
- 43% of Indonesian NextGen say senior leaders block succession (PwC 2025)
- 70% of family businesses don't survive to the second generation (classic multi-country data)
- Only 13% make it to third generation (Deloitte Indonesia)
- 19% of Indonesian family businesses delay succession due to uncertainty (vs 10% globally, PwC 2025)

### 8. Case Studies: Indonesia Context

**Case Study 1: The Printing Shop That Became Sellable (Adapted from existing Tier 3 research)**
- **Persona:** "Pak Tedi, pemilik jaringan percetakan 10 outlet di Jawa Timur"
- **Starting point:** Owner-operated, no SOP, family-run, revenue Rp 500 juta/bulan
- **The problem:** When he wanted to retire, no buyer was interested. Business was "Pak Tedi" — not a system.
- **The transformation (18 months):** Hired operations manager, documented SOPs, separated finances, built a sales team
- **Result:** Sold to a larger digital printing firm for 4.5x EBITDA (Rp 12 Miliar)
- **What if he hadn't prepared:** Would have sold for ~2x EBITDA as a "one-man show" = roughly Rp 5 Miliar
- **Key learning:** Systemization added Rp 7+ Miliar in exit value

**Case Study 2: The F&B Business That Couldn't Exit**
- **Persona:** "Mbak Rina, pemilik kafe di Bandung, 5 tahun berjalan"
- **Starting point:** Omzet Rp 80 juta/bulan, profit Rp 15 juta/bulan, tapi semua resep ada di kepalanya, semua hubungan supplier via WA pribadi, semua keputusan harus lewat dia
- **The problem:** Ingin jual karena pindah ke luar negeri, tapi calon pembeli mundur semua. Alasannya: "Ini bukan bisnis, ini Mbak Rina."
- **Result:** Tidak laku dijual. Akhirnya tutup dan hanya dapat Rp 150 juta dari likuidasi peralatan
- **What could have been:** Jika ada SOP, resep terdokumentasi, manajer yang bisa jalan, nilai bisnis 3x annual profit = Rp 540 juta
- **Key learning:** Bisnis yang tergantung pemilik ≈ Rp 0 ketika pemilik pergi

**Case Study 3: The Software Agency That Achieved 8x Exit**
- **Persona:** "Tim, founder software house di Jakarta, 20 karyawan"
- **Starting point:** Service-based, project-driven, revenue Rp 3 Miliar/tahun
- **Strategic shift (2 years before exit):** Built 2 SaaS products (recurring revenue), hired CTO and COO, systematized sales, created client onboarding SOP
- **Result:** Acquired by Singapore-based PE firm for 8x EBITDA (Rp 48 Miliar)
- **Key learning:** Recurring revenue + management team = premium valuation

**Case Study 4: The Family Business That Just... Stopped**
- **Persona:** "Pak Haji, pemilik toko bangunan di Cirebon, 30 tahun berjalan"
- **Starting point:** Toko besar, omzet stabil, semua anak-anak kerja di toko
- **The problem:** Anak-anak tidak tertarik melanjutkan, ingin karir sendiri
- **The trap:** Tidak pernah menyiapkan exit. Tanpa Pak Haji, toko tidak bisa jalan. Anak-anak tidak punya passion.
- **Result:** Toko tutup setelah Pak Haji pensiun. Aset tanah dan bangunan dijual. Bisnis (goodwill, pelanggan, supplier network) — Rp 0
- **Key learning:** Family business tanpa succession plan = going concern value hilang

### 9. Practical Frameworks

**Framework 1: The "Apakah Bisnis Anda Aset?" Test (Simple 5-Question)**

Tanya diri Anda:
1. Jika saya pergi liburan 1 bulan tanpa HP, apakah bisnis tetap menghasilkan uang? (Ya/Tidak)
2. Apakah ada orang lain yang bisa mengambil keputusan operasional tanpa saya? (Ya/Tidak)
3. Apakah laporan keuangan bisnis terpisah dari keuangan pribadi? (Ya/Tidak)
4. Apakah ada SOP tertulis untuk proses utama bisnis? (Ya/Tidak)
5. Apakah ada sistem yang memastikan pelanggan tetap datang tanpa saya? (Ya/Tidak)

**Interpretasi:**
- 5 "Ya" = Bisnis Anda adalah aset. Bisa dijual dengan harga premium.
- 3-4 "Ya" = Dalam perjalanan. Fokus pada yang masih "Tidak".
- 0-2 "Ya" = Anda tidak punya bisnis. Anda punya pekerjaan dengan risiko lebih tinggi.

**Framework 2: The Exit Readiness Timeline (Simplified for Tier 2)**

**24 Bulan Sebelum Target Exit:**
- Pisahkan keuangan pribadi dan bisnis
- Mulai pembukuan formal (bisa pakai software akuntansi atau buku besar)
- Dokumentasikan semua SOP (operasional, penjualan, pelayanan)
- Catat aset dan inventaris

**12 Bulan Sebelum Target Exit:**
- Bentuk tim manajemen minimal (1-2 orang yang bisa ambil keputusan)
- Bangun recurring revenue (langganan, retainer, kontrak)
- Luruskan legalitas (NIB, izin usaha, HAKI, merek)
- Audit pajak — pastikan bersih

**6 Bulan Sebelum Target Exit:**
- Siapkan data room (laporan keuangan 2-3 tahun, kontrak pelanggan, data supplier)
- Hitung valuasi dengan metode sederhana (lihat section di atas)
- Cari advisor atau broker bisnis
- Prepare teaser/summary bisnis

**3 Bulan Sebelum Target Exit:**
- Uji coba: tinggalkan bisnis 1 minggu penuh
- Perbaiki bottleneck
- Siapkan jawaban untuk due diligence

**Framework 3: Valuation Methods for Small Businesses (Simplified)**

For Tier 2 audience (revenue Rp 500Jt - Rp 50M), use the **Multiple of Earnings** method:

```
Nilai Bisnis = (Laba Bersih Tahunan + Gaji Pemilik + Biaya Pribadi) × Multiple Industri
```

**Example:**
- Laba bersih: Rp 200 juta/tahun
- Gaji pemilik: Rp 120 juta/tahun
- Biaya pribadi (mobil, travel): Rp 30 juta/tahun
- **SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) = Rp 350 juta**
- Multiple untuk jasa/retail: 2.5x - 3.5x
- **Valuasi = Rp 875 juta - Rp 1.225 Miliar**

**Valuasi minimum** (asset-based, jika bisnis tidak sistemik):
- Nilai aset (peralatan, inventaris, kas) - utang
- **Typical: 30-50% lebih rendah dari multiple method**

### 10. Financial Analysis: The Numbers That Prove the Thesis

**Scenario A: "Saya = Bisnis" (Unsellable)**
- Revenue: Rp 50 juta/bulan

**The Difference: >Rp 1 Miliar** — just from systematizing

**ROI of becoming exit-ready:**
- Cost: Rp 20-50 juta (bookkeeper, konsultan legal, SOP documentation)
- Time: 12-18 months preparation
- Potential value gain: Rp 500 juta - Rp 5 Miliar+
- **ROI: 1,000% - 10,000%+**

Source: Forbes, Exit Planning Institute

Reality: Every business — from warung to pabrik kecil — can and should be built for transferability.

Myth: "Jual bisnis itu cepat dan mudah"

- Warning: SOP bukan untuk Anda — untuk orang lain yang akan menjalankan

- Details needed: Identify 1-2 people who can make decisions without you

- Details needed: Langganan, retainer, kontrak pemeliharaan, repeat order system
- Example: Andi buat paket "langganan service mobil per bulan"

Step 6: Clean your legal and tax
- Details needed: NIB, permits, tax reports, entity structure
- Example: If still using personal NPWP for business, upgrade to badan
- Warning: Legal issues discovered during due diligence = deal killer

Step 7: Calculate your valuation — and work to increase it
- Details needed: Use SDE x industry multiple
- Example: Target multiple meningkat dari 2x ke 4x
- Warning: Multiple meningkat KETIKA risiko turun dan sistem terbukti

### Summary

This research document provides comprehensive data and frameworks for the "Exit Ready" article. The core thesis — that a business which cannot be sold is not a true asset — is supported by extensive data:

- **M&A market:** 150 deals worth US$14.2B in Indonesia (2023), mid-market US$6.2B (2025)
- **Exit readiness crisis:** 73% of Asian family businesses lack complete succession plans; only 39% of Indonesian businesses have one
- **Cultural barriers:** 43% NextGen blocked by seniors, 95% of businesses are family-owned
- **Valuation impact:** Sellable businesses command 1.5-2.5x higher multiples than owner-dependent ones
- **The math:** A Rp 300j SDE business at 2x = Rp 600j; same business at 4x = Rp 1.2M — the difference of Rp 600j+ comes from systematization

The article fills a clear gap: existing Duit.co.id content covers *how* to exit (tactical), *what* your business is worth (valuation), and *which* exit option to choose (strategy). This article covers *why* exit thinking matters and *whether* your business is even sellable — the foundational mindset shift.

### Key Insights

1. **The sellability test is brutally simple:** If your business can't run 30 days without you, its resale value is near zero regardless of profits.
2. **Indonesian cultural resistance is the #1 barrier:** "Bisnis keluarga," "warisan ke anak," and "saya yang bangun" are the three most dangerous mindsets for exit readiness.
3. **Systemization is worth millions:** The difference between 2x and 4x EBITDA multiple comes almost entirely from documented systems, team independence, and clean books — not revenue growth.
4. **Recurring revenue = premium multiple:** Businesses with subscriptions, retainers, or repeat contracts command 2-3x higher multiples than project-based ones.
5. **Most owners wait too long:** The best time to prepare for exit is when the business is at its peak — but most wait until burnout, losing 50%+ of potential valuation.
