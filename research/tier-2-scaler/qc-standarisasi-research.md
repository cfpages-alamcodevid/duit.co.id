## Legal & Regulatory Requirements

**Requirement:** SNI (Standar Nasional Indonesia) compliance for regulated products  
**Regulation:** UU No. 3 Tahun 2014 tentang Perindustrian  
**Process:** Register through BSN website, implement standards, self-declare compliance  
**Cost:** Rp 500rb-2 juta for registration and testing  
**Timeline:** 1-3 months  
**Authority:** Badan Standardisasi Nasional (BSN)  
**Link:** https://bsn.go.id/  

**Requirement:** BPOM certification for food, cosmetics, supplements  
**Regulation:** UU No. 36 Tahun 2009 tentang Kesehatan  
**Process:** Register products, submit safety data, undergo testing  
**Cost:** Rp 2-10 juta depending on product category  
**Timeline:** 2-6 months  
**Authority:** Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan (BPOM)  
**Link:** https://bpom.go.id/  

**Requirement:** Halal certification for food products  
**Regulation:** UU No. 33 Tahun 2014 tentang Jaminan Produk Halal  
**Process:** Register with MUI, audit production facilities, obtain certificate  
**Cost:** Rp 1-5 juta for small businesses  
**Timeline:** 1-3 months  
**Authority:** Majelis Ulama Indonesia (MUI)  
**Link:** https://mui.or.id/  

**Requirement:** ISO 9001 certification for quality management systems  
**Regulation:** International standard adopted in Indonesia  
**Process:** Gap analysis, documentation, implementation, internal audit, external certification  
**Cost:** Rp 40-110 juta total (consultant + audit)  
**Timeline:** 3-6 months  
**Authority:** BSN/KAN accredited certification bodies  
**Link:** https://www.iso.org/iso-9001-quality-management.html

## Common Myths & Misconceptions

**Myth:** QC and standardization are only for large companies  
**Reality:** ISO 9001 is designed to be scalable for organizations of any size, including UMKM with <50 employees  
**Why it matters:** Prevents SMEs from accessing certification benefits like export opportunities and efficiency gains  
**Source:** International Organization for Standardization guidelines  
**Quote-ready:** Yes  

**Myth:** Certification costs are too high for small businesses  
**Reality:** Total costs Rp 45-125 juta can be recouped within 6-12 months through revenue increases and cost savings  
**Why it matters:** Fear of costs prevents adoption despite proven ROI  
**Source:** Indonesian SME case studies (2025-2026)  

**Myth:** Six Sigma is too complex for Indonesian SMEs  
**Reality:** Basic Six Sigma tools like DMAIC can be simplified for small operations without advanced statistics  
**Why it matters:** Discourages use of proven quality improvement methods  
**Source:** Lean Six Sigma for Small Businesses literature  

## Action Steps Outline

**Step 1: Assess Current Quality Systems**  
- Details needed: Map existing processes, identify quality gaps, evaluate customer feedback  
- Example: Create simple checklist for product defects and customer complaints  
- Warning: Don't skip this step - understanding current state prevents wasted resources  

**Step 2: Choose Appropriate Standards**  
- Details needed: Match business needs with SNI/ISO/BPOM requirements based on industry  
- Example: Food business starts with BPOM and halal, then ISO 9001  
- Warning: Start with mandatory regulations before voluntary certifications  

**Step 3: Develop Quality Documentation**  
- Details needed: Create SOPs, quality policies, audit procedures  
- Example: Document production process with photos and checklists  
- Warning: Keep documentation simple and practical, not overly bureaucratic  

**Step 4: Train Staff and Implement**  
- Details needed: Conduct training sessions, assign quality responsibilities  
- Example: Weekly quality meetings to review metrics  
- Warning: Ensure management commitment - QC fails without leadership support  

**Step 5: Audit and Certify**  
- Details needed: Internal audits, external certification, continuous improvement  
- Example: Schedule quarterly internal audits, annual external surveillance  
- Warning: Treat certification as starting point, not end goal
