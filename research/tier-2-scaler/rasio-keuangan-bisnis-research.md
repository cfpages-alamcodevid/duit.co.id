## 4. Market Research (For Business Idea Articles)

*Note: This is financial ratios analysis article, not business startup. Market research focuses on ratio benchmarks and trends.*

**Current Benchmarks (2025-2026):**

**Liquidity Ratios:**
- Current Ratio: 1.5-2.5 (healthy), below 1.0 risky
- Quick Ratio: 1.0-2.0 (adequate), below 0.8 concerning
- Cash Ratio: 0.2-0.5 (optimal for operations)

**Profitability Ratios:**
- Net Profit Margin: 5-20% (varies by industry)
- ROA: 5-15% (efficient use of assets)
- ROE: 10-25% (return to owners)

**Solvency Ratios:**
- Debt-to-Asset: <50% (conservative), 50-70% (moderate)
- Debt-to-Equity: <1.0 (safe), 1.0-2.0 (leveraged)

**Efficiency Ratios:**
- Total Asset Turnover: 1.5-3.0x (asset utilization)
- Inventory Turnover: 4-12x (depending on industry)

**Industry Variations:**
- Retail: Higher inventory turnover (6-12x), moderate margins (10-15%)
- Manufacturing: Lower turnover (2-4x), higher margins (15-25%)
- Services: High turnover (3-5x), variable margins (5-20%)

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Jaya Ponsel UMKM
**Background:** Mobile phone sales and repair SME in Indonesia
**Starting Point:** 2022 ratios - Current Ratio 2.5, NPM 20%, ROE 11.11%
**Result:** 2023 ratios - Current Ratio 2.2, NPM 22%, ROE 12.63%
**Timeline:** 1 year improvement
**Key Learning:** Profitability improved despite declining liquidity, showing focus on margins over short-term cash
**Source:** Jurnal Pariwisata Bisnis Digital dan Manajemen, 2024

### Case Study 2: Retail UMKM Indonesia
**Background:** General retail SME
**Starting Point:** High NPM (26.6-26.8%), conservative DER (<10%)
**Result:** Declining efficiency - Total Asset Turnover from 4.92x to 3.02x
**Timeline:** 1 year period
**Key Learning:** Strong profits but operational inefficiencies in inventory management
**Source:** JASABE Journal, 2026

### Case Study 3: Indofood Sukses Makmur
**Background:** Large consumer goods company (proxy for scaled SME)
**Starting Point:** Liquidity below industry standards, high DAR (>100%)
**Result:** Suboptimal ROA/ROE, dependency on debt financing
**Timeline:** Multi-year trend 2020-2023
**Key Learning:** High leverage hurts profitability ratios despite market position
**Source:** JRIP Journal, 2025

### Expert Opinion: Dr. Financial Distress Analysis

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios

### Persona 1: Retail Business Owner
**Situation:** Toko kelontong dengan omzet Rp 500 juta/bulan
**Current Ratios:**
- Current Ratio: 1.8 (Rp 180 juta current assets / Rp 100 juta current liabilities)
- NPM: 12% (Rp 60 juta net profit / Rp 500 juta revenue)
- Inventory Turnover: 8x (good for retail)

**Analysis:** Business is liquid and profitable, but could improve by reducing inventory holding period.

### Persona 2: Manufacturing SME
**Situation:** Pabrik furniture dengan aset Rp 2 milyar
**Current Ratios:**
- Debt-to-Asset: 45% (Rp 900 juta debt / Rp 2 milyar assets)
- ROA: 8% (Rp 160 juta profit / Rp 2 milyar avg assets)
- Asset Turnover: 2.5x (Rp 5 milyar sales / Rp 2 milyar avg assets)

**Analysis:** Moderate leverage, good profitability, efficient asset use.

### Calculation Example: Quick Ratio
```
Current Assets = Rp 150 juta
Minus Inventory = Rp 50 juta
Quick Assets = Rp 100 juta
Current Liabilities = Rp 80 juta
Quick Ratio = 100 / 80 = 1.25x

Interpretation: Good liquidity, can pay 125% of short-term debts with quick assets.
```

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

**Myth:** High profitability ratios always mean good business health
**Reality:** Jaya Ponsel case shows declining liquidity despite rising profits
**Why it matters:** Ignores cash flow and working capital needs
**Source:** Multiple SME studies 2024-2026

**Myth:** Debt ratios below 50% are always safe
**Reality:** Depends on industry; tech firms may need higher leverage for growth
**Why it matters:** Over-conservative financing limits scalability
**Source:** Tech company ratio analysis research

**Myth:** ROE above 20% is excellent
**Reality:** May indicate excessive leverage increasing risk
**Why it matters:** High ROE from debt isn't sustainable if interest coverage weak
**Source:** Financial distress prediction studies
