- Marketing budget (first 3 months): Rp 1-2 million (ads, content creation)  
- Tools & software: Rp 500rb-1 million (inventory management, analytics)  
- Emergency buffer: Rp 1 million (supplier issues, returns)  
- Legal setup: Rp 500rb (business registration, tax preparation)  
**Total:** Rp 3-5 million  

**Where to buy:**

## Legal & Regulatory Requirements
**Requirement:** Business registration (PT Perorangan or CV)  
**Regulation:** UU Cipta Kerja 2024  
**Process:** Register online via OSS (Online Single Submission) system  
**Cost:** Rp 50rb-200rb  
**Timeline:** 1-3 days  
**Authority:** Kemenkumham  
**Link:** https://oss.go.id  

**Requirement:** NPWP and tax compliance  
**Regulation:** UU Pajak Penghasilan  
**Process:** Register NPWP online, file SPT annually, calculate PPh final 0.5% for UMKM  
**Cost:** Free registration, compliance costs Rp 500rb-1 million/year  
**Timeline:** Monthly reporting  
**Authority:** DJP (Direktorat Jenderal Pajak)  
**Link:** https://www.pajak.go.id  

**Requirement:** Consumer protection compliance  
**Regulation:** UU Perlindungan Konsumen  
**Process:** Provide clear product info, handle complaints within 7 days  
**Cost:** Minimal, potential fines up to Rp 200 million for violations  
**Timeline:** Ongoing  
**Authority:** BPKN (Badan Perlindungan Konsumen Nasional)  
**Link:** https://bpkn.go.id

## Common Myths & Misconceptions
**Myth:** Dropshipping lokal mudah dan passive income  
**Reality:** Requires active marketing, customer service, and supplier management  
**Why it matters:** Many fail expecting "set and forget" model  
**Source:** Multiple failed dropship cases from forum discussions  

**Myth:** Modal kecil berarti profit besar otomatis  
**Reality:** Competition drives margins down, scaling requires investment in marketing and systems  
**Why it matters:** Unrealistic expectations lead to quick abandonment  
**Source:** Industry reports showing 70% failure rate in first year  

**Myth:** Supplier lokal lebih aman dari internasional  
**Reality:** Local suppliers can have quality issues, delays, or business closure risks  
**Why it matters:** Diversification still needed for business continuity

**Step 1:** Research and validate niche market  
- Details: Analyze Google Trends, competitor analysis, customer surveys

- Warning: Have backup suppliers for continuity  

**Step 3:** Setup operational systems  
- Details: Choose marketplace platforms, inventory tracking tools

- Warning: Start simple, scale complexity gradually  

**Step 4:** Develop marketing strategy  
- Details: Content marketing, paid ads, influencer partnerships

**Step 5:** Implement scaling systems
