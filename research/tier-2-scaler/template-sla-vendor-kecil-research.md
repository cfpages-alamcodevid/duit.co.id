## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### SLA Legality in Indonesia

SLAs are legally binding in Indonesia when they meet the contract requirements under **Pasal 1320 KUHPerdata**:

| Requirement | Application to SLA |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **1. Kesepakatan (Consent)** | Both parties agree to the SLA terms (signature or digital acknowledgment) |
| **2. Kecakapan (Capacity)** | Both parties are legally capable (18+ / married, not under guardianship) |
| **3. Hal Tertentu (Specific Subject)** | Services, standards, and remedies must be clearly defined |
| **4. Sebab yang Halal (Lawful Cause)** | SLA cannot require illegal acts |

### Key Legal Considerations

```
Requirement: SLA must be signed by both parties
Regulation: Pasal 1320 KUHPerdata
Process: Print + sign OR use digital signature (Meterai Rp 10.000 for formal contracts)
Cost: Rp 10.000 (meterai if printed)
Timeline: Immediate
Authority: Not applicable (private agreement)
```

```
Requirement: Small claims court
Regulation: PERMA No. 4 Tahun 2019
Process: File gugatan sederhana at Pengadilan Negeri for disputes ≤ Rp 500 juta
Cost: Rp 1-5 juta (legal fees, proceeding)
Timeline: 1-3 months
Authority: Pengadilan Negeri
```

```
Requirement: Mediation clause
Regulation: PERMA No. 1 Tahun 2016 tentang Mediasi
Process: Include clause stating "Any dispute will first go through mediation"
Cost: Minimal (usually split 50/50)
Timeline: 2-4 weeks
Authority: Mediator (can be agreed upon mutually)
```

```
Requirement: PPN/withholding tax implications on penalties
Regulation: UU PPh and UU PPN
Process: Penalty received by vendor may be taxable income
Cost: Varies (consult with tax advisor)
Timeline: Ongoing
Authority: DJP (Ditjen Pajak)
Link: pajak.go.id
```

### Cultural Considerations

**Key insight for the writer:**
- In Indonesian business culture, written SLAs can be seen as "tidak percaya" (distrustful)
- Many small vendors operate on relationship-based trust ("sudah kenal lama")
- The article MUST address how to introduce SLAs as PROTECTION FOR BOTH SIDES, not as a weapon
- Frame SLA as "biar sama-sama enak" (mutual benefit) rather than "kalau lu melanggar gua tuntut"
- Important to note: SLAs protect the vendor too — clear scope prevents scope creep, clear payment terms ensure timely payment

### Calculation Example: Penalty for Late Delivery

```
Scenario: Supplier late delivery of raw materials for frozen food business

SLA terms:
- Delivery must be received by 08:00 every Monday and Thursday
- If late: Penalty Rp 50.000 per hour of delay, max Rp 500.000 per delivery
- If >24 hours late: Buyer can source from backup supplier at vendor's cost

Real calculation:
- Supplier ayam delivers at 14:00 instead of 08:00 (6 hours late)
- Penalty: 6 × Rp 50.000 = Rp 300.000
- This covers the cost of Rina having to reschedule production and pay overtime

Impact of NO SLA:
- Rina just accepts the delay
- Production starts 6 hours late
- Staff idle for 6 hours: payroll wasted Rp 200.000
- Some orders delayed to customers: lost goodwill

With SLA:
- Supplier knows there's consequence
- Supplier improves delivery (from 60% on-time to 90%+)
- Even when late, Rina gets compensation
```

### Calculation Example: Quality Rejection

```
Scenario: Packaging supplier delivers boxes with wrong dimensions

SLA terms:
- Quality: Must match approved sample (±2mm tolerance)
- Rejection: 100% replacement at vendor's cost within 48 hours
- Defect rate target: ≤2% of order
- If defect rate >5% for 2 consecutive months: Contract review/renegotiation

Cost of no SLA:
- Rina receives wrong boxes
- Can't use them, but already paid Rp 1.500.000
- Urgent order from different supplier costs Rp 2.000.000 (rush fee)
- Total loss: Rp 3.500.000 + production delay

With SLA:
- Vendor replaces at their cost: Rp 0 loss
- Vendor pays rush shipping: Rp 0 loss
- Vendor motivated to check quality before shipping
```

## 9. Suggested Template Structure (The Core Deliverable)

The article should provide a **fill-in-the-blanks SLA template** in 1-2 pages. Here's what it should include:

### SECTION A: IDENTITAS PARA PIHAK
```
1. [Nama Vendor/Badan Usaha]
   Alamat: [Alamat Lengkap]
   PIC: [Nama Kontak Person]
   No. HP/WA: [Nomor]

2. [Nama Pemilik/Badan Usaha UMKM]
   Alamat: [Alamat Lengkap]
   PIC: [Nama Kontak Person]
   No. HP/WA: [Nomor]
```

### SECTION B: LINGKUP LAYANAN
```
Vendor setuju untuk menyediakan jasa/barang sebagai berikut:
1. [Jenis layanan / barang yang disediakan]
2. [Frekuensi / jadwal pengiriman]
3. [Lokasi pengiriman / pelaksanaan]
4. [Volume / kuantitas per periode]
5. [Harga satuan dan total per periode]
```

### SECTION C: STANDAR KUALITAS
```
1. Spesifikasi barang: [sesuai sample / standar tertentu]
2. Kemasan: [jenis kemasan, label]
3. Pengiriman: [waktu, suhu, handling khusus]
4. Dokumentasi: [surat jalan, faktur, sertifikat]
```

### SECTION D: RESPON DAN RESOLUSI WAKTU
```
1. Response time untuk keluhan: [jam] (misal: 2 jam kerja)
2. Resolution time: [jam/hari] (misal: 1 hari kerja)
3. Jika butuh sparepart / material: [hari]
4. Jam operasional layanan: [hari + jam]
```

### SECTION E: LAPORAN (REPORTING)
```
Vendor wajib memberikan laporan:
1. Frekuensi: [mingguan / bulanan]
2. Format: [WhatsApp / Email / Google Spreadsheet bersama]
3. Isi laporan: [jumlah barang, kualitas, pengiriman, kendala]
4. Deadline laporan: [hari + jam]
```

### SECTION F: PENALTI
```
Atas pelanggaran terhadap SLA ini, berlaku penalti:

1. Keterlambatan pengiriman:
   - Per [jam/hari] keterlambatan: Rp [jumlah]
   - Maksimal penalti: Rp [jumlah] per kejadian
   - Jika telat > [waktu], pembeli berhak beli dari vendor lain

2. Kualitas tidak sesuai:
   - Barang ditolak 100%, vendor wajib ganti dalam [waktu]
   - Biaya pengiriman pengganti ditanggung vendor
   - Jika cacat > [persen] dalam 1 bulan: diskon [persen] dari total tagihan

3. Pembatalan sepihak:
   - Vendor batalkan pesanan tanpa pemberitahuan [waktu] sebelumnya: denda Rp [jumlah]

4. Keterlambatan pembayaran (dari pihak UMKM):
   - Per hari keterlambatan: Rp [jumlah] atau [persen] dari tagihan
```

### SECTION G: ESKALASI
```
Jika terjadi masalah yang tidak terselesaikan dalam [waktu]:

1. Level 1: PIC Vendor ↔ PIC UMKM
2. Level 2: Manager Vendor ↔ Owner UMKM
3. Level 3: Mediasi oleh [nama mediator / lembaga mediasi]
4. Level 4: Gugatan sederhana ke Pengadilan Negeri [kota]
```

### SECTION H: JANGKA WAKTU DAN PEMBAHARUAN
```
1. SLA berlaku mulai: [tanggal]
2. Jangka waktu: [3/6/12 bulan]
3. Review SLA: setiap [bulan] atau setelah [kejadian tertentu]
4. Pembaharuan otomatis jika tidak ada pemberitahuan [hari] sebelum habis
5. Penghentian SLA: salah satu pihak dapat menghentikan dengan pemberitahuan tertulis [hari] sebelumnya
```

## 10. Industry-Specific SLA Considerations

### A. IT/Software Vendor SLA
- **Uptime guarantee:** 99% (monthly) or 95% (minimum)
- **Response time:** Critical issue = 2 hours, Normal = 24 hours
- **Data backup:** Frequency, retention period, restore time
- **Security:** Data breach notification (within 24-48 hours)
- **Key metric:** MTTR (Mean Time To Repair)
- **Key for UMKM:** Keep it simple — "mati total diperbaiki dalam 1 hari kerja"

### B. Cleaning/Maintenance Vendor SLA
- **Frequency:** X times per week, specific days
- **Checklist:** Each visit must have task list signed off
- **Quality inspection:** Random spot check by UMKM owner
- **Key metric:** Checklist completion rate (≥95%)
- **Key for UMKM:** Include photo evidence requirement

### C. Raw Material Supplier SLA
- **Delivery time:** Day + time window (e.g., Monday before 08:00)
- **Quality specs:** Match approved sample, including weight, dimensions, freshness
- **Packaging:** Type, labeling requirements, tamper-evident
- **Key metric:** On-time delivery rate (OTIF — On Time In Full)
- **Key for UMKM:** Include rejection + replacement clause

### D. Logistics/Courier SLA
- **Delivery time:** Same day / next day / express
- **Damage handling:** Photo evidence at pickup and delivery
- **Tracking:** Real-time tracking link required
- **Claim process:** Time limit for damage claims, documentation required
- **Key metric:** Delivery success rate ≥98%
- **Key for UMKM:** Clarify liability for damages (insured value vs actual value)

### E. Production Vendor SLA
- **MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity):** Clearly stated per SKU
- **Lead time:** From PO to delivery
- **Defect rate:** Acceptable percentage (usually ≤2-5%)
- **Rework/replacement:** Vendor absorbs cost
- **Key metric:** FPY (First Pass Yield)
- **Key for UMKM:** Include sample approval process before bulk production

## 12. Action Steps Outline

### Step 1: Audit Vendor Existing
- **Details:** List all vendors, service type, spend per month, current issues
- **Example:** Rina lists 5 suppliers: ayam (Rp 8jt/bln, sering telat), kemasan (Rp 3jt/bln, ok), bumbu (Rp 2jt/bln, ok), box (Rp 1,5jt/bln, kualitas jelek), es batu (Rp 500rb/bln, ok)
- **Warning:** Don't skip small vendors — they can cause big problems too

### Step 2: Prioritize Which Vendors Need SLA First
- **Details:** Start with critical vendors (high spend, high risk)
- **Example:** Ayam supplier = highest risk (business can't run without it) → SLA first
- **Warning:** Don't impose SLA on all vendors at once — start with 1-2

### Step 3: Prepare the SLA Template
- **Details:** Use the 1-page template provided in the article, fill in blanks
- **Example:** Rina fills in: response time 1 hour, delivery by 08:00, penalty Rp 50k/hour late
- **Warning:** Keep numbers realistic — unrealistic SLA = no SLA (vendor will ignore it)

### Step 4: Introduce SLA to Vendor
- **Details:** Frame it positively — "biar usaha kita sama-sama maju, ini aturan main yang jelas buat kita berdua"
- **Example script:** "Pak, kita udah kerja sama 6 bulan. Saya pengen hubungan kita makin baik. Saya bikin semacam perjanjian kerja kecil-kecilan, biar kita sama-sama tahu hak dan kewajiban. Nggak usah takut, ini buat jaga kita berdua."
- **Warning:** DON'T say "kalau lu telat gua denda" — frame as mutual benefit

### Step 5: Implement and Track
- **Details:** Use Google Sheets to track performance
- **Example:** Log every delivery: was it on time? Was quality OK? Calculate penalty if applicable
- **Warning:** Enforce consistently — if you waive penalties once, the SLA becomes meaningless

### Step 6: Review and Renew
- **Details:** Monthly review of SLA compliance, quarterly full review
- **Example:** After 3 months, if vendor scores ≥90%, increase order volume as reward
- **Warning:** Remember to reward good vendors — SLA is not just about punishment
