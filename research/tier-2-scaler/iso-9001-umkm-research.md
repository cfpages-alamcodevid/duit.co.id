## 4. Market Research (Implementation Details)

### Roadmap to Certification (6 Steps)
1. **Gap Analysis:** Comparing current operations with ISO 9001:2015 requirements.
2. **Training & Awareness:** Educating the team that "Quality is everyone's job."
3. **Documentation:** Creating the "Quality Manual," SOPs, and Work Instructions. 
   - *Key for UMKM:* Keep it lean. Don't write 50-page SOPs for a 5-person team.
4. **Implementation:** Running the business using the new SOPs for at least 3 months to generate "records."
5. **Internal Audit & Management Review:** Checking your own work and fixing mistakes before the real audit.
6. **External Audit (Stage 1 & 2):** Official audit by a Certification Body (Lembaga Sertifikasi).

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
- **Standard:** ISO 9001:2015 (Standard Sistem Manajemen Mutu).
- **Accreditation:** Ensure the certification body is accredited by **KAN (Komite Akreditasi Nasional)**. This is mandatory for BUMN tenders and government projects in Indonesia.
- **Platform:** **PaDi UMKM** (Pasar Digital UMKM). ISO 9001 certificates should be uploaded here to increase "Vendor Grade."
- **NIB RBA:** Must have a Risk-Based NIB as a prerequisite.
- **TKDN:** Often paired with ISO 9001 in tenders. Having both is a "Power Combo" for winning government contracts.

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
- **Myth 1: ISO is only for big factories.** 
  - **Reality:** ISO 9001 is "scale-independent." A 2-person coffee roastery can (and should) be ISO certified.
- **Myth 2: It will create a mountain of paperwork.**
  - **Reality:** Modern ISO (2015 version) allows for digital documentation (videos, Trello boards, or simple cloud folders).
- **Myth 3: You have to be perfect to get certified.**
  - **Reality:** ISO is about "Continuous Improvement." The audit checks if you have a system to find and fix errors, not that you never make them.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
1. **The "Free" Check:** Check BSN (SNI Bina UMK) or Kemenkop UKM for active facilitation programs.
2. **Commitment:** Owner must appoint a "Quality Representative" (can be a senior staff or the owner themselves).
3. **Draft the "Big Three":** Quality Policy, Quality Manual, and the 6 Mandatory Procedures.
4. **The 3-Month Run:** Start using forms (e.g., Logbook Masuk Barang, Form Keluhan Pelanggan).
5. **Pre-Audit:** Do a "mock audit" with a friend or a freelancer to find glaring gaps.
6. **The Official Audit:** Choose a KAN-accredited body. Don't be afraid of "Findings" (Non-Conformities); they are just a to-do list for improvement.
