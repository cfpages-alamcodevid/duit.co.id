### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
Requirement: Mandatory preparation of cash flow statement per PSAK 2
Regulation: PSAK 2 (revised 2022), effective 2024
Process: Classify cash flows into operating, investing, financing; use direct or indirect method
Cost: None (compliance cost only for professional preparation if needed)
Timeline: Annual reporting requirement
Authority: Ikatan Akuntan Indonesia (IAI), OJK for listed entities
Link: https://iaiglobal.or.id/psak-2-laporan-arus-kas/

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Cash flow statement is only for large corporations
Reality: Required for all businesses under PSAK 2, crucial for SMEs scaling
Why it matters: Poor cash flow kills 82% of businesses regardless of size
Source: U.S. Bank study, IAI guidelines

Myth: Profit equals cash - businesses with profits don't need cash flow tracking
Reality: Profit is accrual-based; cash flow shows actual liquidity
Why it matters: Many profitable businesses fail due to cash shortages
Source: Harvard Business Review studies

### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Gather financial data (income statement, balance sheet)
- Details needed: Last 12 months transactions
- Example: Compile from bank statements and accounting software
- Warning: Ensure data accuracy to avoid misleading projections

Step 2: Categorize cash flows (operating, investing, financing)
- Details needed: Classify each transaction properly
- Example: Sales = operating inflow, equipment purchase = investing outflow
- Warning: Misclassification leads to incorrect analysis

Step 3: Calculate using direct or indirect method
- Details needed: Choose method based on business complexity
- Example: Indirect method starts with net profit, adjusts for non-cash items
- Warning: Use consistent method year-over-year
