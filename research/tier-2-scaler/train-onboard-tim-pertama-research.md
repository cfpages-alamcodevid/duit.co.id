### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
Requirement: Employment contracts must be in writing for PKWT, probation allowed for PKWTT (max 3 months).
Regulation: UU No. 13/2003 and UU No. 6/2023 (Cipta Kerja), PP 35/2021
Process: Draft in Bahasa Indonesia, include required elements (name, position, salary, duration).
Cost: Free for basic drafting, Rp 500.000-1.000.000 for legal review.
Timeline: 1-3 days for preparation.
Authority: Kemenaker RI
Link: https://jdih.kemnaker.go.id/

Requirement: BPJS Kesehatan and Ketenagakerjaan registration mandatory within 7 days of start.
Regulation: UU No. 24/2011, PP 35/2021
Process: Register online via bpjs-ketenagakerjaan.go.id, employee pays half premium.
Cost: Rp 160.000-320.000/month per employee (employer contribution).
Timeline: 7 working days for card issuance.
Authority: BPJS
Link: https://bpjsketenagakerjaan.go.id/

Requirement: Minimum wage compliance, payslips in Bahasa Indonesia.
Regulation: PP 51/2023, PP 36/2021
Process: Monthly payroll with detailed breakdown.
Cost: Variable, minimum Rp 3.29 million average.
Timeline: Monthly disbursement.
Authority: Kemenaker, BPS for UMK data.
Link: https://kemnaker.go.id/

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Onboarding is just paperwork and orientation day.
Reality: It's a 90-day structured process including training, relationship building, and milestones.
Why it matters: Poor onboarding leads to 20-30% early turnover.
Source: SHRM and Glassdoor data.

Myth: Small businesses can't afford formal onboarding.
Reality: Basic buddy system and checklists cost minimal but yield high ROI.
Why it matters: Informal onboarding costs more in lost productivity.
Source: FirstHR small business guide.

Myth: Probation is for testing, not onboarding.
Reality: Probation is part of onboarding with clear expectations.
Why it matters: Misuse leads to legal issues under UU Cipta Kerja.
Source: LegalMP article.
