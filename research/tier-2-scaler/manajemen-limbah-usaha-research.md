## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

Requirement: Environmental permits (AMDAL/UKL-UPL/SPPL)  
Regulation: PP 22/2021 about Environmental Protection and Management  
Process: Submit application to local environmental agency with business documentation  
Cost: Rp 500 thousand - Rp 5 million depending on business scale  
Timeline: 30-90 days  
Authority: Local KLHK office or provincial environmental agency  
Link: https://jdih.setkab.go.id/PUUdoc/176367/PP_Nomor_22_Tahun_2021.pdf  

Requirement: Hazardous waste (B3) management permit  
Regulation: Permen LHK 6/2021 about B3 waste management  
Process: Register with KLHK, obtain technical permit, implement manifest system  
Cost: Rp 1-3 million for permits and initial setup  
Timeline: 60-120 days  
Authority: KLHK  
Link: https://jdih.maritim.go.id/cfind/source/files/permen-lhk/2021pmlhk006_menlhk_06082021104752.pdf  

Requirement: Wastewater treatment system (IPAL)  
Regulation: Permen LHK 5/2014 about wastewater quality standards  
Process: Design IPAL, obtain operational permit, regular monitoring  
Cost: Rp 5-50 million depending on capacity  
Timeline: 90-180 days for setup  
Authority: Local environmental agency  
Link: Official KLHK website  

Requirement: Waste manifest and reporting  
Regulation: PP 22/2021  
Process: Digital reporting through SIMBA KLHK system  
Cost: Minimal (online system)  
Timeline: Monthly/quarterly reporting  
Authority: KLHK  
Link: https://simba.menlhk.go.id/portal/statistik  

Requirement: PROPER participation  
Regulation: KLHK program  
Process: Register business, undergo evaluation  
Cost: Free registration, potential improvement costs  
Timeline: Annual  
Authority: KLHK  
Link: KLHK website

## 9. Action Steps Outline

Step 1: Assess current waste generation  
- Details: Inventory all waste types, quantities, characteristics  
- Example: Manufacturing business identifies 5m³ liquid waste, 2 tons solid waste daily  
- Warning: Underestimating volumes leads to inadequate systems  

Step 2: Review regulatory requirements  
- Details: Determine needed permits based on business type and location  
- Example: Small manufacturer needs UKL-UPL and B3 permit  
- Warning: Missing permits cause immediate operational stops  

Step 3: Design waste management system  
- Details: Plan segregation, storage, treatment, disposal  
- Example: Implement 3-bin system for different waste types  
- Warning: Poor design increases costs and compliance risks  

Step 4: Obtain necessary permits and certifications  
- Details: Apply for environmental permits, train staff  
- Example: Register for PROPER, get operator certifications  
- Warning: Delays in permitting halt business operations  

Step 5: Implement and monitor system  
- Details: Install equipment, establish monitoring procedures  
- Example: Monthly waste audits, quarterly reporting  
- Warning: Lack of monitoring leads to undetected violations  

Step 6: Regular reporting and audits  
- Details: Maintain digital manifest, prepare for inspections  
- Example: Use SIMBA KLHK for online reporting  
- Warning: Incomplete records result in penalties
