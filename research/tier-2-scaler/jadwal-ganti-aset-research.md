**Regulation:** UU No. 36 Tahun 2008 tentang Pajak Penghasilan  
**Process:** Calculate depreciation using straight-line or declining balance method

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

**Myth:** Assets should be replaced only when they break  
**Reality:** Proactive replacement prevents costly downtime and maintains productivity  
**Why it matters:** Emergency repairs cost 3x more than planned maintenance  
**Source:** Manufacturing industry surveys 2025  

**Myth:** Depreciation schedules are just for tax purposes  
**Reality:** They provide data for replacement timing and budgeting  
**Why it matters:** Helps avoid cash flow problems  
**Source:** Financial planning studies  

## 9. Action Steps Outline

**Step 1:** Inventory all business assets  
- Details: List all fixed assets with purchase date, value, condition  
- Example: Use spreadsheet with columns for asset type, age, maintenance history  
- Warning: Don't forget leased assets  

**Step 2:** Assess asset lifespan and condition  
- Details: Check manufacturer recommendations, usage intensity  
- Example: Vehicles: 5-7 years, Equipment: 8-12 years  
- Warning: Overuse shortens lifespan  

**Step 3:** Calculate replacement costs and timeline  
- Details: Research current market prices, plan 3-5 year schedule  
- Example: Budget 20-30% of asset value annually  
- Warning: Factor in inflation (10-15% annually)  

**Step 4:** Set up maintenance and monitoring schedule  
- Details: Regular inspections, preventive maintenance  
- Example: Monthly checks for critical assets  
- Warning: Skipping maintenance increases replacement frequency  

**Step 5:** Create emergency fund and financing plan  
- Details: Allocate 10-20% of profits for asset replacement  
- Example: Use business savings or bank loans  
- Warning: Don't rely solely on profits
