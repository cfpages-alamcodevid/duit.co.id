### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess Current State
- Inventory all family assets and liabilities
- Evaluate concentration risks and liquidity positions
- Document family goals and risk tolerance
- Details needed: Current asset list, income statements, family tree
- Example: Create comprehensive balance sheet including business interests
- Warning: Don't underestimate hidden liabilities or informal agreements

Step 2: Define IPS Framework
- Establish governance structure (investment committee, family council)
- Set risk parameters and asset allocation targets
- Define spending and distribution policies
- Details needed: Risk tolerance questionnaires, benchmark selections
- Example: Three-bucket allocation with specific percentage ranges
- Warning: Avoid overly complex structures that family can't maintain

Step 3: Implement Governance
- Form investment committee with clear authority matrix
- Select professional advisors (CIO, lawyers, accountants)
- Establish reporting and monitoring processes
- Details needed: Committee charter, meeting schedules, decision protocols
- Example: Quarterly investment reviews with predefined agenda
- Warning: Ensure family members are educated before giving governance roles

Step 4: Integrate Legal & Tax Planning
- Structure entities for tax efficiency and asset protection
- Align IPS with estate planning and succession
- Comply with OJK and AML requirements
- Details needed: Entity selection, tax treaty utilization
- Example: Use Indonesian foundations for philanthropic integration
- Warning: Consult multiple advisors to avoid conflicts of interest

Step 5: Monitor & Review Annually
- Track performance against benchmarks
- Stress test portfolio under various scenarios
- Update IPS for life changes or market conditions
- Details needed: Performance reports, scenario analysis tools
- Example: Annual retreat for IPS review and family education
- Warning: Don't wait for crises to trigger reviews
