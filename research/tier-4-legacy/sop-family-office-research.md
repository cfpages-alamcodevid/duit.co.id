### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Family office operations should be informal to maintain family relationships
Reality: Standardized SOPs for reporting and meetings actually strengthen trust by making expectations clear and ensuring consistent communication
Why it matters: Informal operations lead to misunderstandings and conflicts when major decisions need quick resolution
Source: EY Family Governance Survey 2025

Myth: Dashboards are only for large institutional offices, not suitable for Indonesian family offices
Reality: Modern cloud-based dashboards (like Tableau or PowerBI) are accessible from Rp 10 million/month and can be customized for family-specific metrics including relationship health indicators
Why it matters: Without data visualization, complex family wealth becomes overwhelming and error-prone
Source: Deloitte WealthTech Report 2026

Myth: Weekly meetings are too frequent and disrupt productive work
Reality: 60% of family offices hold meetings bi-weekly or monthly, but use digital tools for asynchronous updates, reducing in-person meeting frequency while maintaining oversight
Why it matters: Less frequent meetings can lead to drift in strategy implementation and missed opportunities
Source: UBS Global Family Office Review 2025

### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess current operational maturity
- Details needed: Map existing communication channels, reporting frequency, decision-making processes
- Example: Create inventory of how decisions were made in past 6 months
- Warning: Don't skip this step; inaccurate assessment leads to wrong SOP design

Step 2: Design reporting framework
- Details needed: Define KPIs, data sources, frequency (daily/weekly/monthly), responsibility matrix
- Example: Financial metrics from accounting system, relationship metrics from surveys
- Warning: Start with 5-7 key metrics, not 50; data overload reduces effectiveness

Step 3: Set meeting cadences and agendas
- Details needed: Strategic (quarterly), operational (monthly), tactical (weekly) meeting formats
- Example: Quarterly family council for long-term planning, monthly for investment reviews
- Warning: Include relationship-building elements alongside data review

Step 4: Implement technology infrastructure
- Details needed: Select dashboard platform, train users, establish data security
- Example: PowerBI for visualization, secure cloud storage for documents
- Warning: Invest in training; technology without user adoption fails

Step 5: Pilot and iterate SOP
- Details needed: Run 3-month pilot, collect feedback, refine processes
- Example: Start with one business unit, expand gradually
- Warning: Measure not just implementation but behavioral changes in family dynamics
