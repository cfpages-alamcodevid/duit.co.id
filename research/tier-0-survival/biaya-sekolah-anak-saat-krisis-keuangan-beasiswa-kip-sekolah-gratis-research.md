## 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess family eligibility
- Details: Check income, DTKS/DTSEN status, school level, PHK/disaster status, and whether the child is in Dapodik
- Example: Use DTKS/Kemensos checker and confirm NISN with school
- Warning: Don't falsify documents; fake SKTM can block future aid

Step 2: Gather documents (KIP, SKTM, KK, KTP orang tua, NISN)
- Details: Prepare documents for school submission, PKH verification, and private scholarship forms
- Example: Request SKTM from kelurahan and scan all files before registration period
- Warning: Expired or inconsistent documents are commonly rejected

Step 3: Register through school Dapodik
- Details: Approach teacher for nomination
- Example: Bring documents to sekolah
- Warning: Miss cut-off dates

Step 4: Monitor status via SIPINTAR
- Details: Check online portal
- Example: Login with NISN
- Warning: Patience needed, takes weeks

Step 5: Activate bank account for disbursement
- Details: Visit bank with school letter
- Example: BRI for SD/SMP
- Warning: Must activate before disbursement

Step 6: Layer PKH, KIP Kuliah, or private scholarship if eligible
- Details: After PIP is submitted, check PKH education component, KIP Kuliah for graduating SMA/SMK students, and foundation/corporate scholarships
- Example: Maya combines PIP with BSI Scholarship application; Rudi checks SMART/YBM BRILiaN or Titian-style foundation options
- Warning: Track deadlines separately because private scholarships often close earlier than school aid

Step 7: Check free-school/local-government options
- Details: For DKI Jakarta and other regions, check official PPDB/local education office programs before paying enrollment fees
- Example: Sari compares public school, subsidized private school, and BOS-supported cost reductions
- Warning: Local programs change every academic year; verify from official portals only
