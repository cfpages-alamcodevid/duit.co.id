### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Small amounts don't matter in savings
Reality: Rp 1,000 daily = Rp 365,000 annually, compounds over time
Why it matters: Psychological barrier prevents starting
Source: BI survey data

Myth: Inflation makes micro-saving worthless
Reality: Consistent saving builds habit; inflation affects all savings equally
Why it matters: Discourages low-income savers
Source: BPS inflation data

Myth: Only rich people can save regularly
Reality: 70% of Indonesians don't save; micro-saving democratizes access
Why it matters: Creates victim mentality
Source: GoodStats survey

### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess current spending habits
- Track 7 days of all expenses
- Identify change sources (daily purchases)
- Calculate realistic daily savings amount

Step 2: Choose saving method
- Digital: Apps like Jago, FINETIKS
- Physical: Piggy bank or jar
- Hybrid: Change to digital wallet

Step 3: Set small goals
- Daily: Rp 1,000-5,000
- Weekly: Rp 10,000-30,000
- Monthly: Accumulate and review

Step 4: Build consistency
- Use reminders/alarm
- Involve family/friends
- Track progress visually

Step 5: Scale up gradually
- Increase amount as habit forms
- Move to interest-bearing accounts
- Plan for larger goals
