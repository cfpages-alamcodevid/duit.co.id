### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: Financial literacy is only for people with money
Reality: Essential for avoiding debt traps, even with zero income
Why it matters: Prevents borrowing from illegal sources
Source: OJK consumer protection data

Myth: Unemployed people can't build savings
Reality: Micro-saving possible through daily habit changes
Why it matters: Builds discipline for future employment
Source: Emergency fund research from Katadata

Myth: Housewives don't need financial knowledge since husbands manage money
Reality: 39.56% of households have wives as decision-makers
Why it matters: Empowers family financial stability
Source: Danareksa Research Institute

### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Assess current financial situation
- Details: Track income (if any), list all expenses, identify debts
- Example: Use free budgeting apps or simple notebook
- Warning: Be honest about spending habits

Step 2: Learn basic financial concepts
- Details: Understand budgeting, saving, debt management
- Example: Follow OJK free online courses
- Warning: Avoid get-rich-quick schemes

Step 3: Create emergency fund
- Details: Start with Rp 10,000/day habit
- Example: Automatic transfers to separate account
- Warning: Don't touch for non-emergencies

Step 4: Protect against financial scams
- Details: Verify all financial offers, report suspicious activities
- Example: Check OJK registered institutions
- Warning: Never share OTP or KTP to strangers

Step 5: Plan for future income
- Details: Learn basic skills for side income
- Example: Online courses for digital skills
- Warning: Invest time, not money initially
