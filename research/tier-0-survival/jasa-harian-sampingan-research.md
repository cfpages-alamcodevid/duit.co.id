### Summary
This research reveals Indonesia's gig economy employs 2.3 million primary workers with $7B GDP contribution, offering viable side income through daily services like delivery, cleaning, and tutoring. Average earnings range from IDR 2.5-3M/month with low entry barriers (IDR 0-10M capital). Success stories show drivers earning IDR 10-20M/month through dedication. Legal requirements are minimal but income reporting needed above PTKP threshold.

### Key Insights
1. Gig economy growing rapidly, providing accessible side income for survival tier
2. Low capital requirements make it ideal for beginners with limited funds
3. Real earnings often below minimum wage but can scale with effort
4. Legal protections weak; BPJS coverage only 1.6% among gig workers
5. Platform choice critical: Gojek/Grab for mobility, Tokopedia for marketplace services
