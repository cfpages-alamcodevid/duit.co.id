## 3. Key Data & Statistics
- **Regulation:** POJK No. 22 Tahun 2023 is the "New Bible" for consumer protection in finance.
- **Fact:** Debt collectors are MANDATED to be certified by SPPI (Sertifikasi Profesi Penagihan Indonesia).
- **Source:** OJK & SEOJK No. 19/SEOJK.06/2023.

## 4. Market Research (Modus Operandi)
- **Modus 1: "Mata Elang" (Eagle Eyes).** People waiting on street corners to "check" license plates and forcefully take motorcycles. These are usually ILEGAL if they don't have Fidusia docs.
- **Modus 2: Fake DC.** Scammers who buy data leaks and visit people, claiming they have debt and asking for "settlement" money in cash.
- **Modus 3: Intimidation.** Using muscle to scare people into paying without showing a single document.

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
- **Mandatory Documents (The "Big 4"):**
    1.  **ID Card** (from the Finance/Bank company).
    2.  **SPPI Certificate** (Proof of professional certification).
    3.  **Surat Tugas** (Assignment letter for the specific debtor name).
    4.  **Sertifikat Jaminan Fidusia** (Crucial for any vehicle/asset seizure).
- **Rules of Conduct:** Monday-Saturday, 08.00-20.00 only. No menaces, no SARA, no third-party harassment.

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
- **Expert Quote:** "A real debt collector will never ask for cash payments in the field. All payments must go through official virtual accounts or the company's office."
- **Case Study:** A woman in Bekasi successfully turned away a DC because they didn't have the original Fidusia certificate. They tried to use a photocopy, which she correctly identified as invalid for seizure.

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**Scenario: DC visits your home**
1. **Debtor:** "Can I see your ID and Surat Tugas?"
2. **DC:** (Shows ID only).
3. **Debtor:** "I need to see your SPPI certificate and the assignment letter with MY name on it. If you are here to take the car, I also need to see the original Fidusia certificate."
4. **Action:** If they refuse or get aggressive, lock the gate and call **110** (Polri).

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
- **Myth:** "DC can take my car if I'm 1 day late."
- **Reality:** There is a process (SP1, SP2, SP3) and they need the Fidusia certificate + court order (in some cases) to take assets forcefully if the debtor resists.
- **Myth:** "I have to follow them to the office."
- **Reality:** You are under no obligation to follow a stranger anywhere. All negotiations can happen at your home with witnesses.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
- **Step 1: Stay Calm & Record.** Start your phone camera. This usually makes fake/illegal DCs back off.
- **Step 2: Ask for the Big 4 Documents.** Check the names and validity dates.
- **Step 3: Verification.** Call the bank/finance company's official customer service to verify the DC's name and ID.
- **Step 4: NO CASH in the field.** Never pay the DC directly.
- **Step 5: Call 110.** If they use violence or block your way, it is a crime (Pasal 368 KUHP).
