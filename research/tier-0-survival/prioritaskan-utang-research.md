## 4. Market Research
*   **Debt Types in Indonesia:**
    1.  **Pinjol (Legal):** 0.3% interest/day.
    2.  **Pinjol (Ilegal):** Unlimited interest, often 1-2% per day.
    3.  **Kartu Kredit:** ~1.75% per month.
    4.  **Paylater:** ~2-4% per month.
    5.  **Utang Teman/Keluarga:** Usually 0% but high social cost.
*   **Price Comparison:** Debt Snowball costs more in total interest but provides better psychological "wins."

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **Method 1: Debt Snowball (Dave Ramsey style):** Focus on small wins. Best for those who lose motivation easily.
*   **Method 2: Debt Avalanche:** Focus on math. Best for high-interest traps like Pinjol.
*   **Case Study:** "Budi had 5 debts: 2 Pinjol (Rp 2M each), 1 Paylater (Rp 5M), and 1 Bank Loan (Rp 50M). By using Avalanche, he saved Rp 15M in interest by killing Pinjol first."

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
### Scenario: Multi-debt Survival
- Debt A: Rp 500k (Friend) - 0% interest
- Debt B: Rp 2M (Pinjol) - 9% interest/month
- Debt C: Rp 10M (Bank) - 1% interest/month

**Snowball approach:** Pay A -> B -> C (Quickest win: A).
**Avalanche approach:** Pay B -> C -> A (Smartest win: B).
**Survival recommendation:** Always kill high-interest "predatory" debt (Pinjol) first, even if it's larger than a friendly debt.

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Bayar utang ke teman dulu karena nggak enak hati."
*   **Reality:** While ethically good, if your Pinjol is growing 9% a month, you'll eventually go bankrupt and won't be able to pay your friend at all. Pay the "fire" (Pinjol) first.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Inventory:** List all debts (Balance, Interest, Due date).
2.  **Choose Strategy:** Snowball vs Avalanche (or Hybrid).
3.  **Stop New Debt:** Do not "gali lubang tutup lubang."
4.  **Execution:** Pay minimums on all, blast the priority one.
