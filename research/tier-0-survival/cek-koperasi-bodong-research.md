## 3. Key Data & Statistics
- **Statistic:** In 2025, the government launched the "Koperasi Desa Merah Putih" program to sanitize the cooperative sector.
- **Source:** KemenkopUKM / Antara News (2025).
- **Red Flag Data:** 90% of fraudulent cooperatives offer services to non-members (which is illegal for KSP).
- **Regulatory Context:** Starting 2025, cooperatives with "open loop" operations (serving non-members) must be supervised by OJK.

## 4. Market Research (Modus Operandi)
- **Modus 1:** "Simpanan Berjangka" with returns 2-5% per month (Bank rate is ~5-6% per YEAR).
- **Modus 2:** Using the name "Koperasi" but operating like a Pinjol (lending to anyone via app).
- **Modus 3:** Fake certificates of NIK (Nomor Induk Koperasi) or mencatut logo Garuda/Kemenkop.

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
- **Requirement:** Must have a Badan Hukum and an active NIK.
- **Regulation:** UU No. 25 Tahun 1992 tentang Perkoperasian & UU PPSK (2023).
- **Process:** Check via Online Data System (ODS).
- **Authority:** Kementerian Koperasi dan UKM.
- **Portal:** [nik.depkop.go.id](https://nik.depkop.go.id)

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
- **Case Study:** KSP Sejahtera Bersama or Indosurya (Historical reference of massive failure).
- **Common Challenge:** People join because "everyone in the village joined" (Social Proof Trap).

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**Persona: Ibu Sumi (45), Pedagang Pasar**
- Offered a "Tabungan Lebaran" by a local cooperative.
- Promised 10% bonus at the end of the year.
- **The Check:** Sumi asks for the NIK number. The collector says "we are registered at the district office." This is a RED FLAG. All cooperatives must be in the ODS Kemenkop system.

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
- **Myth:** If they have an office and a permit from the local sub-district (Camat/Lurah), they are safe.
- **Reality:** Local permits are NOT financial permits. They must have a Badan Hukum Koperasi and NIK.
- **Myth:** Cooperatives are safer than banks because they are "kekeluargaan."
- **Reality:** Fraudsters abuse the "kekeluargaan" term to avoid strict audits.

## 9. Action Steps Outline
- **Step 1: Ask for the NIK (Nomor Induk Koperasi).** If they can't provide it, walk away.
- **Step 2: Visit nik.depkop.go.id.** Enter the name or NIK.
- **Step 3: Check "Status Aktif."** Ensure the status is "Aktif" and they have a recent "Sertifikat NIK."
- **Step 4: Verify the Business Model.** If they offer loans to people who are NOT members, it's illegal.
- **Step 5: Report.** Use Lapor.go.id or OJK 157 if suspicious.
