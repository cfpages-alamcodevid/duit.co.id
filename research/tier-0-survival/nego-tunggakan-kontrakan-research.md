## 3. Key Data & Statistics
*   **Legal Reference:** KUH Perdata Pasal 1548 - 1600 (Sewa Menyewa).
*   **Statistic:** Rent is typically 20-30% of household income. For those in Tier 0, it often exceeds 50%.
*   **Data:** Landlords prefer a paying-but-late tenant over an empty house or a legal battle. Eviction costs time and money for the landlord.

---

## 4. Market Research: Negotiation Options
*   **Installment Plan:** Paying 50% now, 50% later.
*   **Sweat Equity (Tukar Jasa):** Helping with house maintenance, painting, or administrative tasks for the landlord's other properties.
*   **Temporary Discount:** Requesting a 10-20% discount for 2-3 months until stabilized.
*   **Grace Period:** Requesting an extra 14 days without penalty.

---

## 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Prohibition of Forced Eviction:** Landlords cannot simply throw out belongings or lock the door without a court order (though this is often ignored in practice).
*   **Requirement:** Any agreement MUST be in writing (Surat Perjanjian Pembayaran Tunggakan).
*   **Stamp Duty:** Use Materai Rp 10.000 for the agreement to have legal weight.

---

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **The "Silent Runner" Mistake:** The worst thing a tenant can do is ghost the landlord. This triggers anger and immediate eviction.
*   **Case Study:** A tenant lost his job. He approached the landlord, offered to paint the entire fence and fix the leaky roof in exchange for 1 month's rent. The landlord agreed because he was planning to hire a handyman anyway.
*   **Key Learning:** Identify what the landlord *needs* (maintenance, security, administrative help) and offer that.

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**Persona: "Sari, 32 tahun, Ibu Rumah Tangga (Suami baru PHK)"**
*   **Situation:** Tunggakan kontrakan Rp 1,5jt/bulan.
*   **Negotiation Script:**
    "Pak/Bu, mohon maaf sebelumnya. Suami saya baru saja terkena pengurangan di kantor. Kami berniat baik untuk tetap melunasi. Bisakah kami bayar Rp 750rb dulu minggu ini, dan sisanya kami cicil Rp 250rb setiap minggu?"
*   **Outcome:** Landlord appreciates the honesty and the specific numbers.

---

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Kalau nggak bayar, pemilik berhak sita barang saya."
*   **Reality:** Illegal. Seizure of property without a court order is a criminal offense.
*   **Myth:** "Kalau nggak ada kontrak tertulis, saya bisa diusir kapan saja."
*   **Reality:** Verbal agreements still have legal standing in Indonesia, but are harder to prove.

---

## 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Stop Avoiding:** Pick up the phone or meet in person.
2.  **Calculate Your "Max Offer":** Know exactly how much you *can* pay today.
3.  **Draft the Proposal:** Bring a written plan of when and how you will pay the arrears.
4.  **Offer "Sweat Equity":** Mention skills like repairs, cleaning, or marketing if they have other vacant rooms.
5.  **Formalize:** Write the agreement on paper with Materai.
6.  **Exit Gracefully:** If you truly can't pay, negotiate a "voluntary move-out date" to avoid conflict.
