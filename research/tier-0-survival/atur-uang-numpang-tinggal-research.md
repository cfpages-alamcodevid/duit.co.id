## 3. Key Data & Statistics

*   **Source:** Market research (Mamikos, local ads 2025).
*   **Relevance:** Highlighting the "opportunity gain" of staying with relatives.

## 4. Market Research: Typical Contribution Costs
When staying at someone's house, costs increase. Here is the estimated monthly "hidden" cost per person:
*   **Electricity (AC/Electronic):** Rp 100.000 - Rp 250.000
*   **Water (PDAM/Pump):** Rp 20.000 - Rp 50.000
*   **Rice & Gas:** Rp 150.000 - Rp 200.000
*   **Internet/WiFi (Usage):** Rp 50.000
**Total Baseline Contribution:** Rp 320.000 - Rp 550.000

**Where to contribute:**
*   Directly buy token electricity (PLN) via app.
*   Subscribe to monthly groceries (Sembako) via Shopee/Tokopedia/Alfamart.
*   Pay the WiFi bill directly if possible.

---

## 5. Legal & Social Requirements
*   **Requirement:** Mandatory report to RT/RW if staying > 24 hours (Indonesian law).
*   **Regulation:** Peraturan RT/RW setempat.
*   **Process:** Submit KTP copy to Ketua RT.
*   **Cost:** Usually free or small "Iuran Keamanan/Kebersihan".

---

## 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **Common Challenge:** The "Guest Trap" - feeling like a guest for 6 months and never helping with chores, leading to resentment.
*   **Case Study (Forum Insight):** A resident stayed 1 year at his aunt's. He didn't pay rent but he was the "on-call" repairman and grocery runner. Result: Happy household.
*   **Key Learning:** Contribution isn't always cash; it's about "Value Exchange".

## 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**Persona: "Rian, 23 tahun, Fresh Graduate di Jakarta"**
*   **Income:** Rp 5.200.000 (UMR)
*   **Scenario:** Numpang di rumah Paman.
*   **Budget Plan:**
    *   Savings (Goal: Move out in 6 months): Rp 2.500.000
    *   Contribution to Paman (Groceries/Token): Rp 700.000
    *   Transport & Personal Food: Rp 1.500.000
    *   Emergency Fund: Rp 500.000

**Calculation Example:**
If you save Rp 2.5M (the cost of a decent studio/apartment) for 6 months, you have Rp 15M for a DP or deposit for your own place.

## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Saudara sendiri, pasti nggak mau dibayar."
*   **Reality:** They might not *ask*, but they will *feel* the burden. Financial stress ruins family ties.
*   **Why it matters:** Relationships are more expensive than rent.

---

## 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Transparent Discussion:** Talk about duration and contribution expectations upfront.
2.  **Audit the Bills:** Find out which bill is the most burdensome (usually electricity or food).
3.  **Set Up "Silent Payments":** Use apps to pay tokens or buy groceries before they run out.
4.  **The "Plus One" Rule:** Always leave the house cleaner than you found it.
5.  **Exit Strategy:** Set a clear date or financial milestone for when you will move out.
