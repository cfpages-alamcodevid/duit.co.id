### 4.2 Revenue Potential

#### Revenue Stream 1: Accommodation
- **Daily rate per unit (cabin/glamping):** Rp 400,000–1,200,000/night
- **Occupancy rate assumption:** 40-60% (starting low, scaling up)
- **Monthly projection for 8 units:**
  - Conservative (30% occupancy): 8 units × 9 nights × Rp 500K avg = Rp 36,000,000/month
  - Moderate (45% occupancy): 8 units × 13.5 nights × Rp 600K avg = Rp 64,800,000/month
  - Optimistic (60% occupancy): 8 units × 18 nights × Rp 700K avg = Rp 100,800,000/month

#### Revenue Stream 2: Farm Produce Sales
- **Fruit tree yield per hectare (mature orchard):**
  - Durian: 50-100 trees/Ha, 30-50 fruits/tree/year @ Rp 50-100K/fruit = Rp 75-500M/year/Ha

## 5. Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Eptilu Garut (Jawa Barat)
- **Founded:** 2013-2014
- **Founder:** Rizal Fahreza (alumnus IPB, studied agriculture in USA)
- **Location:** Cikajang, Garut, Jawa Barat (75+ Ha total)
- **Business Model:** Closed-loop agribusiness = production + processing + distribution + agrotourism
- **Key Elements:**
  - 75+ Ha hortikultura (jeruk, lemon, cabai, tomat, buncis)
  - Koperasi Produsen with 27 anggota tetap + 125 petani binaan (mostly under 30 years old)
  - Agrotourism: petik jeruk langsung, resto & cafe (farm-to-table)
  - Diversifikasi produk olahan: es krim buah, keripik kentang, cabai bubuk, bola susu
  - IoT greenhouse (4,000 plants, digital irrigation via smartphone)
  - Bank Indonesia partner since 2022
  - B2B supply to Jakarta & Banten, plus hotel/restaurant/cafe
- **Revenue Note:** Not public, but produces >1 ton/panen once a week from greenhouse cabai alone
- **Key Learning:** Integrated closed-loop model reduces waste, increases margin at every stage. Digital technology + cooperative structure makes farming efficient.

### Case Study 2: Desa Wisata Pentingsari, Sleman, Yogyakarta
- **Founded:** 2008
- **Location:** Lereng Gunung Merapi, Cangkringan, Sleman, DIY
- **Scale:** Started with 5 homestays, now 55 homestays
- **Visitors:** < 1,000/year (2008) → 25,000/year (2018-present)
- **Monthly Revenue:** Rp 150–200 juta/month (entire village)
- **Community Impact:** 55 homestays, 25 seni performers, 30 local guides, 60 culinary operators, 20 home industries
- **Awards:** ASEAN Tourism Award (2023, 2025), UNWTO Best Practice, Green Destination Top 100
- **Key Learning:** Community-based tourism with authentic experiences (rice farming, cooking class, traditional arts) generates higher per-visitor spend. Aligns perfectly with cultural immersion trend.

### Case Study 3: Sabila Farm, Sleman, Yogyakarta
- **Founded:** 2005 (pioneer of dragon fruit farming in Indonesia)
- **Founder:** Ir. M. Gunung Soetopo (IPB alumnus)
- **Location:** Kaliurang KM 18.5, Sleman (kaki Gunung Merapi)
- **Scale:** Multiple hectares with three business units: production, recreation, education
- **Products:** Dragon fruit (red & yellow), avocado, durian, soursop, jeruk RGL, srikaya jumbo, pepaya
- **Agrotourism:** Package-based edu-tourism + fruit picking + motivational entrepreneurship class
- **Partnerships:** 17 farmer partnerships across Indonesia
- **Customer Satisfaction:** >96% satisfaction rate
- **Visitors:** Domestic from all provinces + international from 30 countries across 5 continents
- **Key Learning:** Started as pure production farm, then added agrotourism later. This phased approach reduces risk. Land at the foot of Merapi has appreciated enormously since 2005.

### Case Study 4: Ghaisan Farm, Borobudur, Magelang
- **Founded:** 2022
- **Owner:** Puguh Tri Warsono (also runs Anugrah Borobudur Cottage & Homestay)
- **Location:** Dusun Ngaran, Desa Borobudur, Magelang (adjacent to Candi Borobudur)

- **Premium fruits:** Avocado (Nikol, Miki, Kendil), Durian (Musang King, Duri Hitam, Bawor), Jambu Kristal Red Diamond

- **Key Learning:** Location near a major tourist attraction (Borobudur) provides natural foot traffic. The "all-you-can-eat fruit picking" concept works as a powerful marketing hook.

## 6. Asset Analysis: Productive Farmland as a Hedge

### Land Appreciation Thesis

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| Agricultural land in peri-urban areas (1-2 hours from major city) appreciates 10-25% annually | BPS & property data 2024-2026 confirm consistent appreciation |
| Infrastructure projects (tol, airport, IKN) push land prices higher | Example: Sumedang land near Cisumdawu toll exit jumped to Rp 500K/m² |
| Productive farmland with SHM has higher valuation than empty land | Bank LTV for farmland + business: up to 60-70% of appraised value |
| Agrotourism designation can increase land value 30-50% above agricultural-only zoning | Operational business cashflow improves appraised value |
| Inflation hedge: land + hard assets (buildings, irrigation, trees) maintain real value | Indonesian inflation 2024-2026: ~3-4% annually; land appreciation exceeds this |

### Dual-Asset Characteristics for Bank Financing

**Without farm stay:**
- Land valued at agricultural price: Rp 100-500K/m²
- Max LTV from bank: 50-60%
- Loan amount: Rp 500M–1.5B for 1 Ha

**With farm stay + business:**
- Land + business appraised as going concern
- Value includes: land + buildings + productive trees + brand/cashflow
- Max LTV can be higher (60-70%) because business generates repayment cashflow
- Loan amount: Rp 1-3B+ for the same 1 Ha

**Source reference:** Glamping-as-investment analysis in article 3.109 (`glamping-investasi`)

## 8. Integration Strategies

### 8.1 Farm-to-Table Dining

- **Eptilu Garut model:** Resto & cafe menyajikan menu dari hasil panen sendiri — nasi liwet, olahan hortikultura
- **Margin advantage:** Typical restaurant food cost is 35-40%. With own farm produce, food cost drops to 15-25%
- **Menu differentiation:** Seasonal menu based on what's being harvested — creates urgency and uniqueness
- **Examples:** Fresh fruit juices, salads from garden greens, grilled vegetables, fruit-based desserts
- **Estimated investment for farm kitchen + dining area:** Rp 100-200 million

### 8.2 Organic Certification as Differentiator

- **Cost of SNI Organic certification (2025-2026):**
  - Small farm (< 5 Ha): Rp 20-45 million initial, Rp 12-25 million/year surveillance
  - Reference: indonesia-vegetables.com, gikaglobalfarma.co.id
- **Price premium:** 72% of Indonesian consumers willing to pay 30-50% more for organic-certified products (NielsenIQ 2025 survey)
- **Certification bodies in Indonesia:** INOFICE, LeSOS, BIOCert, SUCOFINDO, Mutu Agung Lestari
- **Processing time:** 3-6 months (plus 2-year land transition period for full organic)
- **Marketing value:** Can charge 30-50% premium on room rates as "Organic Farm Stay"

### 8.3 School Trip Programs

- **Target schools:** PAUD/TK, SD, SMP, SMA — all levels can be accommodated
- **Pricing structure (based on Kebun Wisata Pasirmukti 2026 tariffs):**
  - PAUD/TK-SD: Rp 90,000-160,000/student (half day)
  - SMP-SMA: Rp 130,000-200,000/student (full day with activities)
  - With accommodation: Rp 250,000-450,000/student (2D1N)
- **Required certifications for school programs:**
  - NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha)
  - Standard operating procedures for student safety
  - Insurance coverage (recommended)
  - Trained guides who can handle children
- **School trip season:** Peak April-June, August-October; requires advance booking system

## 12. Action Steps Outline

**Step 1: Site Selection & Land Due Diligence**
- Verify SHM certificate (not girik/letter C or tanah sengketa)
- Check accessibility: road condition, distance from toll/highway (max 45 min from major road)
- Verify water availability (year-round, not just rainy season)
- Check zoning: apakah masuk kawasan wisata/agrowisata?
- Use ATR/BPN's "Sentuh Tanahku" app for real-time land data
- Cross-check with NJOP online + market price on Rumah123/99.co

**Step 2: Business Concept Design**
- Define target market: families? school groups? corporate? couples?
- Choose farming focus: fruits? vegetables? livestock? mix?
- Choose accommodation type: cottages? glamping? mixed?
- Design revenue model: which streams to prioritize first?

**Step 3: Business Plan & Financial Modeling**
- Calculate total investment (land + construction + farm setup + buffer)
- Project revenue for each stream (conservative/moderate/optimistic)
- Calculate break-even occupancy and payback period
- Plan funding: cash, KUR, KPR komersial, or investor partnership

**Step 4: Legal & Permit Processing**
- NIB via OSS-RBA (free, 1-3 days)
- Accommodation/restaurant permits via Dinas Pariwisata & Dinkes
- Halal certification (if applicable)
- Organic certification (optional, can be phased in year 2)

**Step 5: Land Preparation & Construction**
- Land clearing and basic infrastructure (access road, water, electricity)
- Build accommodation units (start with 3-5, scale up based on demand)
- Plant fruit trees (choose varieties that fruit year-round or in sequence)
- Set up irrigation system

**Step 6: Launch & Marketing**
- Build website with booking system
- Create Instagram/TikTok presence (content: farm life, harvesting, guest experiences)
- Partner with travel agents and school trip organizers
- List on Airbnb/Traveloka/Agoda for accommodation
- Offer pre-launch discounts for first 100 guests to build reviews

## 15. Writer Notes & Key Insights

### Core Thesis for Article Writer
The article must frame farm stay not just as a "business opportunity" but as an **asset strategy**. The target audience (Tier 3: successful entrepreneurs/executives) thinks in terms of portfolio, hedging, and bankability — not just monthly cashflow.

### 3 Most Important Data Points to Lead With
1. **1.021 billion domestic trips** in 2024 (BPS) — massive and growing demand
2. **Land appreciation 10-25%/year** in peri-urban areas — the real wealth engine
3. **Combined ROI 25-40%/year** when you add property appreciation to operational cashflow

### Key Differentiators vs Competitor Articles
- **Not a "how to start small" article** (those are Tier 1). This is an asset allocation article.
- **Emphasize the hedge thesis:** productive farmland is one of the few assets that produces income AND appreciates AND can be used as bank collateral.
- **Use specific numbers** throughout — don't say "beberapa ratus juta," say "Rp 500-800 juta untuk 5 unit cottage."
- **Reference the glamping-as-investment article** (3.109) for synergy on the LTV/bankability angle.

### Voice & Tone
- Authoritative, data-driven, reserved
- Speak to experienced investors who already understand business
- Avoid explaining basic concepts (they know what ROI is)
- Use "Anda" respectful tone, not "kamu/lo"
