### 4. Market Research
**The Transition: Traditional Kos vs Co-Living**
*   **Traditional:** Focus on "room only", owner-managed, low utility fees.
*   **Co-Living:** Focus on "community + convenience", professionally managed (Rukita/Cove model), premium pricing, included services (Wi-Fi, Laundry, Cleaning).

**Unit Economics (Per Door):**
*   **Investment:** Rp 80jt - 150jt per door (construction + furnishing).
*   **Revenue:** Rp 2.5jt - 5jt per month (Jakarta Premium).
*   **OPEX:** 20-30% (Staff, Electricity, Marketing).
*   **Net Profit:** Rp 1.8jt - 3.5jt per door.

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Requirement:** NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) dengan KBLI yang tepat (e.g., 55900 - Penyediaan Akomodasi Lainnya).
*   **Taxation (2026 Update):**
    *   **PPh Final Pasal 4(2):** 10% for property rental.
    *   **OR UMKM 0.5%:** If gross revenue < Rp 4.8M and meets PP 23/2018 criteria (check with latest 2026 tax consultants).
    *   **No PBJT:** Confirmed by MK and UU HKPD.
*   **Permits:** IMB/PBG must specify residential/boarding use.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Bisnis kos itu pasif income."
*   **Reality:** Traditional kos is a "sweat equity" business unless you hire a manager or use a platform.
*   **Myth:** "Harus bayar pajak hotel 10% ke pemda."
*   **Reality:** Not anymore since UU HKPD 2022. If your local PEMDA still asks for it, show them the law.
