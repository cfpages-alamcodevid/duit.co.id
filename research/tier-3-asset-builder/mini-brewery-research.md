### 5.1 Core Licensing Framework

| Requirement | Regulation | Process | Cost (est.) | Timeline | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) | PP 5/2021 | OSS-RBA online | Rp 0 (gratis) | 1–3 hari | BKPM/OSS |
| IUI (Izin Usaha Industri) | Permenperin 17/2019 | OSS-RBA + Kemenperin verifikasi | Rp 5–15 juta (legal fees) | 1–3 bulan | Kemenperin |
| Izin Khusus Produksi Minuman Beralkohol | Permenperin 17/2019 | Kemenperin — persyaratan khusus | Tergantung kapasitas | 3–6 bulan | Kemenperin |
| Sertifikat Standar Produksi | Permenperin 17/2019 | Verifikasi pabrik/kesiapan produksi | Included in IUI | 1–2 bulan | Kemenperin |
| Izin Edar BPOM | BPOM Regulation | Online notifikasi produk | Rp 3–5 juta/produk | 3–6 bulan | BPOM |
| Cukai (Excise) registration | UU Cukai | DJBC — NPDPN | Gratis | 1–2 bulan | DJBC (Bea Cukai) |
| NPWP | UU PPh | DJP online | Gratis | 1 hari | DJP |

### 5.2 Critical Regulatory Notes

**1. Tertutup untuk Penanaman Modal Asing:**
KBLI 11010 (Industri Minuman Beralkohol) masuk kategori **bidang usaha tertutup** untuk Penanaman Modal berdasarkan Perpres 49/2021. Artinya: **PMA (Penanaman Modal Asing) tidak diizinkan**. Hanya Warga Negara Indonesia atau badan hukum yang 100% dimiliki WNI yang boleh memiliki izin.

**2. Permenperin 17/2019 — Key Requirements:**
- Berlokasi di Kawasan Peruntukan Industri (jika berpotensi menimbulkan pencemaran)
- Wajib memiliki akun Sistem Informasi Industri Nasional (SIINas)
- Kapasitas produksi built-in harus sesuai IUI — produksi melebihi kapasitas = pencabutan izin
- Kelas minuman beralkohol harus sesuai IUI (golongan A/B/C)
- Tidak ada kegiatan produksi 3 tahun berturut-turut = pencabutan izin

**3. BPOM Izin Edar:**
Semua produk minuman beralkohol yang diedarkan (botol/kaleng) wajib memiliki nomor notifikasi BPOM. Proses registrasi memerlukan:
- Hasil uji laboratorium (alkohol content, kontaminan)
- Label dalam Bahasa Indonesia
- Informasi komposisi lengkap
- Sertifikat produksi dari pabrik

**4. Excise Tax (Cukai):**
Minuman beralkohol dikenakan cukai berdasarkan golongan:
- **Gol A:** ≤5% alkohol (bir) — tarif cukai lebih rendah
- **Gol B:** 5%–20% alkohol — tarif cukai menengah
- **Gol C:** >20% alkohol — tarif cukai tertinggi

*Exact rates per liter vary and are updated periodically. 2026 estimates for Gol A (beer): Rp 15,000–25,000/liter depending on specific regulations.*

**5. Local Government Permits:**
- SITU-MB (Surat Izin Tempat Usaha Penjualan Minuman Beralkohol) — untuk tasting room
- SIUP-MB (Surat Izin Usaha Perdagangan Minuman Beralkohol) — untuk distribusi
- Izin Gangguan/HO — untuk lokasi usaha
- Perda setempat: beberapa daerah menerapkan larangan total peredaran miras (Qanun Aceh, beberapa daerah di Sumatera Barat, dll.)

### 5.3 Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Violation | Penalty | Regulation |
|---|---|---|
| Produksi tanpa IUI | Pidana penjara + denda | Permenperin 17/2019 |
| Melebihi kapasitas produksi | Pencabutan IUI | Permenperin 17/2019 |
| Edar tanpa BPOM | Pidana penjara maks. 12 tahun + denda maks. Rp 1.5B | UU Pangan |
| Pelanggaran cukai | Sanksi administratif + pidana | UU Cukai |
| Tidak lapor SIINas 2 tahun berturut | Pencabutan IUI | Permenperin 17/2019 |

## 8. Operations Guide

### 8.1 Brewmaster Recruitment

| Skill | Requirement | Salary Range (monthly) |
|---|---|---|
| Head brewer (certified, 3+ years experience) | Cicerone, IBD, or equivalent; recipe development | Rp 15–30 juta |

| Taproom manager | F&B management, inventory, staff scheduling | Rp 8–15 juta |

**Brewmaster sourcing strategy:**
- Import from craft beer markets (Australia, US, Europe) — expensive but high quality
- Train local talent via IBD (Institute of Brewing & Distilling) certifications
- Partner with existing Indonesian breweries for apprenticeship
- Send Indonesian staff abroad for training (6–12 months)
- Online brewing programs: UC Davis, Siebel Institute, IBD

### 8.2 Ingredient Sourcing

| Ingredient | Source | Typical Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Malt (barley) | Import: Australia, Germany, Belgium | Rp 15,000–30,000/kg | Dominated by imports; price sensitive to AUD/EUR |
| Hops (pellets) | Import: US, Germany, Australia, NZ | Rp 100,000–400,000/kg | Most expensive ingredient; needs cold storage |
| Hops (cryo/extract) | Import | Rp 500,000–1,500,000/kg | For dry-hopping/IPAs; very expensive |
| Yeast | Import or lab-cultured | Rp 500,000–3,000,000/pitch | Can re-pitch 5–8 generations |
| Specialty ingredients (fruit, coffee, honey) | LOCAL — Indonesia rich in these | Variable | **Competitive advantage** for Indonesian brewers |
| Water treatment chemicals | Local (multi-chem, Brenntag) | Rp 200,000–500,000/batch | Essential for consistency |

**Critical insight: Hops are imported.** Unlike malt (which can come from Australia with lower freight), hops are almost exclusively US/German/NZ sourced. This means:
- Exposure to USD exchange rate
- 3–6 month lead time (must contract forward)
- Cold chain required (-18°C to -20°C for long-term storage)
- Budget: Rp 8–15 juta per batch for ingredient cost (500L system)

### 8.4 Distribution Logistics

**Channel Strategy:**

| Channel | Margin | Volume | Logistic Complexity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Taproom (direct) | Highest | Lowest | None |
| Local bars/restaurants | Medium | Medium | Moderate (local delivery) |

## 9. Revenue Model Deep Dive

### 9.1 Taproom Economics (Highest Margin)

**Revenue per pint calculation (500ml pour):**
- Price: Rp 80,000/pint
- Cost of goods (ingredients): Rp 8,000–12,000 (10–15%)
- Cost of packaging (none): Rp 0
- Excise tax (cukai): Rp 8,000–12,500 (10–15%)
- Labor & overhead: Rp 12,000–16,000 (15–20%)
- **Gross margin: 55–65%**

**Taproom throughput:**
- Small tasting room (30 seats): 50–100 customers/day avg
- Revenue/customer: Rp 150,000–250,000 (2–3 pints + food/merch)
- Monthly taproom revenue: Rp 225M–750M (depends on location + tourism season)
- Seasonality: 2x–3x revenue during peak tourist season

### 9.2 Wholesale Economics (Volume Driver)

**Revenue per keg calculation (30L = ~85 pints):**
- Price to bar/restaurant: Rp 1,200,000–1,800,000/keg
- Cost of goods: Rp 250,000–400,000
- Excise tax: Rp 250,000–375,000
- Keg depreciation: Rp 20,000–30,000 (kegs cost ~Rp 600K each, amortized over 20+ uses)
- Logistics: Rp 50,000–150,000
- **Gross margin: 35–50%**

### 9.3 Canned/Bottled Retail (Brand Building)

**Revenue per 330ml can:**
- Retail price: Rp 40,000–65,000
- Wholesale price (to distributor): Rp 25,000–35,000
- Cost of goods: Rp 5,000–7,000

## 10. Common Myths & Misconceptions

### Myth 1: "Brewery equipment is too expensive and depreciates fast"
**Reality:** Stainless steel brewery equipment (SUS304) holds value remarkably well. A well-maintained 5-year-old brewhouse can sell for 50–60% of original price. Fermentation tanks in good condition retain 65–80% value. This makes brewery equipment far more collateralizable than restaurant kitchen equipment.

### Myth 2: "Indonesia's Muslim majority makes craft beer impossible"
**Reality:** Indonesia has 250+ craft breweries and growing. The market targets the non-Muslim minority (~15% = 40M people), foreign tourists (~16M/year), and the urban secular demographic. Bali alone has 50+ breweries/beer bars. The industry exists and thrives within regulated boundaries.

### Myth 3: "You can start a brewery for Rp 500 million"
**Reality:** Rp 500M might cover the brewhouse alone. A realistic minimum for a professional mini brewery with tasting room is Rp 2.5–3 billion. Undercapitalization is the #1 cause of brewery failure globally.

### Myth 4: "Craft beer is just a trend that will fade"
**Reality:** The global craft beer market grew from $128B (2024) to a projected $460B (2035). Craft beer has been growing consistently for 15+ years in developed markets. Indonesia is in early stages. The "trend" is a structural shift in consumer preference toward quality over quantity.

### Myth 5: "I can brew beer at home and sell it"
**Reality:** Illegal and extremely dangerous. Home-brewed beer sold commercially faces severe penalties including imprisonment. All production must occur in a licensed industrial facility with proper quality control, BPOM registration, and excise tax payment.
