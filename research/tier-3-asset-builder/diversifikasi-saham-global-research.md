### 4. Market Research
**Platforms for Indonesians:**
1.  **Bappebti-Regulated (Pluang, Gotrade Indonesia, Nanovest):** Easy IDR deposit, regulated locally, but technically "Derivatives" of US stocks (PALN).
2.  **International Brokers (Interactive Brokers - IBKR):** Real physical shares, lowest fees for large amounts (>Rp 500jt), access to global ETFs (VTI, VOO), but requires USD wiring (SWIFT).

**Asset Allocation Strategy:**
*   **Core:** S&P 500 (VOO) or Nasdaq 100 (QQQ).
*   **Satellite:** Individual stocks (NVDA, MSFT, AAPL) or sector ETFs (Semiconductors, AI).

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Bappebti:** Regulates "Penyaluran Amanat Nasabah ke Bursa Luar Negeri" (PALN).
*   **Taxation (Indonesian Side):**
    *   **Capital Gains:** Reported as global income. Progressive rates (5-35%).
    *   **Dividends:** Reported as global income. 0% if reinvested in Indonesia (UU HPP), but check specific cross-border rules for 2026.
    *   **SPT Reporting:** All global assets must be listed in the "Harta" section of the annual tax return.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**The "SWIFT vs App" Math:**
*   **Investing 10jt:** Use Pluang/Gotrade. SWIFT fees (~350rb) would eat 3.5% of capital immediately.
*   **Investing 500jt:** Use IBKR. SWIFT fee is only 0.07% of capital. You get better spreads and lower management fees on ETFs.
*   **Recommendation:** Use local apps for DCA under 50jt, use international brokers for large "Lump Sum" investments.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Investasi luar negeri itu ilegal."
*   **Reality:** It's legal. Bappebti even provides a specific framework (PALN) for it.
*   **Myth:** "Pajak di AS sangat tinggi (30%)."
*   **Reality:** With the US-Indonesia tax treaty and W-8BEN form, it's reduced to 15%.
