## 4. Market & Cultural Context: The Indonesian Succession Landscape

### The "Who" Problem in Indonesia

**The Three-Headed Barrier:**

1. **"Anak harus lanjutkan" — The Mandatory Heir Assumption**
   - Indonesian business culture strongly assumes children MUST continue the business
   - GEM data: only 31% of NextGen actually want to — the mismatch creates family tension
   - Many founders never ask: "Do you actually WANT this business?"
   - Result: Forced succession → disengaged heir → business decline → eventual failure

2. **"Orang tua tidak boleh mati" — The Death Taboo**
   - Discussing succession is culturally associated with discussing death
   - Most Indonesian business owners avoid the topic entirely
   - PwC data: 19% delay due to uncertainty (vs 10% global) — rooted in this taboo
   - Result: No conversation → no plan → crisis when unexpected event happens

3. **"Saya masih dibutuhkan" — The Founder Identity Trap**
   - Business = identity for many founders (especially Tier 3 who built from scratch)
   - Letting go means losing purpose
   - PwC data: 43% of NextGen say seniors block succession
   - Result: Founders stay too long, train successors too late, create dependency

### Multiple Heirs Dilemma

Tier 3 Asset Builders typically have 2-4 children. Common scenarios:

| Scenario | Challenge | Succession Implication |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 child wants business, others want cash | Fairness perception, liquidity need | Life insurance, buy-sell agreement, dividend policy |
| All children want business | Power struggle, no clear leader | Define roles, consider splitting into divisions or holding structure |
| No child wants business | Forced sale or professional CEO | Need exit-ready business, sell to strategic buyer or 3rd party |
| Children too young (under 25) | Long transition period | Trust structure, professional management until readiness |

### The NextGen Gap: Two Types of Indonesian Heirs

**Type A: The Reluctant Heir (most common)**
- Has education (often overseas)
- Has different interests (tech, startup, creative industries)
- Sees family business as "old school" or "boring"
- Feels pressured but resentful
- Strategy needed: Find their role, give autonomy, adapt business

**Type B: The Enthusiastic but Undertrained Heir**
- Wants the business
- Brought into company too early or too late
- Never given real responsibility
- Resented by long-term employees
- Strategy needed: Structured training, clear authority progression, mentor assignment

## 6. Practical Frameworks

### Framework 1: The 5-Year Succession Roadmap (Year-by-Year)

This is the CORE framework for the article — the "cara memulai" part.

```
Year 1: Foundation & Conversation (NOW!)
- [ ] Start the family conversation (break the taboo)
- [ ] Ask each child: "Apakah kamu MAU meneruskan bisnis ini?"
- [ ] Create a preliminary list of all business & personal assets
- [ ] Get a baseline business valuation (use DCF or multiple method)
- [ ] Identify gaps in legal structure (AD, shareholder agreement, wasiat)
- Estimated cost: Rp 3-10 juta (valuation + legal consultation)
- Key milestone: Clear answer on WHO is interested and capable

Year 2: Exploration & Preparation
- [ ] Successor(s) join business in entry-level or rotational positions
- [ ] Assess successor's strengths, weaknesses, interests
- [ ] Hire professional facilitators if family tension exists
- [ ] Draft/update shareholder agreement for succession clauses
- [ ] Begin SOP documentation (if not already done)
- [ ] Define dividend policy for non-business heirs
- Estimated cost: Rp 10-25 juta (legal + facilitation)
- Key milestone: Successor has real business experience (>6 months)

Year 3: Structured Development
- [ ] Successor takes on a departmental leadership role with KPIs
- [ ] Assign external mentor or board advisor
- [ ] Formal education/training (management, finance, industry-specific)
- [ ] Draft succession clauses in Anggaran Dasar (via notaris)
- [ ] Begin separation: owner vs manager roles
- Estimated cost: Rp 15-40 juta (training + legal + advisory)
- Key milestone: Successor manages a P&L independently

Year 4: Transition & Delegation
- [ ] Founder reduces operational involvement by 40-50%
- [ ] Successor becomes COO/Director with real authority
- [ ] Founder focuses on strategic/board-level decisions
- [ ] Full legal documentation completed (wasiat, AD, hibah if applicable)
- [ ] Life insurance/ liquidity plan in place for non-business heirs
- Estimated cost: Rp 20-50 juta (comprehensive legal + insurance)
- Key milestone: Business runs without founder for 2 weeks

Year 5: Handover & Letting Go
- [ ] Founder transitions to Board of Commissioners/Advisory role
- [ ] Successor becomes CEO/Dirut
- [ ] Founder exit plan: what will YOU do next? (next venture, philanthropy, retirement)
- [ ] Monitor: 6-month and 12-month post-handover reviews
- [ ] Celebrate the transition — make it a positive family event
- Estimated cost: Rp 5-10 juta (final legal)
- Key milestone: Founder does not make operational decisions for 3 months
```

**Total estimated cost for 5-year comprehensive succession plan:** Rp 53-135 juta — less than 0.1% of business value for most Tier 3 asset builders (Rp 10-50B+ businesses).

### Framework 2: Heir Selection Criteria — Choose Your "Who"

```
The 5-Factor Heir Assessment:

Factor 1: MINAT (Interest) — Weight: 30%
- Does the person genuinely WANT to run this business?
- Or are they doing it out of obligation?
- Red flag: "Saya terpaksa" — forced heirs rarely succeed
- Green flag: Proactive ideas, asks questions, shows curiosity

Factor 2: KOMPETENSI (Competence) — Weight: 25%
- Does the person have (or can develop) the skills needed?
- Technical: industry knowledge, operations, finance
- Leadership: people management, decision making, communication
- Red flag: No relevant experience at age 35+
- Green flag: Has worked outside the family business for 2-5 years

Factor 3: KOMITMEN (Commitment) — Weight: 20%
- Is the person willing to put in 10-15 years of hard work?
- Are they willing to start from the bottom?
- Red flag: Wants CEO role without proving capability first
- Green flag: "Saya mau belajar dari bawah"

Factor 4: KARAKTER (Character) — Weight: 15%
- Integrity, work ethic, ability to handle pressure
- Respect for employees, suppliers, customers
- Red flag: Entitlement mentality, disrespects long-term employees
- Green flag: Humble, teaches/mentors others

Factor 5: KECOCORAN (Fit) — Weight: 10%
- Does their vision align with the business direction?
- Can they work with other family members professionally?
- Red flag: Personal conflicts with siblings/cousins in business
- Green flag: Brings complementary skills the business needs
```

**Decision Scenarios:**

| Assessment | Recommended Action |
|------------|-------------------|
| 1 child scores 80%+ | Clear successor — start the 5-year roadmap |
| 2 children score 60-70% | Consider co-leadership or split into divisions with clear boundaries |
| No child scores >50% | Professional CEO + family as owners OR sell the business |
| Mixed: 1 high, others low | Non-business heirs get cash/assets; business heir gets company |

### Framework 3: Succession Models — The 4 Options

```
Option A: Full Family Succession (Business → 1 child)
Best when: 1 clear candidate with high interest + competence
Risks: Other children feel unfair, successor feels entitled
Mitigation: Life insurance/liquidity for other heirs, clear dividend policy
Legal: Wasiat + shareholder agreement + buy-sell clause

Option B: Sibling Partnership (Business → 2+ children)
Best when: Multiple capable children, complementary skills
Risks: Conflict, deadlock, diffused accountability
Mitigation: Clearly defined roles + tie-breaker mechanism + family council
Legal: Shareholder agreement with deadlock resolution

Option C: Professional Management (Family owns, hired CEO runs)
Best when: No child wants business OR no qualified child
Risks: Agency problem (CEO doesn't care like owner), loss of family culture
Mitigation: Strong board, family council, clear KPIs for CEO
Legal: Management contract + board governance structure

Option D: Exit / Sale (Sell to 3rd party, strategic buyer, or MBO)
Best when: No interest from any child, business has sellable value
Risks: Emotional loss, selling below value if unprepared
Mitigation: Get exit-ready first (3-5 years preparation), clean up books
Legal: M&A advisory, due diligence preparation
```

### Framework 4: The Financial Impact of Delay — A Calculated Example

```
Case: PT Sentosa Abadi — Manufacturing company, annual revenue Rp 50 M, EBITDA Rp 8 M

Scenario A: Start succession NOW (Founder age 55)
- Year 1-5: Succession preparation cost: Rp 100 juta
- Year 5+: Professional management + gradual founder exit
- Valuation at exit/sale: 5x EBITDA = Rp 40 M (systemized, not owner-dependent)
- Wealth preserved for family: Rp 40 M
- Cost of planning: 0.25% of value

Scenario B: Delay until crisis (Founder dies suddenly at age 62)
- No succession plan, no trained successor
- Business valuation: 2x EBITDA = Rp 16 M (owner-dependent, forced sale)
- Wealth preserved for family: Rp 16 M
- Plus: 2 years of business disruption, key customer loss, employee uncertainty
- Effective loss: Rp 24 M (60% value destruction)

COST OF DELAY: Rp 24 M — or 60% of business value
```

### Framework 5: The Family Conversation Script — How to Bring Up the Topic

This is the HARDEST step for most Indonesian founders. Provide a script:

```
"Anak-anak, Papa/Mama mau ngomong serius. Bukan karena Papa/Mama sakit atau mau pergi — justru karena Papa/Mama sehat dan mau memastikan masa depan bisnis ini baik-baik saja.

Papa/Mama sudah bangun bisnis ini [X] tahun. Ini bukan cuma soal uang — ini soal melanjutkan apa yang sudah dibangun. Tapi Papa/Mama sadar: mungkin kalian punya mimpi sendiri.

Jadi pertanyaan Papa/Mama sederhana: 
Apakah di antara kalian ada yang BENAR-BENAR MAU melanjutkan bisnis ini?

Papa/Mama tidak akan marah kalau jawabannya 'tidak'. Yang Papa/Mama takutkan adalah: kalian menjawab 'iya' tapi sebenarnya tidak mau, atau kita tidak pernah ngomong sama sekali.

Pikirkan dulu. Bulan depan kita ngobrol lagi."
```

**Key communication principles:**
1. Frame as "stewardship" not "death planning"
2. Ask before assuming — let children answer honestly
3. Normalize "tidak mau" as a valid answer
4. Give time for reflection — don't demand instant answer
5. Involve spouse/partner in the conversation

```

Reality: Only 31% of Indonesian NextGen actually want to continue the family business (GEM 2022). The majority would prefer other paths.
Why it matters: Assuming without asking creates pressure, resentment, and eventual failure.
Source: Global Entrepreneurship Monitor 2022; JurnalPost 2026
```

```
Myth: "Succession planning itu mahal dan ribet, nanti saja"
Reality: A comprehensive 5-year plan costs Rp 53-135 juta — less than 0.1-0.5% of business value. The cost of NOT planning: 40-70% value destruction.
Why it matters: "Nanti saja" is the most expensive phrase in family business.
Source: Exit Planning Institute, estimated based on Indonesian legal fees
```

```
Myth: "Yang penting bisnis berjalan — urusan penerus bisa diatur belakangan"
Reality: If the founder dies without a succession plan, the business STOPS. Bank accounts freeze, contracts can't be signed, payroll can't run. The business CANNOT legally continue until the estate is settled (6-24 months).
Why it matters: The business dies the same day as the founder if no plan exists.
Source: Corporate law practice in Indonesia; KUHPerdata estate settlement
```

```
Myth: "Saya masih muda (50 tahun) — masih ada waktu 20 tahun lagi"
Reality: A proper succession takes 5-10 years. Starting at 50 = ready at 55-60. Starting at 60 = crisis mode. Sudden health issues, accidents, or economic shocks can strike at any age.
Why it matters: The best time to plant a tree was 10 years ago. The second best time is now.
Source: Succession planning best practices
```

```
Myth: "Satu anak melanjutkan, yang lain pasti setuju"
Reality: Unequal inheritance (one child gets the business, others get cash/assets) is the #1 cause of family conflict. Without clear documentation AND communication, disputes are almost guaranteed.
Why it matters: Even harmonious families can fracture over money.
Source: Mahkamah Agung data: >3.500 inheritance dispute cases in 2022, 15% increase YoY
```

```
Myth: "Kalau anak tidak mau, lebih baik bisnis dijual saja"
Reality: Selling is ONE option, but not the only one. Professional management (CEO hired, family remains owners) can work brilliantly. Many of Indonesia's largest family businesses (Djarum, Kalbe Farma) use professional management with family ownership.
Why it matters: "Exit" is not the only alternative to "family succession."
Source: PUG-BMKI (Pedoman KNKG 2022); various successful family business examples
```

```
Myth: "Succession planning = surat wasiat"
Reality: A will is only ONE component. True succession planning includes: successor training, shareholder agreement, governance structure, family communication, financial planning, and psychological preparation. The will is the end of the process, not the beginning.
Why it matters: A will without trained successor = the business still fails.
Source: Family business governance best practices
```

## 10. Action Steps Outline (Year-by-Year)

### Year 1: Start the Conversation (NOW — within 30 days)
- **Details:** Hold a family meeting with spouse + all adult children. Ask the ONE question: "Siapa yang benar-benar MAU meneruskan?"
- **Example script provided above in Framework 5**
- **Warning:** Do NOT pressure for instant answer. Give children 1-2 months to reflect.

### Year 1 (Month 2-6): Assessment
- **Details:** Get baseline business valuation. List all assets. Identify each child's interest and capability.
- **Include:** Formal or informal personality/competency assessment
- **Warning:** Don't assume the eldest child is the successor

### Year 1 (Month 6-12): Decision & Communication
- **Details:** Decide on succession model (family, professional, sale). Communicate decision to ALL children clearly.
- **Document:** Minutes of family meeting — written, signed by all
- **Warning:** Vague promises create future conflict — be specific

### Year 2: Integration & Training
- **Details:** Successor starts working in the business. Not as "boss" — in operational roles.
- **Include:** Rotational program (finance, operations, sales — at least 3 months each)
- **Warning:** Don't put successor directly in management — they must earn respect

### Year 3: Leadership Development
- **Details:** Successor takes departmental leadership with real P&L responsibility.
- **Include:** External training / MBA / industry certification if needed
- **Warning:** Founder must let successor make mistakes (within reason)

### Year 4: Delegation Phase
- **Details:** Founder hands over 50%+ of operational decisions.
- **Include:** Establish board/advisory — formalize governance
- **Warning:** Founder must actually LET GO — not "micromanage from afar"

### Year 5: Handover
- **Details:** Formal transfer of leadership. Founder moves to board/strategic role.
- **Include:** Legal transfer of shares (gradually or at once, tax-optimized)
- **Celebration:** Hold a formal handover event — positive framing

### Ongoing: Communication
- **Hold annual family meetings**
- **Update plan every 2-3 years as circumstances change**
- **Keep non-business heirs informed and engaged**
