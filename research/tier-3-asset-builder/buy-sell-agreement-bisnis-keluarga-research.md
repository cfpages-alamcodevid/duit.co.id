## Legal & Regulatory Requirements
**Requirement:** BSA must comply with company's Articles of Association (AD/ART)  
**Regulation:** Article 55 UU No. 40/2007 tentang Perseroan Terbatas  
**Process:** Include BSA clauses in AD/ART during company establishment or amendment via RUPS  
**Cost:** Notary fees for AD/ART amendment (Rp 500,000-1,000,000)  
**Timeline:** 30 days for Menteri Hukum dan HAM approval of AD/ART changes  
**Authority:** Menteri Hukum dan Hak Asasi Manusia  
**Link:** https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/Home/Details/12421/uu-no-40-tahun-2007  

**Requirement:** Share transfers require RUPS approval if specified in AD/ART  
**Regulation:** Article 55 UU No. 40/2007  
**Process:** Call RUPS meeting, obtain shareholder approval for BSA terms  
**Cost:** Meeting administration costs (Rp 1-5 million)  
**Timeline:** 14 days notice for RUPS meeting  
**Authority:** Direksi perusahaan  
**Link:** https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/Home/Details/12421/uu-no-40-tahun-2007  

**Requirement:** BSA valuation must be reasonable and ascertainable  
**Regulation:** General contract principles from KUHPerdata Article 1320  
**Process:** Use licensed appraiser or predetermined formula  
**Cost:** Appraisal fees (Rp 5-20 million depending on business size)  
**Timeline:** Annual valuation updates recommended  
**Authority:** Penilai Publik (licensed appraiser)  
**Link:** https://peraturan.bpk.go.id/Home/Details/12421/uu-no-40-tahun-2007

## Common Myths & Misconceptions
**Myth:** BSA is only for large corporations  
**Reality:** Essential for any family business with multiple owners to prevent disputes  
**Why it matters:** Small family businesses face same succession risks  
**Source:** PwC survey showing 80% of disputes in SMEs  

**Myth:** Oral agreements are sufficient  
**Reality:** Must be in writing and comply with UU 40/2007  
**Why it matters:** Oral agreements unenforceable in court  
**Source:** Indonesian Commercial Court cases  

## Action Steps Outline
**Step 1:** Assess current ownership structure  
- Details: Review AD/ART, identify shareholders, current valuation  
- Example: Map family tree and ownership percentages  
- Warning: Don't proceed without legal review  

**Step 2:** Determine triggering events and valuation method  
- Details: Define death, disability, retirement triggers; choose formula  
- Example: Book value + 20% premium  
- Warning: Ensure valuation is fair and updated annually  

**Step 3:** Set funding mechanism  
- Details: Life insurance, sinking fund, or installment payments  
- Example: Cross-purchase with key person insurance  
- Warning: Test funding availability during implementation  

**Step 4:** Draft and implement BSA  
- Details: Work with notary and corporate lawyer  
- Example: Include in shareholders agreement  
- Warning: Get all shareholder signatures  

**Step 5:** Regular review and updates  
- Details: Annual valuation updates, policy reviews  
- Example: Amend BSA for new family members  
- Warning: Don't neglect maintenance
