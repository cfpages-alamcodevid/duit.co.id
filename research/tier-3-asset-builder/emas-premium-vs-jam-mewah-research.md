## 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

### Myth 1: "Gold and watches appreciate similarly"
**Reality:** Gold has averaged ~8-10% pa over 30+ years. Only specific watch models (steel sports Rolex/Patek/AP) beat gold. Most watches (90%+) depreciate or stay flat. Gold is MORE predictable but LOWER ceiling.

### Myth 2: "Jam mewah sama liquid-nya dengan emas"
**Reality:** This is the biggest myth. Gold has a government-backed buyback program (Antam) with published daily prices and same-day cash. Watches depend entirely on finding a willing buyer. In a market downturn, watch liquidity dries up faster than gold. 2008 GFC: gold held value, luxury watches dropped 30-50%.

### Myth 3: "Emas perhiasan sama dengan emas batangan"
**Reality:** Jewelry has 15-30% spread (ongkos pembuatan hilang saat jual). Emas batangan Antam hanya ~6,4% spread. Jika Anda beli perhiasan Rp 10 juta, saat jual kembali mungkin hanya Rp 7-8 juta. Emas batangan Rp 10 juta, jual kembali Rp 9,36 juta.

### Myth 4: "Belanja jam di luar negeri lebih murah + bisa dijual di Indonesia dengan harga pasar Indonesia"
**Reality:** Ada risiko Bea Cukai (bea masuk 25-30% + PPn 11% + PPNBM 20%). Kalau tidak lapor, jam dianggap ilegal dan susah jual karena buyer minta bukti resmi (box & papers + invoice). Jual tanpa invoice resmi Indonesia = potongan harga tambahan 15-20%.

### Myth 5: "Semua Rolex naik 15% per tahun"
**Reality:** Hanya steel sports models (Submariner, Daytona, GMT-Master II) dan itupun dalam bull market 2016-2025. Gold/diamond bezel models, two-tone, dan dress watches (Datejust, Day-Date) have FLAT to NEGATIVE returns in secondary market.

### Myth 6: "Gadai emas di Pegadaian rugi karena bunganya tinggi"
**Reality:** Gadai emas Pegadaian bunga ~1-1,5% per bulan (12-18% pa). But when you need emergency cash WITHOUT selling your asset, this is cheaper than the 15-25% haircut you'd take selling a watch. "Gadai Emas" is a liquidity feature watches don't have.

### Myth 7: "Gold is boring, watches are exciting — same return"
**Reality:** Gold return 2021-2026 (5 years) = ~165% total return (US$ 1.800 -> US$ 4.750). Best steel Rolex models ~80-125% in same period (exception: Daytona from Rp 800 jt -> Rp 1,8 M). Gold actually outperformed many watches in this 5-year window.

## 11. Risks Unique to Each Asset

### Gold Risks
- **Storage:** Safe deposit box required for meaningful amounts. Home storage risky for theft.
- **Price volatility:** Gold dropped ~35% in 2013 (from US$ 1.900 to US$ 1.200). Recovery took 7 years.
- **Certification:** Loss of Antam certificate reduces buyback value. Keep certs safe.
- **Counterfeit certificate:** Rare but exists. Buy only from official Antam/Pegadaian.
- **Tax changes:** Government could change PPh Final rate on gold transactions.

### Watch Risks
- **Counterfeit:** Superclone Rolex can fool even experienced buyers. Always buy from AD or certified pre-owned with authentication guarantee.
- **Market cycles:** Watch bubble could burst. 2022 saw 20-30% correction on hype models (Daytona, Nautilus). Recovery not guaranteed.
- **Theft:** Wearing a Rp 500 juta watch in public = theft target. Insurance non-negotiable.
- **Liquidity illusion:** Just because Chrono24 lists a watch at Rp X doesn't mean you'll get Rp X. Actual sale price can be 20-30% below ask.
- **Model risk:** Buying wrong model (dress, gold, quartz) = guaranteed loss.
- **Condition sensitivity:** Scratches, missing papers, unoriginal parts all reduce value significantly.
