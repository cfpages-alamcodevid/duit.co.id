### 4. Market Research
**The "Multi-Unit" Advantage:**
1.  **Shared Management:** 1 Area Manager covers 5 outlets.
2.  **Purchasing Power:** Buying 1,000 eggs/day vs 100 eggs/day for better margins.
3.  **Local Dominance:** Blocking competitors from entering a specific neighborhood by owning all the best corners.
4.  **Cross-Training:** If a staff member in Outlet A is sick, Outlet B can send a backup.

**The "Tipping Point" Challenge:**
*   1 Unit: You are the manager.
*   2 Units: You are exhausted.
*   3 Units: Things start breaking (theft/quality drop).
*   5+ Units: You MUST hire an Area Manager. Profit per unit might drop, but total profit and freedom increase.

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Area Development Agreement (ADA):** A separate contract from the standard Franchise Agreement. Ensures territorial exclusivity.
*   **Labor Compliance (UU Cipta Kerja):** Managing 50 employees across 5 sites requires professional HR/Payroll systems to avoid legal fines.
*   **Pajak Terpusat:** Choosing between Individual tax reporting vs. setting up a PT (Holding) for the 5 units.

### 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **Expert Quote:** "The first unit is for learning. The fifth unit is for earning. Don't stop at one if you want true wealth." - Multi-unit Owner (Indomaret).
*   **Case Study:** "Sandi, Jakarta." Owned 1 Self-Service Laundry. Profit 10jt. Opened 4 more in the same district. Used 1 technician for all 5. Profit per unit rose to 12.5jt due to shared costs. Total profit: 62.5jt/mo.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**The "District Domination" Map:**
*   Target a 5km radius.
*   Open 3 outlets in the high-traffic spots.
*   Use a "Hub and Spoke" inventory system: 1 outlet has a larger warehouse and supplies the other 2.
*   Result: Lower logistics cost and complete brand awareness in that district.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Kalau 1 unit untung, 5 unit pasti untung 5x lipat."
*   **Reality:** Profit might be 4x or 6x depending on management. 5 units also mean 5x the risk of a bad local manager.
*   **Myth:** "Bisa dikelola sendiri semua."
*   **Reality:** Beyond 3 units, your "Owner" brain must switch from "Operational" to "Strategic".

### 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Step 1: Master the First Unit.** (SOPs must be perfect).
2.  **Step 2: Negotiate ADA (Area Development Agreement).** Get a discount on the franchise fee for bulk purchases.
3.  **Step 3: Build the Management Layer.** Hire your first Area Supervisor.
4.  **Step 4: Centralize Finance.** Use a unified POS and accounting system.
5.  **Step 5: Standardize Training.** Create a "Training Center" in your best outlet for all new hires.
