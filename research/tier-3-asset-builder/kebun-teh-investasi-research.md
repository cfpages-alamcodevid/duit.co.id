## 6. Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Malino Highlands (Gowa, South Sulawesi)

**Background:** Former 900 ha tea plantation (now 200 ha operational) transformed into integrated agrotourism destination.
**Location:** Tinggimoncong, Gowa, South Sulawesi (1,200 m altitude)
**Key Features:**
- Green Pekoe Café (Japanese-style architecture at 1,600 m altitude)
- Tea garden tours and educational walks
- Villas (Rp 1.75-10 Jt/night)
- Adventure activities (horse riding, paragliding, bungee)
- Hutan pinus, Air Terjun Takapala
**Revenue Model:**
- Ticket: Rp 50,000 (adult), Rp 25,000 (child)
- Food & beverage (tea-based menu)
- Accommodation (villa rentals)
- Adventure activity fees
- Tea sales (exclusive Malino Green Tea, not sold in stores)
**Key Lessons:**
- Diversification from pure plantation to destination increases revenue per visitor dramatically
- Tea house/café becomes the anchor attraction — not just an add-on
- Exclusive product strategy (tea available only at location) creates scarcity and demand
- Partnership with Kemenparekraf provides promotional support
**Source:** Kemenparekraf, MalinoHighlands.com, various travel media

### Case Study 2: PPTK Gambung + Pepeteka Cafe (Pasirjambu, Bandung)

**Background:** Pusat Penelitian Teh dan Kina (PPTK) — Indonesia's only dedicated tea research institution. Manages 636 ha tea estate. Started agro-eduwisata program in 2017.
**Location:** Desa Mekarsari, Pasirjambu, Kab. Bandung, Jawa Barat
**Key Features:**
- Pepeteka Cafe (opened ~2022, 360° tea garden view, capacity ~100 pax)
- Educational tea programs (EduTea Half Day Trip: Rp 30,000-38,000/person)
- Tea processing factory tour
- Mini processing plant for specialty teas (white tea, wood oolong, silver dew)
- 21 researchers on staff (agronomy, soil, pest control, processing)
- Historical value: Makam R.E. Kerkhoven (founder)
**Revenue Model:**
- Cafe beverage sales (specialty tea Rp 45,000-50,000/pot, serves 4-6 people)
- Educational tour packages
- Processed tea sales (100% green tea since 2016 shift)
- Wisma (guest house) rental
- Research services
**Key Lessons:**
- Partnered with CV Jenggala Lestari for cafe operations (asset-light model)
- 82.4% positive response from digitalization of drying temperature monitoring (Telkom University collaboration)
- Shifted from black tea to green tea in 2016 to match market demand
- White tea as premium specialty product (highest antioxidant)
- 1907 heritage tea plants used as unique selling point
**Source:** DetikJabar, TCR Journal, Kompasiana, Jadesta Kemenpar

### Case Study 3: PTPN I — Teh Malabar Premium Export

**Background:** State-owned plantation enterprise. Kebun Malabar (since 1890) produces premium leafy grade OP FO tea.
**Achievement:** First export to Taiwan (July 2025) with 42 tons/year commitment potential.
**Market:** Specialty tea buyers in Taiwan, with expanding demand from Japan, UAE, Europe.
**Pricing:** Premium pricing with "heritage story" marketing.
**Key Lessons:**
- Premium storytelling (origin, heritage, quality) commands higher prices
- Direct buyer relationships (B2B) reduce intermediary margins
- Certification (organic, fair trade) enables premium export pricing
- 42 tons/year single-buyer commitment demonstrates demand for Indonesian specialty tea
**Source:** WartaEkonomi, Agricom.id, AnalisNews

## 7. Operational Details

### 7.1 Tea Plant Growth & Harvest Cycle

| Stage | Duration | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Nursery | 6-12 months | From cutting/graft to ready-to-plant |
| Immature (TBM) | 3-5 years | No economic harvest; requires pruning, weeding, fertilizing |
| Productive (TM) | 5-30+ years | Peak production 8-15 years; regular pruning every 3-4 years |
| Old/damaged (TR) | 30+ years | Productivity declines; may need replanting |

**Harvest Cycle:**
- Plucking interval: Every 10-15 days
- Harvests per year: 24-36 cycles
- Peak productivity: ~1,200-1,800 kg/ha/yr dried tea
- Labor required: ~1 worker per 0.5-1 ha for plucking + maintenance

### 7.2 Processing Flow

| Stage | Method | Duration | Equipment |
|-------|--------|----------|-----------|
| Withering (Pelayuan) | Air drying + controlled airflow | 12-18 hours | Withering troughs |
| Rolling (Penggulungan) | Mechanical rolling to break cells | 20-45 min | Rolling machine |
| Oxidation/Fermentation (Oksidasi) | Controlled temp/humidity (black tea only) | 1-4 hours | Temperature-controlled room |
| Fixation/Panning (Pemanggangan) | Heat stops oxidation (green tea) | 2-5 min | Panning machine (6CST series) |
| Drying (Pengeringan) | Hot air drying to 3-5% moisture | 15-30 min | Rotary drying machine |
| Sorting/Grading (Sortasi) | Screen mesh + air winnowing | Continuous | Sorting machine |
| Packaging | Manual or auto packing | Per batch | Packing machine |

**Labor requirement for small-scale processing plant:** 5-10 workers

### 7.3 Quality Grading

| Grade | Characteristics | Price Premium |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Pekoe (P) | Whole leaf, tip included | Highest |
| Orange Pekoe (OP) | Long, wiry whole leaf | Premium |
| Flowery Orange Pekoe (FOP) | Young leaves + golden tips | High |
| Broken Orange Pekoe (BOP) | Broken leaf, stronger brew | Medium |
| Fanning | Small pieces | Low |
| Dust | Powder | Lowest (CTC) |
| Orthodox | Traditional rolled leaf | Premium (71.9% of Indonesian production) |
| CTC (Crush-Tear-Curl) | Granular, fast brew | Commodity (6.6%) |

## 8. Asset Analysis

### 8.1 Land Appreciation

**Historical Data:**
- Highland land in West Java has appreciated ~5-10% annually over the past decade
- Tourism-driven areas (Ciwidey, Lembang, Puncak) appreciate faster: 10-15%/year
- Conversion pressure: 8% of tea land in Garut converted to vegetable farming (2018-2023)
- Total tea area declined from 150,000 ha (2000) → 106,000 ha (2023) → 97,539 ha (2024)
- Supply contraction + demand growth = long-term land value appreciation thesis

**Price Comparison (2024-2026 Listings):**
- Untreated/abandoned tea land: Rp 50,000-70,000/m²
- Productive tea land: Rp 100,000-200,000/m²
- Premium villa lots with tea view: Rp 800,000-1,000,000/m²
- This ~10x spread between raw land and developed lot demonstrates value creation opportunity

### 8.2 Processing Equipment Depreciation

- Machinery depreciates 10-15%/year (straight line)
- Expected life: 10-15 years with proper maintenance
- Tea-specific equipment has reasonable resale value in used market
- Mini processing lines (under Rp 500 Jt) are modular and expandable

### 8.3 Overall Asset Quality

| Asset | Liquidity | Appreciation | Income Yield | Risk |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|------|
| Plantation land (SHM) | Low-Medium | Medium-High (5-15%/yr) | Nil if unplanted | Land dispute, zoning |
| Productive tea garden | Low | Medium (3-8%/yr) | 2-8% of land value | Pest, climate, price |

## 9. Competition Analysis

### 9.1 Market Structure

| Player | Market Share (Area) | Focus | Competitive Advantage |
|--------|-------------------|-------|---------------------|
| PTPN (BUMN) | 26.1% | Large-scale, export orthodox black tea | Scale, heritage brands (Malabar, Gambung) |
| Private estates | 23.5% | Domestic bulk tea | Operational efficiency |
| Smallholders (rakyat) | 50.4% | Green leaf supply to factories | Low cost base, land ownership |
| Premium/specialty startups | <1% | Organic, specialty, direct-to-consumer | Premium pricing, storytelling |

### 9.2 Market Gaps

1. **Organic/Specialty Tea:** Massive gap — only <1% of Indonesian tea is certified organic. Global demand for organic tea grows 10-15%/year.
2. **Tea Tourism/Education:** Few plantations have integrated tea house + workshop experiences. PPTK Gambung, Malino, and Rancabali are exceptions.
3. **Direct-to-Consumer Premium Brands:** Most Indonesian tea is sold as bulk commodity. Branded premium tea (like Javara) commands 3-5x price.
4. **Matcha Production:** Indonesia imports significant matcha from Japan and China. Local production is minimal but has potential.
5. **Tea-based products beyond drinking:** Cosmetics, food ingredients, functional foods (as already piloted by Medini Tea Garden in Kendal).

## 10. Common Myths & Misconceptions

| Myth | Reality | Why It Matters |
|------|---------|---------------|
| "Beli kebun teh langsung menghasilkan" | Teh butuh 3-5 tahun TBM (tanaman belum menghasilkan) | Cash flow planning — need operating capital for first 3-5 years |
| "Teh Indonesia sudah mati" | Produksi turun, tapi konsumsi naik. Premium specialty tea adalah peluang baru | Creates opportunity for investors who target the growing premium segment |
| "Bisnis teh cuma cocok korporasi besar" | 50.4% lahan dikuasai petani rakyat. Private investor with 5-20 ha can compete | Scale barrier is lower than perceived |
| "Tea house itu bonus kecil" | Malino Highlands shows tea house can be the PRIMARY revenue driver | Revenue model should be designed around visitor experience, not just leaf sales |
| "Izin susah dan mahal" | NIB dan PIRT gratis via OSS. PIRT terbit 1 hari (sementara) | The regulatory barrier is lower than most investors assume |
