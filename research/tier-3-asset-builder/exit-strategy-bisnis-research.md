### 4. Market Research
**Exit Options in Indonesia:**
1.  **M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions):** Selling to a larger competitor (Strategic Buyer) or a Private Equity firm (Financial Buyer).
2.  **Management Buyout (MBO):** Selling to your loyal managers (often financed by future profits).
3.  **Family Succession:** Passing it to the next generation (requires a different legal structure).
4.  **IPO (Initial Public Offering):** Listing on the "Papan Akselerasi" or "Papan Pengembangan" at IDX.
5.  **Liquidation:** Closing down and selling assets. (The least profitable option).

**The "Exit Prep" Timeline:**
*   **2 Years Out:** Start cleaning up the books. Stop mixing personal and business expenses. Fix all legal permits (NIB, HAKI).
*   **1 Year Out:** Build a management team that can run without the owner (Autopilot status).
*   **6 Months Out:** Hire a business broker/advisor and prepare the "Information Memorandum" (Teaser).

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Due Diligence Ready:** Ensure all tax payments (SPT) are clear for the last 5 years.
*   **Permits:** Ensure NIB, PBG, and industry-specific licenses (BPOM, Halal, etc.) are up to date and transferable.
*   **Contracts:** Review supplier and customer contracts for "Change of Control" clauses.

### 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **Expert Quote:** "The best time to sell your business is when it's performing at its peak. Most owners wait until they are burnt out and the business is declining—that's when you lose 50% of your valuation." - M&A Specialist.
*   **Case Study:** "Pak Tedi, Owner of a Printing Chain." Wanted to retire. He spent 18 months systematizing his 10 outlets and hiring an Operations Manager. He sold to a larger digital printing firm for 4.5x EBITDA (Rp 12 Miliar). If he had sold as a "one-man show", he would have only gotten 2x EBITDA.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**Valuation "Bump" Strategy:**
*   Business Profit: 100jt/mo. Valuation (3x): 3.6B.
*   By cleaning up "waste" and formalizing contracts, Pak Tedi increased profit to 120jt/mo.
*   By hiring a manager, he increased the Multiplier from 3x to 4x.
*   **New Valuation:** (120jt x 12) x 4 = 5.76B.
*   **Gain from Prep:** Rp 2.16 Miliar.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Bisnis saya berharga karena saya sudah kerja keras 20 tahun."
*   **Reality:** Buyers don't pay for your hard work; they pay for *future* cash flow that *doesn't* require you.
*   **Myth:** "Jual bisnis itu cepat."
*   **Reality:** It takes 6-12 months of high-stress negotiations to close a good deal.

### 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Step 1: Get an Objective Valuation.** (Don't guess; hire a pro or use EBITDA math).
2.  **Step 2: Fire Yourself.** Can the business run for 30 days without you answering the phone?
3.  **Step 3: Clean the Books.** Get an independent audit or at least a professional tax review.
4.  **Step 4: Prepare the Teaser.** Highlight growth potential, not just history.
5.  **Step 5: Control the Narrative.** Why are you selling? (Retirement/New project are good; "Business is failing" is bad).
