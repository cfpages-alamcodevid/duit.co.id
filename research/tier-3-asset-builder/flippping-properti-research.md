### 4. Market Research
**The 70% Rule (Adapted for Indonesia):**
Don't pay more than 70% of the ARV (After Repair Value) minus renovation costs.
*Example:*
*   Expected Selling Price (ARV): Rp 1.000.000.000
*   Renovation Cost: Rp 200.000.000
*   Max Purchase Price: (1M x 0.7) - 200jt = Rp 500.000.000.

**Hot Flip Locations 2026:**
*   Older clusters in BSD/Gading Serpong.
*   "Kampung" houses near MRT/LRT stations in Jakarta.
*   Distressed villas in North Bali (Lovina/Singaraja).

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Pajak Pembeli (BPHTB):** 5% (Check for NPOPTKP in each city).
*   **Pajak Penjual (PPh):** 2.5%.
*   **Notary Fees (AJB/BN):** 1%.
*   **Check Legalitas:** Certificate status (SHM vs HGB), checking for "Blokir" at BPN, and inheritance issues (Warisan) which are common in "dirty/old" properties.

### 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **Expert Quote:** "You make your money when you buy, not when you sell. If you overpay for the 'ugly' house, no amount of paint will save your margin." - Professional Flipper.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**The "Lipstick on a Pig" Strategy (Cosmetic Flip):**
*   Focus on: Painting (Nippon/Dulux), New Floor Tiles (Granit 60x60), Modern Lighting, and Curb Appeal (Garden/Gate).
*   Avoid: Moving structural walls or fixing foundation issues unless necessary (too expensive for a quick flip).

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Flipping itu gampang kaya di TV."
*   **Reality:** Managing contractors in Indonesia is a full-time job. "Tukang kabur" is a real risk.
*   **Myth:** "Semua rumah kusam bisa di-flip."
*   **Reality:** Some houses have structural "cancer" (leaks/subsidence) that will eat all your profit.

### 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Step 1: Build the "A-Team".** (A reliable contractor, a smart notary, and a hungry property agent).
2.  **Step 2: Hunting the Distressed.** Look for Bank auctions, inheritance disputes, or "Butuh Uang" signs.
3.  **Step 3: The "Flash Reno".** Target 45-60 days for renovation. Every day extra is a cost (Carrying cost).
4.  **Step 4: Staging for Sales.** Rent furniture if needed to make it look "livable".
5.  **Step 5: Exit.** Price it 5% below market to sell within 30 days.
