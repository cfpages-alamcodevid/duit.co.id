### 4. Market Research
**The Bond Hierarchy (Indonesian Market):**
1.  **SBN Ritel (ORI, SR, SBR, ST):** Max investment Rp 3-5B, very liquid, guaranteed by State.
2.  **SBN FR (Fixed Rate):** The "Giant" market. No max limit, tradable in secondary market. Yield fluctuates with market interest rates.
3.  **Corporate Bonds (Obligasi Korporasi):** Issued by companies (e.g., ADMF, INDF, BBRI). Higher risk (Credit Risk), higher yield. Requires credit rating analysis (PEFINDO).

**Credit Ratings Explained:**
*   **AAA:** Highest safety (Blue Chip companies).
*   **AA/A:** High safety but sensitive to economic changes.
*   **BBB:** Investment Grade (Minimum for Tier 3 investors).
*   **Below BBB:** Junk Bonds (High risk of default).

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **SID (Single Investor Identification):** Required for all bond trades.
*   **Custodian Bank:** Corporate bonds are held in KSEI via a custodian bank or securities firm.
*   **WPP (Wakil Perantara Pedagang):** Direct bond trading (OTC) often requires a dedicated broker or Relationship Manager.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**The "Bond Ladder" Strategy:**
*   Investor has Rp 3 Miliar.
*   Maturity 1: 1B in 1-year SBN (Liquidity).
*   Maturity 2: 1B in 3-year Corporate Bond (AAA).
*   Maturity 3: 1B in 5-year SBN FR.
*   **Result:** You get cash back every few years to reinvest at current rates, while maintaining a high average yield.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Obligasi itu pasti aman."
*   **Reality:** Corporate bonds have "Default Risk". If the company goes bankrupt, bondholders are paid after creditors but before shareholders, but you could still lose money.
*   **Myth:** "Harus dipegang sampai jatuh tempo."
*   **Reality:** Bonds are tradable. If interest rates drop, your bond price rises, and you can sell for a "Capital Gain" before maturity.
