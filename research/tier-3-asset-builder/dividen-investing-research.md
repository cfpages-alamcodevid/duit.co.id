### 4. Market Research
**The Dividend Champions (Indonesian Context):**
1.  **Big Banks (BBCA, BBRI, BMRI, BBNI):** Stable growth, consistent payouts, safe haven.
2.  **Consumer Staples (ICBP, UNVR, MYOR):** Defensive during inflation.
3.  **Commodities (PTBA, ADRO, ITMG):** Very high yields but cyclical risk (Dividend Trap).
4.  **Telecommunication (TLKM):** Steady cash flow from data usage.

**Dividend Investing Styles:**
*   **Yield Shield:** Focusing on current high yields (e.g., Coal stocks).
*   **Dividend Growth:** Focusing on companies that increase dividends every year (e.g., Banks).

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Taxation:** 10% Final Tax on Dividends.
*   **Tax Exemption:** 0% Tax if dividends are reinvested into specific instruments (Stocks, SBN, Gold, etc.) within 3 months and held for 3 years.
*   **Reporting:** Must be reported in SPT (Annual Tax Return) even if tax-exempt.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**The "Dividend Trap" Warning:**
*   Stock X has a 20% Dividend Yield because the price dropped 50% due to declining business.
*   Investor buys for the dividend.
*   Next year, the company cuts the dividend to 0%.
*   Result: Investor lost 50% capital and gets no dividend.
*   **Strategy:** Check "Dividend Payout Ratio" (DPR) and earnings stability.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Dividen itu uang gratis."
*   **Reality:** The stock price usually drops by the dividend amount on "Ex-Date". Dividends are a distribution of wealth, not a creation of it (until the price recovers).
*   **Myth:** "Hanya untuk orang tua/pensiunan."
*   **Reality:** Dividend growth is the fastest way to compound wealth for young investors due to "Reinvestment Power".
