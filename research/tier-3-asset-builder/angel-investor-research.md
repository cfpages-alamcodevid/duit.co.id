### 4. Market Research
**The Angel Investment Process:**
1.  **Deal Flow:** Finding startups via networks (ANGIN), demo days, or personal referrals.
2.  **Due Diligence:** Checking the Founder's integrity, Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM), and Unit Economics (Burn rate vs. Growth).
3.  **Term Sheet:** Negotiating valuation and rights (Board seat, anti-dilution).
4.  **Value-Add:** Helping the startup with networking, regulatory access, or hiring.

**Sectors to Watch 2026:**
*   **Climate Tech & Circular Economy:** Waste management, EV infrastructure.
*   **MSME Digitization:** Tools for local shops to go digital.
*   **HealthTech:** Specialized clinics and digital patient management.

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Legal Instruments:**
    *   **Direct Equity:** Buying shares directly (requires Notary and AHU update).
    *   **Convertible Note:** A loan that converts to equity later. (Simpler, faster).
    *   **SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity):** Increasingly common in Jakarta startup circles.
*   **OJK:** Angel investing is generally unregulated private lending/investing, but beware of "Securities Crowdfunding" (SCF) platforms which are OJK-regulated.

### 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
*   **Expert Quote:** "If you can't afford to lose 100% of the money you put into a startup, you shouldn't be an angel investor. It's 'ego money' that occasionally turns into 'legacy money'." - Early Stage VC.
*   **Case Study:** "Herman, Angel Investor." Invested 500jt in 5 startups (total 2.5B). 3 failed completely (0). 1 is "zombie" (flat). 1 was acquired by a larger firm for 20x. Total return: 10B. Net Profit: 7.5B. IRR over 5 years is huge, despite the 60% failure rate.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**The "Value-Add" Checklist:**
*   Can you introduce the founder to 3 potential big clients?
*   Do you understand the regulations in their industry?
*   Can you help them with their next round of funding (VC connections)?
*   **Conclusion:** If you only provide money, you are a "Silent Angel". If you provide help, you are a "Lead Angel".

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Harus ngerti teknologi/coding."
*   **Reality:** Most startups need help with sales, legal, and finance—areas where Tier 3 investors are usually experts.
*   **Myth:** "Bisa kaya mendadak dalam 1 tahun."
*   **Reality:** Angel investing is a "Long Game" (5-10 years until exit).

### 9. Action Steps Outline
1.  **Step 1: Join a Syndicate.** (e.g., ANGIN) to see deals without having to find them yourself.
2.  **Step 2: Allocate a "Mad Money" Bucket.** (Max 5-10% of total wealth).
3.  **Step 3: Founder First.** Bet on the jockey, not the horse. Is the founder resilient?
4.  **Step 4: Diversify.** Aim for 10-20 small bets rather than 1 big bet.
5.  **Step 5: Master the Term Sheet.** Understand "Post-Money Valuation" vs. "Pre-Money".
