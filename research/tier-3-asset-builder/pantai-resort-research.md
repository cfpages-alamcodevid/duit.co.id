## 6. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### 6A. Coastal Zone Regulations

#### UU No. 27/2007 (as amended by UU No. 1/2014 and UU Cipta Kerja No. 11/2020)
**"Undang-Undang Pengelolaan Wilayah Pesisir dan Pulau-Pulau Kecil"**

Key provisions:
- **Sempadan Pantai (Coastal Border):** Minimum 100 meters from highest tide point inland. Building is RESTRICTED in this zone.
- **Perpres No. 51/2016** further defines coastal border limits.
- In Bali, Perda RTRW Provinsi Bali No. 2/2023 reinforces the 100m GSP (Garis Sempadan Pantai) as protected area.
- **IMPORTANT:** Building within the 100m coastal border requires special permits and is generally prohibited for permanent structures. Most beachfront resorts build beyond the 100m line or obtain special dispensation.
- Reclamation in coastal areas is heavily restricted — Perpres No. 122/2012 requires izin lokasi and izin pelaksanaan reklamasi.
- **PKKPRL (Persetujuan Kesesuaian Kegiatan Pemanfaatan Ruang Laut):** Mandatory document for ANY business using marine/coastal space. Violations can result in immediate shutdown and demolition.

#### REAL CASE — Enforcement Examples:
1. **Maratua, Kaltim (April 2026):** PT Storm Diving Resort shutdown and sealed by KKP for building over water without PKKPRL. 1 of 16 resorts in Maratua caught — proof that enforcement is active.
2. **Mawatu Resort, Labuan Bajo (2025):** Construction halted for building seawall outside permitted 6.5 Ha zone and conducting illegal reclamation. Perda NTT No. 4/2024 prohibits reclamation outright.
3. **Anyer-Carita, Banten (May 2026):** 70% of 154 beach destinations don't have NIB. Government crackdown underway.

### 6C. Land Certificate Types

| Certificate | Eligibility | Duration | Transferability | Notes |
|-------------|------------|----------|-----------------|-------|
| SHM (Sertifikat Hak Milik) | WNI only | Perpetual | Fully transferable | **Cannot be issued on land within 100m of coastline** if within sempadan pantai. Most beachfront land is HGB, not SHM. |
| HGB (Hak Guna Bangunan) | WNI & PMA (PT) | 30 years + extend 20 + renew 30 | Transferable | Most common for beachfront resorts. Can be owned by PT PMA (foreign investment). |
| HPL (Hak Pengelolaan) | Government/state-owned entities | Varies | Limited | Rare for private resort |
| Leasehold (Sewa) | Foreigners, investors | 25-50 years | Contract-based | Common in Raja Ampat, Lombok; no ownership rights |

**Key Warning — Pagar Laut Tangerang Case (2025):**
Menteri ATR/BPN canceled 209 SHGB/SHM certificates issued on sea territory. Principle: **"Laut tidak bisa disertifikasi sebagai tanah"** — the sea cannot be certified as land. Any certificate beyond the coastline (garis pantai) is void.

### 6D. Business Entity Options

| Entity | Ownership | Tax Rate | Liability | Notes |
|--------|-----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| PT (Perseroan Terbatas) | WNI | PPh Badan 22% | Limited | Best for Indonesian investors |
| PT PMA (foreign investment) | Foreign up to 100% | PPh Badan 22% | Limited | Minimum investment ~Rp 10B (BKPM rules). HGB only, not SHM. |
| CV (Commanditaire Vennootschap) | WNI | Personal tax bracket | Unlimited for active partners | Not recommended for resort-scale investment |
| Individual (perseorangan) | WNI | Personal tax | Unlimited | For very small (<5 room) homestay only |

**Best practice:** Establish PT for liability protection. Land and business can be under separate entities for tax optimization.

### 6E. Tax Obligations

| Tax Type | Rate | Frequency | Notes |
|----------|------|-----------|-------|
| PPh Badan (Corporate income tax) | 22% | Annual | For PT entities |

## 9. Operations & Management

### 9.1 Seasonality Management

**Peak season strategy:**
- July-August (European summer, Australian winter): 80-86% occupancy achievable
- December 20 - January 5 (Christmas/NY): 85%+
- Easter week, Chinese New Year: 75-85%
- Strategy: Dynamic pricing — raise ADR 30-50% from base rates during peak

**Low season strategy:**
- February-March: Lowest demand (55-60% Bali benchmark)
- Strategy for low season:
  - Domestic market targeting (Indonesian holiday calendar)
  - Corporate retreat packages (discounted group rates)
  - "Stay 3 pay 2" promotions
  - Wellness/yoga retreat packages
  - Wedding/event bookings (often planned months in advance)
  - School holiday alignment (June-July, December-January)

**Year-round strategies to smooth demand:**
- Diving/snorkeling package (activity-driven, less seasonal)
- Weddings (planned events, not weather-dependent)
- Corporate retreats (can be scheduled in low season)
- Digital nomad long-stay packages (monthly rates)
- Partnership with travel agents for shoulder-season bookings

### 9.2 Staffing Challenges in Remote Areas

**Typical staffing for 10-room boutique resort:**

| Position | Headcount | Monthly Salary Range |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| General Manager/Resort Manager | 1 | Rp 8-15 million |
| Receptionist/Front Desk | 2 | Rp 3-5 million |
| Housekeeping | 3 | Rp 2.5-4 million |
| Chef/Kitchen | 2 | Rp 5-10 million |
| F&B Service | 2 | Rp 2.5-4 million |
| Maintenance/Technician | 1 | Rp 3-5 million |
| Activity Guide/Dive Master | 1-2 | Rp 4-8 million |
| Security | 2 | Rp 2.5-3.5 million |
| Gardener/Landscaper | 1 | Rp 2.5-3 million |

**TOTAL STAFF: 16-18 people**
**TOTAL MONTHLY SALARY: Rp 45-75 million**

**Remote area challenges:**
- Difficulty attracting skilled hospitality talent (higher salary needed)
- Staff turnover higher (isolation, limited social life)
- Training costs higher (need to train local staff from scratch)
- Solution: Employ 70-80% local staff + key management from hospitality hubs
- **Case study:** Buahan Banyan Tree achieves 80% local hiring as core strategy, not CSR

### 9.3 Logistics

**Remote resort logistics costs:**
- Construction materials: 15-30% premium over urban prices (shipping + handling)
- Food supplies: weekly/monthly barge/shipment from nearest city
- Drinking water: either RO system (capital investment Rp 50-150 juta) or bottled supply
- Electricity: PLN connection if available; otherwise generator + solar hybrid
- Internet: Starlink (Rp 1-2 juta/month) or VSAT (Rp 5-15 juta/month) for remote islands
- Waste management: mandatory on-site treatment (STP — Sewage Treatment Plant)
- Medical evacuation plan required for remote island resorts (DAN insurance for diving)

**Power cost comparison:**
| Power Source | Installation Cost | Monthly Cost | Reliability |
|-------------|------------------|--------------|-------------|
| PLN grid connection | Rp 5-20 million (connection fee) | Rp 5-15 million | High (urban), variable (remote) |
| Generator (diesel) | Rp 50-150 million | Rp 10-30 million (fuel) | High (if maintained) |
| Solar panel system | Rp 200-500 million (10-20 kWp) | Rp 0-1 million (maintenance) | Moderate (weather dependent) |
| Hybrid (solar + generator + battery) | Rp 300-800 million | Rp 3-10 million (fuel backup) | Best reliability |

### 9.4 Environmental Sustainability

**Mandatory requirements:**
- AMDAL or UKL-UPL (environmental permit) — ALL beach resorts require this
- STP (Sewage Treatment Plant) — treated water only, no raw discharge to ocean
- Solid waste management plan — no burning, proper disposal
- Coastal erosion management plan (especially for beachfront properties)
- Mangrove preservation (if applicable on property)

**Competitive advantage through sustainability:**
- Certification options: Green Globe, EarthCheck, Green Key
- Eco-tourism market segment is growing at 15-20% annually
- Ken Research: eco-tourism in Indonesia projected USD 1.5 billion
- Buahan and Cap Karoso prove sustainability commands premium pricing

## 11. Common Myths & Misconceptions

```
Myth: "Saya bisa bangun resort persis di bibir pantai"
Reality: Sempadan pantai minimal 100 meter — bangunan permanen tidak diizinkan dalam zona ini.
  Resort harus dibangun di belakang garis 100m dari titik pasang tertinggi.
Why it matters: Membeli tanah di bibir pantai tanpa cek sempadan = risiko pembongkaran.
Source: UU 27/2007 jo. UU 1/2014, Perpres 51/2016

Myth: "Tanah pantai bisa SHM"
Reality: Sebagian besar tanah pantai hanya bisa HGB (Hak Guna Bangunan) atau bahkan hanya
  leasehold. SHM di zona sempadan pantai sangat langka dan berisiko.
Why it matters: SHM memberikan kepemilikan penuh, HGB terbatas waktu. HGB tetap bisa
  diperpanjang dan dijadikan agunan bank.
Source: Permen ATR/BPN No. 17/2021, PP 18/2021

Myth: "Bisnis resort itu passive income"
Reality: Resort adalah active business — full-time management required. But you CAN
  hire a GM. Operator model exists (franchise lite, revenue share operator).
Why it matters: Realistis: ini bisnis aktif minimal 2-3 tahun pertama.
Source: Multiple operator case studies

Myth: "Bali adalah satu-satunya lokasi yang menguntungkan"
Reality: Bali showing signs of oversaturation (Revenue -10.9% YoY for short-term rentals).
  Emerging markets (Lombok +9.1% revenue growth) offer better risk-adjusted returns.
Why it matters: First-mover advantage in emerging destinations > following the crowd in Bali.
Source: Airbtics 2025, Horwath HTL 2026

Myth: "Resort kecil (under 10 kamar) tidak bankable"
Reality: Banks lend based on proven cash flow, not room count. A 10-room resort with
  2 years of audited financials showing 20%+ margin IS bankable.
Why it matters: Small format can still access 70-80% LTV once operating history established.
Source: Major Indonesian bank lending guidelines 2026
```

## 12. Action Steps Outline

### Step 1: Site Selection & Due Diligence
- **Details:** Identify 3-5 potential locations based on budget, target market, and personal access
- **Checklist:**
  - Check RDTR (Rencana Detail Tata Ruang) for zoning — must be pink (tourism) or yellow (settlement)
  - Verify certificate type (HGB preferred for resort, verify remaining duration)
  - Check coastal border (sempadan pantai) — minimum 100m from highest tide
  - Verify no mangrove or protected ecosystem on site
  - Check accessibility (nearest airport, road condition)
  - Check utility availability (PLN, water, internet — or plan alternatives)
- **Warning:** Never buy land without PPAT/notary verification and BPN certificate check
- **Example:** Investor buys 2,000 m² in Kuta Lombok for Rp 4B (SHM verified, tourism zone, 150m from high tide line)

### Step 2: Business Planning & Financial Modeling
- **Details:** Build detailed financial model including construction, 12-month operating buffer
- **Key numbers:**
  - Construction cost per key: Rp 120-200 million (mid-range boutique)
  - Operating buffer: minimum 6 months
  - Revenue model: 55-65% rooms, 15-25% F&B, 8-15% water activities, 5-10% spa
  - Target occupancy: 55-65% conservative first year
- **Warning:** Most first-time resort investors underestimate operating costs by 20-30%

### Step 3: Permit Acquisition (Start 6-12 months before construction)
- **Critical path:**
  1. Establish PT entity → Get NIB (OSS-RBA)
  2. Apply for PKKPRL (if any marine activity) → 2-6 months
  3. Engage AMDAL consultant → 3-8 months
  4. Apply for PBG (building permit) → 2-4 months
  5. Apply for TDUP (tourism permit) → 2-4 weeks
- **Warning:** Do NOT start construction before PBG and AMDAL approval. Sealing risk is real.

### Step 4: Development & Construction
- **Timeline:** 12-18 months for 8-10 room boutique resort
- **Construction phasing:**
  - Phase 1: Land clearing, infrastructure (3 months)
  - Phase 2: Main building, villas (8-12 months)
  - Phase 3: Finishing, landscaping, pool (3-4 months)
  - Phase 4: FF&E, pre-opening (2-3 months)
- **Contractor strategy:** Use experienced hospitality contractor, not general contractor
- **Warning:** Remote locations = 15-30% material premium

### Step 5: Pre-Opening & Marketing
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months before opening
- **Key actions:**
  - Booking engine setup (direct website + OTA integration)
  - Professional photography/videography (critical for hospitality)
  - Rate strategy: opening rates (15-20% below target for first 3 months)
  - PR campaign (travel media, influencer familiarization trips)
  - Staff training (service standards, safety, hospitality)
  - Test stay program (friends & family for operational debugging)

### Step 6: Ongoing Operation & Asset Management
- **Year 1 focus:** Occupancy building, operational efficiency, guest reviews
- **Year 2 focus:** Rate optimization, revenue diversification, staff retention
- **Year 3 focus:** Bank financing optimization (refinance with proven cash flow), expansion planning
- **Exit options after Year 5:**
  - Sell as going concern (3-5x net profit multiple typical)
  - Sell land (land value likely 50-100% higher than purchase)
  - Refinance (pull equity out for next project)
  - Retain as retirement income (stable cash flow)
