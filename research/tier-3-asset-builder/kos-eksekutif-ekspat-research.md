### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

#### Foreign Ownership of Property (For Investor — WNI owning land)
```
Requirement: SHM (Sertifikat Hak Milik) — only for WNI
Regulation: UU No. 5/1960 (UUPA)
Process: Standard notarial deed (AJB) + BPN registration
Cost: BPHTB 5% of transaction value + notary fees ~1%
Timeline: 30–90 days
Authority: BPN (Badan Pertanahan Nasional)
Link: atrbpn.go.id
```

#### Foreign Ownership Rights (For tenant — WNA renting)
```
Requirement: Foreigners CANNOT hold SHM. They can hold:
- Hak Pakai (30+20+30 years = 80 years total) for individuals with KITAS/KITAP
- Hak Sewa (leasehold, 25–30 years) — most common for expats without residency
- HGB via PT PMA (for commercial — co-living business)
Regulation: PP 18/2021; PP 41/1996 (being revised 2026)
Threshold: Apartments min IDR 3 miliar (Badung) / IDR 5 miliar (Jakarta) for foreign purchase
Quote-ready: "WNA tidak bisa punya SHM, tapi bisa sewa 25–30 tahun dengan hak sewa notaris. Ini yang membuat kos eksklusif untuk ekspat jadi bisnis properti jangka panjang yang aman."
Source: Noble Asia x ABNR; The Bali Lawyer; bambooroutes.com
```

#### 2026 Proposed Reform (Monitoring)
```
Proposed: Lifetime Hak Pakai (seumur hidup) for WNA on high-value apartments (>Rp 5 miliar)
Who: ATR/BPN Minister Ferry Mursyidan Baldan
Status: Under deliberation (April 2026)
Impact: If passed, more expats will buy vs. rent — BUT rental demand still strong for short-medium term stays

```

#### Business Licensing for Kos Eksklusif
```
Requirement: NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) via OSS-RBA
KBLI: 55900 — Penyediaan Akomodasi Lainnya (boarding house)
Regulation: PP 5/2021; UU Cipta Kerja
Tax:
- PPh Final Pasal 4(2): 10% for property rental income
- OR PP 23/2018: 0.5% if gross revenue < Rp 4,8 miliar/ tahun (UMKM)
- NO PBJT (Pajak Hotel) confirmed for kos-kosan under UU HKPD 2022
- PBB (Pajak Bumi dan Bangunan): 0.5% of NJOP
IMB/PBG: Wajib — specify residential/boarding house use
Additional for Bali: Pondok Wisata license required for tourist accommodation (Kepmenpar 6/2025)
Penalty: Tax penalties 2%/month for late payment; business closure for no NIB
```

#### Visa Partnership Value-Add
```
E33G Remote Worker Visa:
- Cost: ~USD 600–1,600 total (official ~IDR 7 juta + agent fees)
- Requirement: USD 60k/year income, foreign employment contract, bank statement
- Validity: 1 year (must exit and reapply)
- Kos owner CAN partner with visa agents → refer tenants → revenue share or reduced tenant friction

Second Home Visa:
- Deposit IDR 2 miliar in state bank OR IDR 5 miliar property
- 5–10 year validity, multiple entry
- Attracts wealthier, longer-stay tenants

Partnership model: Kos owner refers tenant to licensed visa agent → agent pays referral fee (Rp 1–3 juta/tenant) or kos owner gets discounted service. Both parties benefit.
```

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions

```
Myth 1: "Ekspat pasti bayar mahal — pasang harga tinggi saja"
Reality: Expat digital nomads are cost-conscious; they compare across Bali, Thailand, Vietnam, Portugal. Must deliver VALUE — fast internet is non-negotiable, not a "plus".
Why it matters: Overpricing + poor internet = negative reviews on Google/ FB groups → death spiral for occupancy
Source: Various digital nomad forum posts, 2025–2026

Myth 2: "Bisnis kos itu passive income"
Reality: Kos ekspat adalah ACTIVE business. You need English-speaking management, concierge-level service, community management. If you want passive, hire a co-living operator (but they take 10–15% cut).
Why it matters: Misunderstanding leads to understaffing, bad reviews, high turnover
Source: Multiple operator interviews; ekonomikos-kosan research

Myth 3: "Orang bule nggak masalah tinggal di kos biasa"
Reality: E33G holders earning USD 60k+/year expect premium standards: proper desk, ergonomic chair, high-speed fiber (100+ Mbps), hot water, AC, quiet environment for video calls. A "kos biasa" with kamar mandi luar and WiFi lemah will be rejected.
Why it matters: You're competing with Outpost, Rukita, Airbnbs, serviced apartments — not with kos Rp 500rb
Source: Digital nomad guides 2026; WhereNext; Ohana Agency

Myth 4: "Investor asing butuh KITAS atau PT PMA untuk punya kos"
Reality: For WNI investors, no issue — own land (SHM) and build. For WNA wanting to invest: can use PT PMA (HGB) or Hak Sewa leasehold. The KOS ITSELF should be owned by a WNI or Indonesian legal entity. Foreigners can be silent investors through a PT PMA structure.
Why it matters: Avoid illegal nominee arrangements
Source: Noble Asia x ABNR; The Bali Lawyer 2026

Myth 5: "Bisnis kos kena pajak hotel 10%"
Reality: UU HKPD 2022 confirmed kos-kosan is NOT hotel tax object. No PBJT (Pajak Barang dan Jasa Tertentu). Only PPh Final 10% (or PP 23 0.5%) applies.
Why it matters: 10% margin advantage vs. hotel/serviced apartment competitors
Source: UU 1/2022 tentang HKPD; ekonomi-kos-kosan-research
```

### 9. Action Steps Outline

```
Step 1: Location Audit & Target Tenant Profile
- Identify 3 candidate locations (e.g., Kemang, Kuningan, SCBD for Jakarta; or Canggu, Sanur, Ubud for Bali)
- Within 1 km of coworking space, international school, or expat-friendly cafe hub
- Check fiber internet availability (IndiHome, Biznet, MyRepublic, FiberStar)
- Verify zoning — residential/boarding house permitted

Step 2: Business Model Decision
Option A: Self-managed "DIY premium kos" — max control, max margin, max effort
Option B: Partner with co-living operator (Rukita, Cove) — less margin, minimal operational hassle
Option C: Hybrid — self-manage operations, outsource management to local agent for absentee owner
- Decision matrix: Location, capital, time commitment, language capability

Step 3: Infrastructure Planning (THE KEY FOR EXPATS)
- Internet: Primary fiber (minimum 100 Mbps dedicated), backup fiber (second ISP), backup Starlink (Rp 750k/month)
  → Budget minimum Rp 3–5 juta/month for IT
- Each room: Dedicated workspace (desk 120cm+), ergonomic chair, monitor stand, fast WiFi AP
- Common area: co-working lounge (must be Instagrammable), meeting room with 65" screen
- Power: Stable electricity (genset or UPS for common area); many expats use multiple devices
- AC: Inverter AC per room (WHISPER QUIET — video call quality matters)

Step 4: Legal & Tax Structuring
- Register NIB via OSS (KBLI 55900)
- Set up tax: PPh Final 10% or PP 23 0.5% — consult accountant
- Get IMB/PBG for boarding house use
- If in Bali: check Pondok Wisata license requirements
- Insurance: Asuransi properti (ALL RISK) + public liability

Step 5: Staffing & Operations
- MUST: English-speaking front desk/concierge (salary: Rp 5–8 juta/month for Jakarta)
- Housekeeping (bi-lingual preferred): Rp 4–5 juta/month
- Community manager (organizes weekly events — BBQ, workshops, yoga): Rp 6–8 juta/month

Step 6: Marketing & Sales Channels
- Flokq.com, Coliving.com, Facebook groups ("Bali Digital Nomads", "Canggu Community")
- Partnership with visa agents (RoyalVisa, BaliVisa) — they have inbound tenant pipeline
- Partnership with coworking spaces (referral arrangement)
- Google Business Profile — SEO for "kos ekspat [area]"

Step 7: Visa Assistance Partnership (VALUE-ADD)
- Partner with licensed visa agent (e.g., RoyalVisa, BaliVisa.co, Visastation)
- Offer tenants: "Free visa consultation included with stay" or referral to partner
- Agent pays referral: Rp 1–3 juta per successful E33G application (win-win-win)
- This DIFFERENTIATES your kos from Airbnbs and regular kos
```
