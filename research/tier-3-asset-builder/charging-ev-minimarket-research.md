### Revenue Potential

#### Revenue Stream 1: Charging Fees
- **AC Charger (22 kW):** Rp 2.466/kWh (PLN tariff) + service margin
- **DC Fast Charger (50 kW):** Rp 2.466/kWh + service fee Rp 15.000-25.000/session
- **Monthly charging revenue estimate (per charger, 10 sessions/day average):**
  - AC: 10 session × 15 kWh × Rp 3.000 = Rp 450.000/hari = Rp 13,5 juta/bulan (kotor)
  - DC Fast: 10 session × 35 kWh × Rp 3.200 = Rp 1.120.000/hari = Rp 33,6 juta/bulan (kotor)
  - DC Ultra-Fast: 10 session × 50 kWh × Rp 3.500 = Rp 1.750.000/hari = Rp 52,5 juta/bulan (kotor)

#### Revenue Stream 2: Minimarket Sales
- Average transaction: Rp 25.000-45.000
- Customer capture rate: 40-70% of charging customers also shop
- Daily customers: 20-50 (correlated with charging traffic)
- **Monthly minimarket revenue estimate:**
  - Conservative (20 trans/day × Rp 25rb × 30) = Rp 15 juta/bulan
  - Moderate (35 trans/day × Rp 35rb × 30) = Rp 36,75 juta/bulan
  - Optimistic (50 trans/day × Rp 40rb × 30) = Rp 60 juta/bulan
- Gross margin minimarket: 15-25%
- Typical monthly profit: Rp 3-12 juta

#### Revenue Stream 3: Advertising & Digital
- Digital screen ads on charging units: Rp 2-5 juta/unit/bulan
- Partnership promos (FMCG brands, EV brands): Rp 5-10 juta/bulan

#### Revenue Stream 4: Membership/Subscription
- Monthly membership Rp 50.000-100.000 (priority charging, discount)
- Target: 100-500 members in year 1
- Revenue: Rp 5-50 juta/bulan

## 5. Case Studies

### Case Study 1: PLN SPKLU Rest Area KM 38B Tol Jakarta-Cikampek
- **Operator:** PLN (100% owned)
- **Location:** Rest Area KM 38B Tol Jakarta-Cikampek
- **Facilities:** Multiple DC fast chargers (50-200 kW), AC chargers, convenience stores (tenant), musala, toilet
- **Model:** PLN builds and operates charging infrastructure; retail tenants run F&B and convenience stores
- **Revenue:** Charging fee to PLN; rent/rev-share from retail tenants
- **Key learning:** Rest area locations generate captive traffic; EV charging wait time (20-40 min) drives retail sales naturally. PLN focuses on charging; retail is leased to specialists.
- **Source:** PLN official statements, multiple news reports 2024-2025

### Case Study 2: Pertamina Green Energy Station — SPBU Konversi + SPKLU
- **Operator:** Pertamina
- **Location:** 6 SPBU dikonversi menjadi integrated EV hubs (per 2025)
- **Facilities:** DC fast charging, convenience store (Bright), cafe, tire/wash services
- **Model:** Pertamina converts existing fuel stations; leverages existing land, foot traffic, and retail infrastructure
- **Key learning:** Existing retail + fuel station infrastructure dapat di-upgrade dengan charging. Biaya konversi lebih rendah daripada build from scratch.
- **Source:** WifiTalents, Pertamina releases 2025

### Case Study 3: VinFast-V-Green Indonesia — Massive Private Investment
- **Operator:** VinFast & V-Green (Vietnam)
- **Investment:** $300 million (Rp 4,8 triliun) for 63,000 charging points
- **Partners:** Chargecore, Chargepoint, Amarta Group, CVS
- **Model:** V-Green invests 20% directly; 80% through business cooperation contracts (BCC)
- **Status:** MoU signed May 2025; deployment underway
- **Key learning:** International players melihat Indonesia sebagai pasar utama. Model BCC (business cooperation contract) bisa menjadi template untuk investor lokal yang ingin bermitra.
- **Source:** Jakarta Globe, May 2025

### Case Study 4: Private Operator — Starvo/Voltron Network
- **Operator:** Starvo, Voltron (ex-EVCuzz), Terra Charge
- **Locations:** Mall parking, hotel, SPBU, perkantoran (Jakarta, Bandung, Surabaya, Bali)
- **Model:** Independent SPKLU operator; charges market-rate tariffs (Rp 1.800-3.022/kWh)
- **Key learning:** Private operators have pricing flexibility above PLN tariff. Location in malls and hotels provides built-in waiting area (no need for separate minimarket).
- **Source:** Otodriver tariff comparison, Play Store app listings

## 8. Asset Analysis — Land as Hedge

### Dual-Income + Land Appreciation Thesis

The core investment thesis for Tier 3 readers:

1. **Charging revenue covers operating costs** + provides cash flow (but alone may not provide attractive ROI in early years)
2. **Minimarket provides second income stream** with higher margin (15-25%) and daily cash flow
3. **Land appreciation provides the real wealth creation** — 7-15% annual appreciation in strategic highway-adjacent locations

### Land Valuation Scenarios

| Location Type | Land Price/m² | Minimum Land | Land Investment | Est. Annual Appreciation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rest area adjacent (outside toll, 100m from exit) | Rp 3-8 juta/m² (Jawa) | 500 m² | Rp 1,5-4 M | 10-15%/year |
| Highway corridor (rural-suburban) | Rp 500rb-2 juta/m² | 1,000 m² | Rp 500jt-2 M | 8-12%/year |
| Urban commercial (Jakarta periphery) | Rp 10-25 juta/m² | 300 m² | Rp 3-7,5 M | 7-10%/year |
| Secondary city (Bandung, Surabaya, Medan) | Rp 2-8 juta/m² | 500 m² | Rp 1-4 M | 8-12%/year |

### Collateral Value
- Charging equipment (DC fast charger) can serve as collateral for asset-backed financing
- Some banks may provide 60-70% LTV on SPKLU equipment
- Land + building provides stronger collateral (80% LTV typical for commercial properti)
