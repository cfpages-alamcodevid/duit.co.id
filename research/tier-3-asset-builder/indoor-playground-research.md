### Equipment Depreciation Schedule

| Equipment Type | Useful Life | Annual Depreciation | Replacement Cost (% of original) |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| Soft play structures (foam, vinyl) | 5-7 tahun | 15-20% | 100% (full replacement) |
| Multi-level play frames (steel + HDPE) | 8-12 tahun | 8-12% | 70-80% (partial replacement) |
| Ball pits (balls only) | 2-3 tahun | 33-50% | 30-50% (replace balls only) |
| Safety flooring (foam tiles) | 3-5 tahun | 20-33% | 60-80% |
| Electronic/interactive games | 3-5 tahun | 20-33% | 80-100% |
| Café equipment | 5-8 tahun | 12-20% | 80-100% |
| HVAC system | 10-15 tahun | 7-10% | 100% |
| Furniture & fixtures | 5-7 tahun | 15-20% | 60-80% |

**Key Insight:**
- Play equipment depreciates FAST. A Rp 1 miliar play structure is worth near zero after 7-8 years.
- **BUT** the land+bangunan underneath APPRECIATES. This is the hedge.
- The business profits pay for equipment replacement; the land value growth builds wealth.

## 6. Asset Analysis: Land + Building as Hedge

### Core Investment Thesis (for Tier 3 Audience)

Indoor playground is a **dual-asset investment**:

1. **Operating Business:** Cash flow positive, 25-40% net margins
2. **Real Estate Asset:** Land + building appreciates 8-15% annually in strategic locations

**The Hedge:**
- Play equipment depreciates (10-20%/year, 5-10 year life) = business expense
- Land + building appreciates (8-15%/year, perpetuity) = wealth creation
- Result: Net worth increases even if business margins compress

### Location Strategy for Maximum Asset Appreciation

| Location Type | Pros | Cons | Appreciation Potential |
|--------------|------|------|----------------------|
| Mall (sewa) | High foot traffic, built-in parking | No land hedge; rental escalation every 3-5 years | Zero (no asset ownership) |
| Standalone owned building in residential growth corridor | Full land hedge; can expand | Must build traffic from scratch | 10-15%/year |
| Strip mall/ruko owned | Moderate foot traffic; asset ownership | Limited parking; smaller format | 8-12%/year |
| Inside mixed-use development (owned unit) | Captive audience; shared parking | Strata title limitations | 6-10%/year |
| Suburban land + build (1,000-3,000 m²) | Maximum hedge; future subdivision potential | Requires major marketing investment | 12-20%/year (emerging corridor) |

## 8. Case Studies

### Case Study 1: KidZania Jakarta (Pacific Place Mall)
- **Type:** Premium edutainment (roleplay city concept)
- **Location:** Pacific Place Mall, SCBD Jakarta (floor 6)
- **Opened:** November 2007 (18+ years operating)
- **Size:** ~4,000+ m²
- **Scale:** 60+ establishments (professions), multiple zones
- **Investment:** Estimated Rp 50-100+ miliar (franchise fee + build-out + equipment)
- **Pricing (2026):** Weekday anak Rp 325.000, dewasa Rp 260.000; Weekend half-day dewasa Rp 225.000
- **Revenue model:** Entry fees, birthday parties, school programs, merchandise, F&B
- **Key success factors:**
  - Strong brand recognition (global franchise)
  - Prime mall location (high foot traffic, affluent catchment)
  - Corporate sponsorship model (branded establishments — Coca-Cola, Pizza Hut, etc. pay for branding)
  - Recurring school program partnerships
- **Key learning:** The sponsorship model offsets ~30-50% of initial build-out costs. Corporate brands pay for themed zones.
- **Relevance to article:** Pro model for massive scale with deep corporate partnerships

### Case Study 2: Playparq (Multiple Locations — BSD, Bekasi, Bandung, etc.)
- **Type:** Indoor playground chain (franchise model)
- **Concept:** Ball pit, slides, climbing, trampoline, soft play for kids 1-12
- **Business model:** Franchise + company-owned stores
- **Pricing (est.):** Rp 60.000 - Rp 150.000/child
- **Franchise fee (est.):** Rp 100-300 juta
- **Total investment per outlet (est.):** Rp 500 juta - Rp 3 miliar
- **Key success factors:**
  - Replicable format
  - Balance between affordability for middle class and quality
  - Strong presence in suburban malls (BSD, Bekasi) where family demographics are high
- **Key learning:** Franchise model allows faster expansion; but quality control across outlets is critical

### Case Study 3: Local mid-size playground (Generic model — synthesized from multiple observations)
- **Type:** Independently owned indoor playground + café, 350 m²
- **Location:** Mid-sized mall in Surabaya (second-tier city example)
- **Investment:** ~Rp 4 miliar (build-out + equipment)
- **Monthly revenue (stabilized):** Rp 350-450 juta
- **Monthly expenses:** Rp 200-250 juta
- **Net monthly profit:** Rp 100-200 juta
- **Break-even on investment:** ~24-36 months
- **Key challenges reported:**
  - Staff turnover (childcare staff is demanding work)
  - Weekend revenue is 3-4x weekday — weekday utilization is the challenge
  - Equipment wear-and-tear requires constant vigilance
  - Competition from new places opening nearby
- **Key learning:** Location is EVERYTHING. Being in a growing residential corridor with 50,000+ families within 15 minutes drive is table stakes.

### Staff Training Requirements

1. **Child Safety & Supervision:**
   - Child-to-staff ratio: 1:10-15 (recommended)
   - Crowd control during peak hours
   - Identifying distressed or lost children
   - Preventing bullying or rough play
   - Emergency response for injuries

2. **First Aid Certification:**
   - Minimum 2 staff per shift with certified PPPK (Pertolongan Pertama Pada Kecelakaan)
   - Pediatric first aid (choking, falls, allergic reactions)
   - Annual recertification recommended

3. **Background Checks:**
   - Police clearance (SKCK) for all staff
   - Reference checks for prior work with children
   - No tolerance policy for any history of violence, abuse, or theft

4. **Customer Service:**
   - Birthday party hosting skills
   - Complaint handling
   - Upselling membership packages

### Daily Operations Checklist

**Morning (pre-opening):**
- [ ] Full visual inspection of all play equipment
- [ ] Test all interactive/electronic games
- [ ] Ball pit: remove debris, check for sharp objects
- [ ] Clean and sanitize all high-touch surfaces
- [ ] Safety gate and barrier checks
- [ ] First aid kit inventory check
- [ ] Café prep and food safety check
- [ ] Staff briefing (capacity, special events, safety focus)

**Throughout the day:**
- [ ] Continuous spot-cleaning of play areas
- [ ] 2-hourly restroom checks and cleaning
- [ ] Monitor capacity limits (fire safety + experience quality)
- [ ] Birthday party room setup/turnaround
- [ ] Incident reporting (any injury, no matter how minor)

**Closing:**
- [ ] Full equipment inspection for damage
- [ ] Ball pit deep-clean (rotation schedule)
- [ ] Trash removal, floor mopping, sanitation
- [ ] Cash reconciliation, POS closing
- [ ] Security system arm
- [ ] Incident log review and sign-off
