### 4. Market Research
**Corporate Tenant Profiles:**
1.  **Minimarkets (Indomaret/Alfamart/Lawson):**
    *   Requirements: 120-150 m2, facade width min 8m, high visibility, parking for 4-6 cars.
    *   Contract: 5 years + 5 years.
2.  **Banks/ATM Centers:**
    *   Requirements: Strong floor load for safes, high security, 24/7 access for ATMs.
    *   Rent: Usually premium due to "brand visibility" on the building facade.
3.  **National Chains (Kopi Kenangan/Mixue):**
    *   High turnover, aggressive expansion, prefers high foot-traffic spots (corners).

**Landlord Math:**
*   Leasing to Corporate: Lower rent per m2 but 0% vacancy for 10 years + professional maintenance.
*   Leasing to Local Cafe: Higher rent but 30-50% risk of failure in year 1-2.

### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
*   **Permits:** PBG (Persetujuan Bangunan Gedung) must be for "Commercial" use.
*   **Zoning:** Check RDTR (Rencana Detail Tata Ruang) for "Zona Ungu/Komersial".
*   **Contracts:** Corporate tenants have "Standard Contracts" that often favor them (e.g., right of first refusal for renewal). Need legal review.

### 7. Practical Examples & Scenarios
**Scenario: The ATM "Side-Hustle"**
*   Investor has a ruko with a small 2x2m unused corner on the sidewalk.
*   Lease to Bank for ATM: Rp 22jt/year.
*   Cost: Zero (Bank pays for electricity & security).
*   Result: "Found Money" that covers PBB and maintenance for the entire building.

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
*   **Myth:** "Perusahaan besar pasti bayar mahal."
*   **Reality:** Big companies are tough negotiators. They pay "Fair Market Value" but offer "Reliability".
*   **Myth:** "Bisa sewakan lahan apa saja ke Minimarket."
*   **Reality:** They have strict radius rules (cannot be too close to existing branches) and traffic count requirements.
