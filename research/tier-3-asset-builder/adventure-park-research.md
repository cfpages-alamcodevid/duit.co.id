## 7. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### Business Licensing (Perizinan Berusaha)
```
Framework: Perizinan Berusaha Berbasis Risiko (PBBR) via OSS-RBA
Base Regulation:
- PP No. 5 Tahun 2021 tentang Penyelenggaraan Perizinan Berusaha Berbasis Risiko
- PP No. 28 Tahun 2025 tentang Perizinan Berusaha Berbasis Risiko (update)
- Permenparekraf No. 4 Tahun 2021 → digantikan Permenparekraf No. 6 Tahun 2025
  (Standar Kegiatan Usaha, Tata Cara Pengawasan, Sanksi Administratif)
- UU No. 10 Tahun 2009 tentang Kepariwisataan (diubah UU Cipta Kerja)

KBLI for adventure park:
- 93212: Wisata Buatan/Bermain (amusement park, theme park) — Menengah Tinggi
- 93222: Wisata Alam/Outdoor Activities — Menengah Tinggi
- 68120: Kawasan Pariwisata — Risiko Tinggi

Requirements for Menengah Tinggi:
- NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) via OSS
- Sertifikat Standar (sertifikasi oleh LSU Pariwisata)
- Memiliki izin lokasi/lingkungan sesuai ketentuan
- Memenuhi standar usaha sesuai Permenparekraf
```

### Safety & Guide Certification
```
Regulation: PP No. 24 Tahun 2023 tentang Sertifikasi Kompetensi Kerja di Bidang Kepariwisataan
- Mencabut PP No. 52/2012
- Mewajibkan sertifikasi bagi tenaga kerja pariwisata termasuk pemandu adventure

Sertifikasi Guide:
- Badan: BNSP (Badan Nasional Sertifikasi Profesi)
- Skema: Pemandu Wisata (Tour Guide) — Rp 1.275.000/orang (PUKOMINDO)
  - Pemandu Arung Jeram (River Guide) — SKKNI khusus bidang arung jeram
  - KKNI Bidang Pemanduan Keselamatan Wisata Tirta (Safety Guard Water Tourism) — SK Kemenparekraf 2024
- Masa berlaku: 3 tahun
- Biaya: Rp 900.000 - 2.000.000 per orang (tergantung skema)
- Program subsidi pemerintah: Sertifikasi gratis via Kemenpar (kuota terbatas)
  - Contoh: 50 guide rafting Bali (Mei 2026) — Kemenpar + LSP Pramindo

FAJI (Federasi Arung Jeram Indonesia): Pembina standar arung jeram nasional
- Sungai diklasifikasikan Grade I-VI
- Grade I-II: wisata aman dengan guide
- Grade III: perlu prosedur pengamanan + tim rescue
- Grade IV-V: hanya untuk berpengalaman

Safety Requirements:
- Safety talk wajib sebelum aktivitas berisiko tinggi
- Peralatan SNI untuk helm, harness, carabiner, kabel baja
- Rasio guide:pengunjung minimum (rafting: 1 guide per boat, typically 1:4-6)
```

## 8. Asset Analysis: Large Land as Primary Hedge

### Why Land is the Core Asset

Adventure park business belongs to the "Business-as-Investment" cluster precisely because:

**1. Land appreciation in tourist corridors:**
- Lembang: Rp 1-4,5 juta/m2, historically appreciating 10-20% annually
- Puncak: Rp 3,5-4,5 juta/m2, land-banked since colonial era
- Malang/Batu: Rp 500rb - 3 juta/m2 in tourism zones
- Even in a worst-case business scenario, land value tends to rise

**2. Infrastructure depreciation to consider:**
- Wood structures (gazebo, jembatan): 3-5 years lifespan
- Steel cables (flying fox): 2-3 years before recommended replacement
- ATV units: 3-5 years with heavy use, need regular maintenance
- Climbing holds/wall panels: 5-8 years
- Paintball markers: 3-5 years with maintenance
- Buildings: 20+ years with maintenance

**3. Maintenance costs (estimated annual):**
- Equipment inspection & certification: Rp 25-50 juta/tahun
- Cable replacement (flying fox): Rp 10-20 juta per cable
- ATV maintenance & parts: Rp 15-30 juta/tahun per 3-4 units
- General facility maintenance: Rp 50-100 juta/tahun
- Safety gear replacement: Rp 20-40 juta/tahun
- Land maintenance (gardening, cleaning): Rp 30-60 juta/tahun
- **Total annual maintenance: ~Rp 150-300 juta** (medium park)

**4. Bank loan optimization:**
- Land as collateral value: Typically 70-80% of market value
- Business cash flow for debt service coverage
- Adventure park business enhances the "productive asset" profile
- Combined collateral (land + business) > land-only collateral

## 9. Safety: Accident Liability & Risk Management

### Risk Profile by Activity

| Activity | Risk Level | Common Injuries | Insurance Premium Impact |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------------|
| Flying Fox | Medium | Grazes, impact at landing | Moderate |
| Rafting (Grade II-III) | High | Drowning risk, bumps | High |
| Paintball | Low-Medium | Bruises, welts | Low |
| ATV | Medium-High | Rollovers, collisions | High |
| Wall Climbing | Medium | Falls (if belay fails) | Moderate |
| High Ropes | Medium-High | Falls from height | High |
| Hiking/Trekking | Low-Medium | Slips, sprains | Low |
| Camping | Low | Weather, animal encounters | Low |

### Safety Protocols Required:
1. **Pre-activity safety briefing** for every participant — mandatory
2. **Equipment inspection log** — weekly documented checks
3. **Guide certification valid** — ensure all guides have current BNSP certification
4. **Emergency response plan** — posted and practiced quarterly
5. **First aid station** on-site with trained personnel
6. **Weather monitoring** — SOP for lightning/high wind/rain cancellation
7. **Maximum capacity limits** per activity
8. **Age/health restrictions** posted for high-risk activities
9. **Incident reporting system** — document all accidents

### Liability Case Reference:
- **Perhutani-Askrindo model:** Standard for gov't-managed parks, Rp 25-53 juta coverage per visitor
- **Jasindo-Penglipuran model:** Rp 50 million death benefit, included in Rp 25,000 ticket
- **Best practice:** Bundle personal accident insurance into ticket price (Rp 2,000-5,000 premium per ticket)

### Low Seasons
```
1. Rainy season peak (December-February): Day visitors drop 40-60%
   — Rafting activities may shut down if river levels dangerous
   — Can pivot to indoor/covered activities
2. Ramadhan month (before Lebaran): Day visitors drop significantly
   — Opportunity for night operations (buka puasa packages)
3. Weekdays outside holiday season: Low traffic
   — Focus on corporate/outbound bookings
```

### Weather Mitigation Strategies:
- **Covered activity areas:** At least 2-3 covered shelters/wahana for rain
- **F&B as weather buffer:** Indoor cafe/restaurant generates revenue even when wet
- **Corporate booking focus:** Target companies for weekday outbound (weather-dependent)
- **Glamping/camping accommodation:** Overnight stays generate multi-day revenue
- **Seasonal pricing:** Lower ticket prices in low season to maintain traffic
- **Maintenance window:** Use rainy season low traffic for equipment maintenance

## 11. Common Myths & Misconceptions

```
Myth 1: "Bisnis adventure park modalnya besar banget, nggak balik modal."
Reality: Infrastructure cost (non-land) is relatively modest — Rp 1-3 billion for 
         a solid 3-5 activity park. Land is the big cost, and land APPRECIATES. 
         Most parks recoup infrastructure investment within 6-12 months.
Why it matters: Investors overlook the land-hedge value.

Myth 2: "Cukup punya tanah luas, tinggal bikin flying fox, pasti ramai."
Reality: Location, accessibility, marketing, safety standards, and experience 
         design are equally critical. Many parks in remote areas struggle.
Why it matters: Poorly designed adventure parks fail despite good land.

Myth 3: "Asuransi wisata itu opsional, bisa di-skip untuk hemat biaya."
Reality: 2025-2026 trend: Asuransi wajib melekat di tiket. Askrindo cover 195 
         lokasi Perhutani; Jasindo cover Desa Penglipuran. Tanpa asuransi, 
         risiko kecelakaan bisa mematikan bisnis secara hukum dan reputasi.
Why it matters: Satu kecelakaan fatal tanpa asuransi = potensi gugatan Rp 1-5 miliar.

Myth 4: "Adventure park hanya laku di Java-Bali."
Reality: Emerging destinations in Sumatra (Bukittinggi, Danau Toba), Sulawesi 
         (Toraja, Makassar), NTB (Mandalika, Rinjani) showing strong demand.
Why it matters: First-mover advantage in emerging tourism corridors.

Myth 5: "Maintenance wahana itu murah, cuma sekali pasang."
Reality: Flying fox cable harus diganti 2-3 tahun sekali. ATV butuh parts rutin. 
         Safety equipment (helm, harness) punya masa pakai terbatas.
Why it matters: Undercounting maintenance costs is the #1 reason adventure 
         parks become dangerous or close down.
```

## 16. Key Insights for the Article Writer

1. **Main investment thesis:** Land is the primary asset hedge (appreciates 10-20%/year in tourist corridors). The activities provide cash flow to service debt. Combined, it's a bankable asset for larger loans.

2. **The mental shift:** This isn't a "small business" — it's an asset strategy. The land costs dominate (80-95% of total investment), but it's also the safest part of the investment.

3. **Money isn't made on tickets alone:** F&B, corporate outbound, packages, and accommodation are where margins are higher. Single-ticket models struggle.

4. **The 2025-2026 timing is perfect:** Domestic tourism is at an all-time high (1.2B perjalanan), outdoor activities are trending post-pandemic, and the government is actively promoting adventure tourism (DXI 2026, certification programs).

5. **Risk management is non-negotiable:** One serious accident without insurance can bankrupt the business and create criminal liability. Bundle insurance into tickets.

6. **Start small, expand smart:** Don't build all activities at once. Start with 2-3 core activities, learn the market, grow from profits.
