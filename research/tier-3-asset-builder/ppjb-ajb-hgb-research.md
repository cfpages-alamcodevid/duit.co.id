### 5. Legal & Regulatory Requirements
```
Requirement: PPJB must be made before notary with specific clauses
Regulation: PP 12/2021, Article 22J
Process: Include identity, object description, price/payment, guarantees, rights/obligations, delivery timeline, maintenance, usage, transfer rights, cancellation, dispute resolution
Cost: Notary fees Rp 500,000 - Rp 2 million
Timeline: Can be signed before construction completion
Authority: Notary/PPAT

Requirement: AJB must be made by PPAT for title transfer
Regulation: PP 24/1997 as amended by PP 18/2021
Process: Verify clean title, full payment, sign before PPAT, register at BPN
Cost: PPAT fees 1% of transaction value (max Rp 50 million), registration Rp 50,000-100,000
Timeline: After PPJB conditions met, 1-2 months for registration
Authority: PPAT and BPN

Requirement: HGB rights subject to mortgage under UU Hak Tanggungan
Regulation: UU 4/1996, PP 18/2021
Process: Register mortgage (APHT) at BPN, priority given to first mortgage holder
Cost: APHT fees Rp 500,000-1 million
Timeline: Can be done anytime during HGB validity
Authority: BPN

Requirement: BPHTB payment mandatory for acquisition
Regulation: UU 12/1985, local Perda
Process: Calculate after NPOPTKP deduction, pay before AJB
Cost: 5% of taxable value
Timeline: Before AJB signing
Authority: Local tax office
```

### 6. Expert Opinions & Case Studies
```
Case Study: Developer Bankruptcy After PPJB Signing
Background: Buyers sign PPJB for apartment units, pay installments, developer declares bankruptcy before AJB
Result: Buyers lose rights as PPJB doesn't transfer title, courts treat as contractual dispute not property ownership
Timeline: 6-18 months legal process
Key learning: Always ensure AJB completion and title registration before full payment
Source: Legal Indonesia case studies, Supreme Court decisions on PPJB disputes

Case Study: AJB "Fully Paid" But Incomplete Payment
Background: Seller signs AJB stating payment received, but buyer hasn't paid fully
Result: Seller cannot claim outstanding payment, buyer faces fraud charges, PPAT liable
Timeline: Civil lawsuit 6-12 months
Key learning: Verify actual payment before signing, use escrow services
Source: MNL Law analysis, KUHP Article 266

Case Study: HGB Expiration and Mortgage Loss
Background: Property owner mortgages HGB expiring soon, rights return to state
Result: Mortgage holder loses priority, becomes concurrent creditor
Timeline: At expiration (30+20+20 years)
Key learning: Check HGB expiration dates, avoid long-term mortgages near expiry
Source: PP 18/2021, UU Hak Tanggungan Article 18
```

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
```
Myth: PPJB transfers property ownership
Reality: PPJB only binds parties to future sale; AJB transfers title
Why it matters: Buyers think they own property, lose rights in disputes
Source: PP 12/2021, legal expert analyses

Myth: HGB rights last forever like freehold
Reality: HGB expires after 70 years, returns to state land
Why it matters: Long-term investments become worthless
Source: PP 18/2021 Article 37

Myth: AJB "fully paid" means payment complete
Reality: Statement can be false; law presumes truth until proven
Why it matters: Sellers lose right to unpaid amounts
Source: KUHP Article 266, civil code Article 1321
```
