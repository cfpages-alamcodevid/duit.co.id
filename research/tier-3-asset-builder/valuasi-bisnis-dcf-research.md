- Discount rate (WACC): 12%

### 8. Common Myths & Misconceptions
Myth: DCF is always more accurate than asset-based valuation
Reality: Asset-based methods are superior for businesses with stable tangible assets like real estate or equipment
Why it matters: Using wrong method can lead to 20-50% valuation error
Source: Indonesian valuation practitioners
Relevance: Method selection guidance

Myth: Country risk premium doesn't affect private business valuation
Reality: Indonesia's 2.46% country risk premium must be added to WACC calculations
Why it matters: Ignoring it underestimates discount rates by 2-3%
Source: Damodaran data
Relevance: Proper risk adjustment

Myth: Business valuation is only for sale transactions
Reality: Essential for succession planning, investment decisions, and tax compliance
Why it matters: Proactive valuation prevents disputes and optimizes tax
Source: Tax authorities
Relevance: Broad application awareness

### 9. Action Steps Outline
Step 1: Gather financial data
- Details needed: 3-5 years historical financials, asset register, market comparables
- Example: Collect P&L, balance sheet, cash flow statements
- Warning: Ensure data accuracy - errors compound in DCF

Step 2: Choose appropriate method
- Details needed: Business characteristics (growth stage, asset intensity)
- Example: DCF for high-growth tech, NAV for property-heavy businesses
- Warning: Don't mix methods inappropriately

Step 3: Calculate WACC
- Details needed: Cost of equity (CAPM + country risk), cost of debt, capital structure
- Example: WACC = 13% equity cost + 7% debt cost weighted
- Warning: Use current Indonesian market rates

Step 4: Perform valuation calculations
- Details needed: Cash flow projections, asset appraisals, sensitivity analysis
- Example: Run base/moderate/optimistic scenarios
- Warning: Document assumptions for audit trail

Step 5: Cross-check and triangulate
- Details needed: Compare DCF vs asset vs market methods
- Example: Average of three approaches for final value
- Warning: Single method valuations are unreliable
