### 8.3 Return on Land Value (ROLV) — The "Asset-Builder" Metric

This is the key differentiator metric for the Duit.co.id article:

| Investment Type | Value After 5 Years | Annual Return |
|---|---|---|
| **Bare land** (Rp 1M purchase) | Rp 1.3M - 1.5M (5-8% annual appreciation) | **5-8%** (unrealized) |
| **Land + Pool + Café** (Rp 1.8M total) | Land Rp 1.3-1.5M + Business asset value (3-5× annual profit) | **Combined 15-30%** |

**LTV Analysis:**
- **Bare land LTV at bank:** max 60-70% of NJOP/market value
- **Land + building (pool included) LTV at bank:** 60-70% of appraised value — but the building+pool adds to the appraised value significantly
- **Key argument:** The physical improvements (pool, café building, landscaping) cost Rp 500-800 juta but increase the property's appraised value by MORE than their cost in most cases because:
  1. Income-producing property is valued at higher multiples
  2. The pool+café generates cash flow that supports the valuation
  3. Buyers pay premium for turnkey income properties

### 8.4 Loan/Financing Options

| Type | Bank | Max LTV | Rate | Term |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Kredit Pemilikan Rumah (KPR) komersial | Bank konvensional | 70-80%* | 8-12% | 10-15 years |
| Kredit Modal Kerja (KMK) | Bank BUMN/swasta | 60-70% of inventory/receivables | 9-14% | 1-5 years |
| Kredit Investasi | Bank pembangunan daerah | 60-70% of asset value | 9-13% | 5-10 years |
| *For property with existing building; pool considered part of building structure |

**Key Insight:** Most banks will treat the pool as part of the building structure in a property appraisal. A standalone pool without land/building is harder to finance. The café + pool + land combination creates the strongest collateral package.

The bank sees a property that:
1. Generates documented income (café receipts, membership fees)
2. Has an active business license
3. Has clear operational history
4. Has tangible physical improvements on the land

This is dramatically more financeable than raw land.

## 11. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

| Requirement | Details | Timeline | Cost |

## 13. Action Steps Outline

### Step 1: Land Feasibility & Zoning Check
- **Verify zoning:** Ensure land zoning allows for kolam renang umum + tempat usaha makanan/minuman
- **Check access:** Width, road condition, parking availability for 10-20 cars
- **Utilities:** Confirm PDAM capacity, PLN 3-phase availability (pump needs), internet
- **Details needed:** Minimum land area 200-400m²; road access ≥ 4m width

### Step 2: Concept & Design
- **Choose theme:** Minimalist modern vs natural (free-form) vs resort-style
- **Decide pool size:** 3×6m (basic), 4×8m (recommended), 5×10m (premium)
- **Café integration:** Indoor-only, semi-outdoor (poolside), or full open deck
- **Target audience:** Family-friendly, young adult social, sports-focused, or premium membership

### Step 3: Construction & Equipment
- **Get 3 contractor quotes** with detailed RAB
- **Negotiate timeline:** Typical 3-4 months for mini pool + café build
- **Equipment selection:** Balance between cost and energy efficiency (inverter pump saves 30-50% on electricity)
- **Warning:** Don't skimp on filtration system — undersized pump/filter is the #1 cause of operational issues

### Step 4: Permits & Licensing
- **OSS-RBA registration:** Get NIB (free, online)
- **IMB/PBG:** Hire a consultant if city processes are complex
- **Health permit:** For café kitchen
- **Timeline:** Budget 3-6 months for full permitting

### Step 5: Pre-Opening & Launch
- **Staff hiring:** Train lifeguards (sertifikat penjaga kolam renang), baristas, waitstaff
- **Membership presale:** Offer founding member discounts (20-30% off first year)
- **Soft launch:** Invite local influencers, community leaders
- **Systems:** POS, membership management, booking system
