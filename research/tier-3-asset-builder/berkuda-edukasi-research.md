## 9. Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### PORDASI (Persatuan Olahraga Berkuda Seluruh Indonesia)
- **Regulation:** Olahraga berkuda di Indonesia dibawah naungan PORDASI — anggota KONI
- **Requirement:** Riding schools/klub harus terdaftar di PORDASI untuk mengikuti kompetisi resmi
- **Website:** PORDASI (under KONI)
- **Benefits of registration:** Official competition participation, access to coaching certification, network of 50+ clubs

### Business Licenses
| License | Detail | Authority |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) | Mandatory for all businesses | OSS-RBA |
| SIUP / Izin Usaha | KBLI specific for sports education/recreation | Dinas Penanaman Modal |
| Izin Lokasi | Required for land use > certain size | BPN / Pemda |
| Izin Mendirikan Bangunan (IMB) | For stables, arena, clubhouse | Dinas PUPR setempat |
| Izin Usaha Olahraga | Specific for sports facilities | Dispora / Kemenpora |
| Sertifikat Laik Fungsi | Building occupancy permit | Dinas terkait |

### Tax Obligations
| Tax Type | Rate | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| PPh Badan | 22% (2026 rate) | From net profit |
| PPh 21 (karyawan) | Progressive | Withhold from salaries |
| PPN | 11–12% | For F&B, certain services |
| PBB (Pajak Bumi & Bangunan) | 0.5% of NJKP | Land + building tax |
| PPh Final for rent/boarding | 10% | If applicable |

### Animal Welfare Regulations
- Indonesia has **UU No. 18/2009 jo. UU No. 41/2014** tentang Peternakan dan Kesehatan Hewan
- Animal welfare standards: proper housing, nutrition, veterinary care, transport
- **Penalties for neglect:** Criminal penalties possible under animal cruelty laws (Pasal 302 KUHP, UU 41/2014)

### Import Regulations for Horses
- **Quarantine:** All imported horses must go through **BKP Kelas 1** (Karantina Hewan) — minimum 14 days
- **Requirements:** Health certificate from country of origin, blood tests, Coggins test negative
- **Cost:** Rp 10–20 Juta per horse for quarantine + transport
- **Authority:** Badan Karantina Indonesia

## 10. Safety & Insurance

### Insurance Requirements

| Type | Why | Estimated Premium |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| **Public Liability Insurance** | **CRITICAL** — coverage for rider injury on premises. A fall from a horse can cause serious injury (head trauma, fractures, spinal injury) | Rp 15–30 Juta/year for Rp 1–5 Billion coverage |
| **Horse Mortality Insurance** | If a horse dies (colic is the #1 killer), you lose a Rp 50–500 Juta asset | 3–5% of horse value/year |
| **Personal Accident Insurance for Staff** | Grooms and trainers at higher injury risk | Rp 2–5 Juta/year per person |
| **Property Insurance** | For stables, equipment, clubhouse | 0.5–1% of insured value/year |

### Safety Standards for Children
| Item | Standard | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Riding helmets | ASTM/SEI certified, SNI recommended | **Mandatory for all riders.** No exceptions. |
| Body protectors / safety vests | For jumping, cross-country | Optional for flatwork, recommended |
| Footwear | Boots with 1–2cm heel | Prevents foot slipping through stirrup |
| Age restrictions | Minimum 4–5 years for pony rides; 6+ for lessons | Physical development matters |
| Instructor certification | PORDASI / FEI coaching certification | Official certification required |
| Student-instructor ratio | Private: 1:1; Group max 6:1 | Safety in numbers but quality suffers >6 |
| Emergency protocol | Written protocol, first aid kit on site, nearest hospital mapped | Must be practiced regularly |

**Warning:** The biggest legal risk is a child getting injured and parents suing. Indonesia's legal system is increasingly plaintiff-friendly for personal injury cases involving children.

## 11. Asset Analysis — The Triple-Play Investment Thesis

### Land as Appreciating Asset
- **Thesis:** Large land parcels (1+ ha) in peri-urban areas (Bogor, Bandung, Tangerang, BSD) have consistently appreciated 10–20% per year for the last decade
- **Hedge against inflation:** Land is a proven inflation hedge in Indonesia
- **Liquidity:** Not as liquid as stocks, but the business generates cash flow while land appreciates
- **Exit strategy:** If the riding business doesn't work out, the land itself can be sold as a development parcel (housing, commercial)

### Horses as Depreciating (Then Appreciating) Assets
- **Myth:** "Horses are always a money pit"
- **Reality:** A well-trained horse INCREASES in value:
  - Buy young/unstarted: Rp 30–80 Juta
  - After 6–12 months of professional training: Rp 60–150 Juta (50–100% increase)
  - A competition horse with show record: Rp 200–500 Juta+
- **Depreciation risk:** Injury, age, poor training, market downturn
- **Insurance mitigates mortality risk**

### Business Cash Flow
- Riding schools generate consistent monthly cash flow
- Recurring revenue from monthly package memberships
- Weekend/school holiday spikes

### Summary: Why This Works as an Investment

| Component | Risk | Return | Liquidity |
|-----------|------|--------|-----------|
| Land | Low-Med (market cycle) | 10–20%/yr appreciation | Low-Med |
| Horses | Medium (injury, mortality) | 30–100% if well-trained | Low |
| Riding lessons | Low-Med (demand-driven) | 15–30% operating margin | High (cash) |
| Café/F&B | Low | 20–40% margin | High |
| **COMBINED** | **Medium** | **15–25% total ROI (including appreciation)** | **Medium** |

## 12. Common Myths & Misconceptions

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "Bisnis berkuda butuh modal kecil" | **False.** Ini high-capital business. Minimum realistic investment Rp 5–7 Miliar. Land alone bisa Rp 3–20 M. |

## 15. Action Steps Outline

### Step 1: Site Selection & Land Acquisition
- **Details:** Minimum 1 ha, preferably 2 ha. Proximity to upper-middle-class residential areas (within 30–60 minute drive). Good road access for horse transport.
- **Land considerations:** Flat or gently sloping, good drainage, access to water source
- **Warning:** Avoid land that floods or has restricted access for horse trailers

### Step 2: Legal & Regulatory Setup
- Form PT (PERSEROAN TERBATAS) — required for formal business
- Register with PORDASI through regional chapter
- Obtain NIB, IMB, Izin Usaha Olahraga
- Register horses with PORDASI if competing

### Step 3: Facility Design & Construction
- Prioritize: stables (ventilation!), arena footing (sand/fiber mix), drainage
- Clubhouse can be phase 2 — start with basic shelter
- Café can be phase 3 — build after membership base is established

### Step 4: Horse Acquisition
- Start with 3–5 school horses (safe, calm temperament — "schoolmasters")
- Add 2 ponies for children's program
- One "show horse" if you want competition presence
- **DO NOT** buy all horses at once — buy in batches, quarantine each new horse

### Step 5: Staff Recruitment
- Head trainer: must have PORDASI certification or FEI coaching level 1 minimum
- Grooms: the hardest to find and retain — train from local community
- Consider revenue-sharing model for head trainer (base salary + per-lesson commission)

### Step 6: Marketing & Launch
- Target: Instagram-heavy (visual content of horses, kids riding, beautiful facility)
- Partner with international schools (Jakarta/Bogor/Bandung) for field trips
- Offer "free trial lesson" to generate leads
- Word-of-mouth: join parenting communities in Jabodetabek/wealthy areas
